# copywriting-team v4.15.0 Grounding Research

## Cluster 1: PREP 法 (Point-Reason-Example-Point)

**Canonical attribution**: **None identifiable**. Strongest documentable
claim: emerged from English-language corporate communication training
in the 1980s; entered Toastmasters International canon as a
recommended impromptu-speaking framework.

**Primary sources**:
- Toastmasters International "90 Tips From Toastmasters" (tip #83
  lists PREP; no originator cited)
  https://www.toastmasters.org/About/90th-Anniversary/90-Tips
- Wikipedia (JP) 「PREP法」— carries explicit 出典不足 + 独自研究 banners;
  does NOT name an originator
  https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/PREP法
- BiteSize Learning "The PREP framework" — "exact origins are debated...
  widely credited to communication training programmes in the
  corporate world during the 1980s"
  https://www.bitesizelearning.co.uk/resources/the-prep-framework-communicate-ideas-with-clarity
- Lumen Learning / SUNY Oswego *Business Communication Skills for
  Managers* §11.3 — textbook-level treatment of PREP as standard
  impromptu framework
- 照屋華子・岡田恵子 (2001)『ロジカル・シンキング』東洋経済新報社 —
  JP canonical logical-thinking text; core concepts are MECE +
  So What?/Why So?, NOT PREP specifically. PREP commonly taught
  alongside but not defined in this book.

**Stage confirmation**: 4-stage P-R-E-P unanimously confirmed across
every EN and JP source. No order variation. Variants (PREPR, SDS 法,
DESC 法) are sibling frameworks, not PREP variants.

**JP adoption**: Pervasive in business-writing, web-writing, and
presentation training. Treated as common knowledge rather than
attributed. No JP canonical book.

**Relationship to CREMA**: Confirmed as JP web-marketing community
claim, NOT academic derivation. Sources explicitly state CREMA adds
Method + Action to PREP. Derivation is JP circle convention; no
non-JP or academic CREMA source exists.

**Attribution drifts identified**:
- **Drift #15**: Brian Tracy as PREP originator — appears in Medium
  articles and blog posts; no primary citation to any Tracy book.
- **Drift #16**: McKinsey as PREP originator — conflation with
  Barbara Minto's *Pyramid Principle* (1987). Likely via 照屋's
  McKinsey background leaking into PREP attribution.
- **Drift #17**: PREP = Toulmin model — Toulmin (1958) has 6
  components (claim / grounds / warrant / backing / qualifier /
  rebuttal), structurally distinct from P-R-E-P.

**Confidence level**: Low on authorship; Medium on "1980s Anglo
corporate communication training" general origin; High on JP
adoption and stage definition.

**Unresolved**:
- Earliest documented print attestation of "PREP" acronym (no
  pre-2000 source located).
- Whether any Toastmasters internal document from 1980s-90s formally
  introduced the acronym.

## Cluster 2: CREMA 法 (Conclusion-Reason-Evidence-Method-Action)

**Canonical attribution**: **None**. Exhaustive searches for
「CREMA 提唱者」「CREMA 考案者」「CREMA 生みの親」「CREMA 発祥」
「CREMAの法則 誰が」returned zero named individuals or organizations.
CREMA is a process-driven template circulating in JP web-marketing /
copywriting community content since late-2021, with no canonical
author claim.

**Primary sources (8 independent JP industry media)**:
- **看板のサインシティ** 2021-10-25/27 — https://www.trade-sign.com/magazine/4657/
  **(earliest confirmed documentation)**
- **ブログライター** 2021-10-30 — https://www.blogwriter-blog.info/2021/10/crema.html
- PASSITE (note.com) 2023-03-27 — https://note.com/passite/n/n77f4d33a89cb
- ライターズ.com / Fereple 2023-09-07 (updated 2026-03-31) — https://fereple.com/writers-apc/cremas-law/
- つばき (note.com) 2025-03-05 — https://note.com/tubaki_0426/n/n634529ebe083
- 侍AIラボ 2024-02-04 — https://samurai-work.com/crema-law/
- SEOタイムズ — https://seotimes.jp/crema-rule-seo/
- ぼかん屋 — https://write-sentence.org/crema-rule/
- 株式会社アーティス「6つの型」— https://www.asobou.co.jp/blog/web/writing-format
- Content Kessaku — https://content-kessaku.jp/news/crema-law/

**Stage confirmation**: 5-stage Conclusion → Reason → Evidence →
Method → Action **consistent across all 8 sources**. No variants.
Minor lexical variation only: Evidence = 「証拠」or「根拠」;
Method = 「手段」or「方法」.

**Etymology finding**: **Espresso-foam (クレマ) naming is NOT
confirmed**. Sources that address the name state it is simply the
5 English head-word initialism (「頭文字をとった造語」). The
espresso-foam connection is post-hoc reader interpretation, not
documented etymology.

**Scope consensus**: **"軽い行動" framing confirmed** across all
sources. CREMA targets light-commitment conversions: メルマガ登録,
オプトインページ, 無料オファー, LINE 登録, 記事型コンテンツ,
low-price affiliate, LP click-through. No source pitches CREMA for
high-ticket sales (PASONA/QUEST reserved for that).

**PREP relationship**: Sources uniformly contrast CREMA with PREP
and state Method + Action are additions. Phrased prose-style:
「PREP 法の弱点を補う」(つばき), 「『行動を促す』文章を書く部分が
組み込まれています」(ブログライター). The formula "CREMA = PREP +
M + A" is analytical synthesis, not verbatim canon.

**Earliest documentation**: **2021-10-25** (看板のサインシティ).
Second source (ブログライター) 5 days later. Tight clustering suggests
the term was coined/popularized in JP copywriting circles around
mid/late 2021.

**Attribution drifts identified**:
- **Drift #18**: Espresso-foam etymology — appears in reader
  speculation but no source documents this. CREMA is an initialism
  per all consulted sources.
- **Drift #19** (weak): Content Kessaku analogizes CREMA's flow to
  David Kolb's experiential learning cycle — this is comparison, not
  attribution claim, but could be misread as such.

**Confidence level**: **High (~90%)** for "no canonical author" —
8 independent sources over 4.5 years consistently present CREMA
without attribution. Residual risk: paywalled JP book predating
2021-10-25.

**Unresolved**:
1. Is there a pre-2021 JP source (book/seminar/paid newsletter) that
   the 2021-10 web articles drew from?
2. Did a single influencer/agency coin CREMA, or emerge via
   convergent acronym construction?

## Cluster 3: Light-Action Taxonomy

**Existing grounded taxonomies**:

- **Micro/Macro Conversion** — Avinash Kaushik (2007/2008) "Excellent
  Analytics Tip #13: Measure Macro AND Micro Conversions" on
  *Occam's Razor* (kaushik.net). **Primary-source grounded.** Macro
  = primary business outcome (purchase, lead); Micro = process
  milestones + secondary behaviors. NN/g formalized circa 2019.
- **Foot-in-the-Door (FITD)** — Freedman, J. L. & Fraser, S. C.
  (1966) "Compliance without pressure: The foot-in-the-door
  technique," *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 4(2),
  195–202. **Primary-source grounded.** Small prior commitment
  raises compliance with later larger request (76% vs 20% compliance
  in the lawn-sign study).
- **Cialdini Commitment & Consistency** — Cialdini, R. B. (1984)
  *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*, Morrow, Ch. 3.
  **Primary-source grounded.** Replicates and popularizes FITD;
  argues small-active-public-effortful-freely-chosen commitments
  escalate to larger compliance.
- **Soft/Hard Conversion** — practitioner vernacular (Choozle,
  Insivia, Search Engine Journal). **No single canonical academic
  primary source** — industry layering atop Kaushik's micro/macro
  distinction.

**Primary sources**:
- Kaushik, A. (2007/2008) "Excellent Analytics Tip #13" —
  https://www.kaushik.net/avinash/excellent-analytics-tip-13-measure-macro-and-micro-conversions/
- Freedman & Fraser (1966) *JPSP* 4(2), 195–202.
- Cialdini (1984) *Influence*, Morrow, Ch. 3.
- NN/g JP「マクロ/マイクロコンバージョン」— https://u-site.jp/alertbox/micro-conversions

**JP-specific terminology**: 「マイクロコンバージョン」/
「マクロコンバージョン」fully naturalized in JP digital marketing
(U-Site, Digital Identity, WACUL, Anagrams, Mieruca, Aun Consulting,
Lifrell). **No JP-specific term for "light-action copy vs heavy-
action copy" as a copywriting-framework axis.** JP copywriting texts
distinguish by **媒体** (sales-letter / LP / メルマガ / SNS) and
**購買段階** (認知 / 興味 / 比較検討 / 購入), not action commitment
weight per se.「フック型コピー」appears in practitioner blogs but
not formalized.

**Framework-to-action-weight mapping**: **Post-hoc analytical
synthesis, not documented JP canon**. JP sources document PASONA as
sales-letter / LP / DM framework and CREMA as light-commitment copy
framework, but the explicit pairing "CREMA/PREP → light action,
PASONA/QUEST/PASTOR → heavy action" is analytical layering over
documented framework length/structure characteristics, not a named
JP taxonomy. **Confidence the synthesis is defensible**: Medium-High.
**Confidence it pre-exists in literature**: Low. This standard must
OWN the synthesis and cite Kaushik + Cialdini as theoretical
grounding rather than JP copywriting canon.

**Connection to Cialdini commitment principle**: **Confirmed.**
Cialdini (1984) explicitly argues small active public commitments
escalate to larger compliance — theoretical bridge linking
light-action CTAs (micro-conversions) to downstream heavy-action
conversion. CXL and multiple digital-marketing secondary sources
dual-verify the Cialdini → micro-conversion funnel lineage.

**Confidence level**: High for micro/macro + Cialdini grounding;
Medium for soft/hard vernacular; **Low-Medium for the framework-to-
weight mapping being pre-existing** (it is largely our synthesis —
must flag as such in the standard).

**Unresolved**:
- No JP copywriting text explicitly organizes frameworks by
  action-weight axis.
- Whether "add to cart" counts as light or heavy action is contested
  (Kaushik: process milestone; some practitioners: hard conversion).
