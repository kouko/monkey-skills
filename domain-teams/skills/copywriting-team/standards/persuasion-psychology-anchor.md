# Persuasion Psychology Anchor

Tier 2 common-layer standard: provides a **Western psychology anchor**
for copywriting-team's JP frameworks (PASONA / PASBECONA / BEAF /
キャッチコピー). The JP frameworks are canonically established at the
structural level, but without psychological explanations for their
persuasion mechanisms they risk cookbook-style application. This
standard enumerates Cialdini's 6 principles, Schwartz's 5 awareness
levels, Kahneman's System 1/2, and Thaler & Sunstein's Nudge — four
psychology frameworks serving as copywriting's theoretical foundation.
This file governs "how to leverage psychological mechanisms for
persuasion"; ethical and legal boundaries (FTC / 景品表示法 / dark
patterns / 小霜「嘘をつかない」) are independently governed by the
ethics standard and are out of scope here.

## Primary Sources

- Robert Cialdini (1984/2006) *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*, Harper Business — canonical source for the 6 principles (reciprocity / commitment & consistency / social proof / authority / liking / scarcity). 1984 first edition; 2006 Collins Business is the full revision. The 7th principle (Unity) was added in 2016's *Pre-Suasion*.
- Eugene Schwartz (1966) *Breakthrough Advertising*, Boardroom/Bottom Line Personal. 2017 reprint by Titans Marketing — canonical source for long-form LP copywriting. Originator of "5 levels of awareness" and "3 stages of market sophistication." Schwartz is one of the four giants of 20th-century direct-response copywriting alongside Dan Kennedy, Gary Halbert, and David Ogilvy.
- Daniel Kahneman (2011) *Thinking, Fast and Slow*, Farrar, Straus and Giroux — System 1 (fast, intuitive, emotional) / System 2 (slow, deliberative, logical) dual-process theory; popularized source for prospect theory and loss aversion. Kahneman received the 2002 Nobel Prize in Economics.
- Richard Thaler & Cass Sunstein (2008) *Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness*, Yale University Press — canonical source for choice architecture, default options, and nudge. Thaler received the 2017 Nobel Prize in Economics. 2021 *Nudge: The Final Edition* published.

## Cialdini's 6 principles + copywriting application

Cialdini 1984/2006's 6 principles in canonical order. Each is a
**legitimate tool**; manipulated applications (false scarcity / fake
testimonials, etc.) fall under the ethics standard scope and are not
repeated here.

| # | Principle | Definition (Cialdini canonical) | Typical copywriting application | PASONA / PASBECONA anchor |
|---|------|-----------------------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Reciprocity | Receiving something free creates social pressure to reciprocate | Free trial, free PDF report, free webinar, lead magnet → main-offer staged design | Benefit / Offer stages: "we've already provided this much" meta-reciprocity |
| 2 | Commitment & Consistency | Obtaining a small commitment increases compliance with subsequent larger commitments | Stepped CTAs (email → trial → paid), yes-ladder, self-image appeals | Problem / Affinity stages: reader agreement with "you're struggling with X" pre-books subsequent Offer acceptance |
| 3 | Social Proof | Under uncertainty, people imitate majority behavior | Testimonials, "X people using", case studies, review counts / star ratings | Evidence stage's primary building block. Representativeness: FTC §255.2 (see ethics standard) |
| 4 | Authority | People follow expert / authority recommendations | Expert endorsements, credentials, scientific evidence / paper citations | Evidence stage's two pillars alongside social proof. False authority: FTC §255.1 violation |
| 5 | Liking | Similarity, familiarity, and attractiveness increase persuasion | Reader-segment self-projection narratives, brand persona, founder stories | Affinity stage (新 PASONA 2016) psychology anchor. 糸井 / 岩崎 voice as JP implementation |
| 6 | Scarcity | Limited availability / remaining stock / deadline raises perceived value | Limited quantity, limited time, restricted access | **Narrow down stage** anchor. **Truthfulness mandatory** (false scarcity = 景品表示法 有利誤認 — see ethics standard §False scarcity) |

Cialdini's 2016 *Pre-Suasion* added a 7th principle (Unity — communal
identity). v1.0.0 scopes only the above 6.

## Schwartz's 5 Levels of Awareness (essential for long-form LP)

Defined in Part 1 of Schwartz 1966 *Breakthrough Advertising*. The
reader's level of problem awareness / product knowledge determines
long-form copy structure and density. For long-form LP (PASBECONA
range), **diagnosing which awareness level the reader occupies** must
come first.

| Level | Reader state | Required copy | Matching framework (v1.0.0 scope) |
|-------|---------|-------------|-----------------------------|
| **1. Most Aware** | Product, price, features already known | Price, offer, bonus, immediacy | 旧 PASONA Narrow down + Action compression, short promotional email |
| **2. Product Aware** | Category known, comparing multiple options | Differentiation, comparative advantage, evidence, guarantee | BEAF (Advantage stage is core), 新 PASONA's Offer stage |
| **3. Solution Aware** | Knows solutions exist, does not know the specific product | Education on why this solution is superior | 新 PASONA's Solution stage, PASBECONA's Solution + Benefit |
| **4. Problem Aware** | Pain is self-recognized, resolution means unknown | Verbalize the problem, empathize, suggest resolution possibility | 新 PASONA's Problem + Affinity, PASBECONA's Problem + Affinity + Benefit |
| **5. Unaware** | Problem itself is not self-recognized | Story, metaphor, case studies to make the reader "discover" the problem | PASBECONA's Problem + Affinity + Benefit + Evidence (full long-form) |

**Core rule (Schwartz 1966)**: **using Offer as a closer directly on
Unaware readers is prohibited.** Skipping the awareness ladder
collapses conversion. Level 5 requires narrative-driven problem
discovery.

## Kahneman System 1 / System 2 — short vs. long copy allocation

Applying Kahneman 2011's dual-process theory to copywriting:

### System 1 (fast, intuitive, emotional)

- Snap judgment, no deliberation, driven by emotion and heuristics
- **Suited copy**: short form (キャッチコピー, headlines, SNS)
- **Weapons used**: emotion, rhythm, familiarity, visual coordination,
  7–15 character bodily impact
- **JP tradition mapping**: 糸井's "state proposal," 岩崎's "いのち
  lineage," first-second judgment in the 3 秒ルール — all System 1
  territory

### System 2 (slow, deliberative, logical)

- Logical verification, evidence evaluation, comparative analysis
- **Suited copy**: long form (PASBECONA-range / sales letter)
- **Weapons used**: argument structure, evidence, logical consistency,
  numbers, case studies
- **JP tradition mapping**: 神田 PASONA's Solution–Offer sequence
  persuades via the System 2 route

### Implication

**Division of labor within a single campaign**: short copy grabs
System 1 → body convinces System 2 → closing returns to System 1
emotion. Kahneman's insight is that "neither alone sells": if System 1
does not react, the body is never read; if System 2 is unconvinced,
purchase does not happen.

## Nudge (Thaler & Sunstein 2008) — choice architecture

Thaler & Sunstein 2008's nudge concept: the **presentation format of
choices** (choice architecture) has an outsized effect on decisions.
Copywriting relevance:

- **Power of default options**: opt-in vs. opt-out default settings
  dramatically shift subscription / enrollment rates. In the copy
  layer: presentation order of "default-selected options" and
  recommendation markers ("Most popular", etc.).
- **Anchoring**: presenting a reference-comparison point (list price →
  discount price). Risk of 景品表示法 有利誤認 violation, so the anchor
  must be truthful (see ethics standard §有利誤認).
- **Framing**: presenting the same fact as "90% survival rate" vs.
  "10% mortality rate" changes decisions (Kahneman 1979 prospect theory
  application). Directly affects copywriting vocabulary choices.

Nudge complements Cialdini's 6 principles: Cialdini is **the driver
of persuasion**; Nudge is **the design of the choice environment**.
Both are legal within the bounds of ethical libertarian paternalism;
when manipulated, they become dark patterns (see ethics standard).

## PASONA framework psychology-anchor mapping

Mapping Western psychology anchors to each stage of 新 PASONA
(神田 2016) / PASBECONA (神田・衣田 2021):

| PASONA stage | Primary psychology anchor | Supporting anchor |
|------------|------------------|-------------|
| Problem | Schwartz Level 4→3 shift | Kahneman System 1 (instant pain evocation) |
| Affinity | Cialdini Liking | 糸井 / 岩崎 JP emotional voice |
| Solution | Schwartz Level 3→2 shift | System 2 (logical validity) |
| Benefit (PASBECONA) | Kahneman prospect theory (gain frame) | Cialdini Authority (expert guarantees benefit) |
| Evidence (PASBECONA) | Cialdini Social Proof + Authority | System 2 (logical evidence verification) |
| Contents (PASBECONA) | Schwartz Level 2→1 shift | System 2 (feature comparison) |
| Offer | Kahneman loss aversion (loss-frame) | Cialdini Reciprocity (bonus reciprocity) |
| Narrowing | Cialdini Scarcity | **Truthfulness mandatory** — see ethics standard §景品表示法 |
| Action | Cialdini Commitment & Consistency | Nudge default / recommended marker |

This mapping is a tool for explaining "why PASONA works" through
psychology. It **cannot be used in reverse** (mechanically deriving
PASONA from psychology).

## Anti-Patterns

- **Using Offer as a closer directly on Unaware readers**. Skipping
  Schwartz's awareness ladder collapses conversion. Level 5 requires
  narrative + problem discovery.
- **Cramming System 2 elements into short copy**. Packing numbers,
  evidence, and logic into 7–15 character short copy disables the
  System 1 route, causing 3-second reader drop-off.
- **Writing long copy entirely with System 1 emotion**. For high-ticket
  / BtoB PASBECONA-level long copy, emotion-only (Agitation-heavy)
  fails to convince System 2, causing conversion bounce.
- **Cookbook-applying Cialdini's 6 principles mechanically**. Ignoring
  context, brand voice, and reader awareness to spam "scarcity, so
  only 3 left" damages trust (+ 景品表示法 risk — see ethics standard).
- **Selecting framework without Schwartz-level diagnosis**. Which
  PASONA variant (旧 / 新 / PASBECONA) to use is determined by the
  cross-product of awareness level and product complexity (Level 5 +
  high-ticket → PASBECONA / Level 1 + known → short copy).
- **Detaching anchors and framing from truth**. Nudge's legality
  depends on libertarian paternalism (choices themselves remain
  unchanged); false anchors violate 景品表示法 有利誤認.
- **Performing liking in Affinity stage while misaligned with brand
  voice**. Confirm alignment with the voice standard (voice standard
  reference).
- **Pushing loss aversion into intimidation**. The "loss" frame is
  powerful in System 1, but excessive agitation conflicts with
  小霜 2014's「嘘をつかない」and PASONA's anti-agitation thesis;
  ethics boundary is governed by the ethics standard.
