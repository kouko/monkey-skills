# Long-Form Copywriting — PASONA Canon

Tier 3 standard: fully self-contained. Consolidates the three PASONA-
family long-form frameworks originating in 神田昌典's canon —
旧 PASONA (5 stages), 新 PASONA (6 stages), PASBECONA (9 stages) —
defining book-first-publication year, stage definitions, applicable
character-count ranges, inter-stage flow design principles, and
ethics boundaries. This standard is the "long-form layer" canon
reference for copywriting-team v1.0.0's three-layer routing (long /
mid / short).

## Critical Attribution Corrections

- **Drift #4: "PASONA proposed in 1999" is conventional wisdom.**
  The popular narrative claims 神田昌典 proposed PASONA at seminars in
  1999 and published it in that year's『あなたの会社が 90 日で儲かる!』.
  However, **the 1999 book does not contain an explicit PASONA 5-stage
  formula** (per convergent testimony from secondary sources canvas-works
  / shanon). **Canonical first book publication is 2016's『稼ぐ言葉の
  法則』.** Literature citing 1999 as "first book appearance" relies on
  secondary-source hearsay and is not adopted as PASONA canon.

- **Drift #5: PASBECONA was named in 2021, not 2016.**
  The 2016 book『稼ぐ言葉の法則』is the book for **新 PASONA (6
  stages)**, not PASBECONA. The PASBECONA 9-stage structure (B/E/C
  insertion) was first systematized in **2021's『コピーライティング
  技術大全』**. Citations dating PASBECONA to 2016 are incorrect.
  Because B/E/C is co-authored by 神田 + 衣田順一, per-stage creditor
  attribution (whose idea each stage was) cannot be confirmed from
  secondary sources — label as co-authored.

- **Not a drift, but related caution: interpretations that push 旧
  PASONA's Agitation toward "aggressive fear appeal"** exist in some
  secondary sources, but since 神田 himself replaced Agitation with
  Affinity in 新 PASONA, aggressive escalation is not this canon's
  recommended approach (cross-reference with `persuasion-ethics.md`;
  addressed only in the Anti-Patterns section here).

## Framing — positioning the three frameworks

The PASONA family is **not a single formula but a 3-generation
framework family** used depending on character count, medium, and
persuasion depth. 旧 PASONA targets short-to-mid text (DM / email),
新 PASONA targets mid-to-long text (LP / newsletter), and PASBECONA
targets long text exclusively (web LP / high-ticket products). Three
switching axes:

- **Persuasion depth** — how much intermediate persuasion (Benefit +
  Evidence + Contents) is needed
- **Inter-stage drop-off risk** — as character count grows, drop-off
  points multiply, and Affinity (empathy) and Narrow down (scarcity)
  bear more weight
- **Ethical tone** — Agitation (fear appeal) may be tolerable in
  short copy, but in long copy, Affinity (empathy-based opening)
  outperforms in completion rate / LTV, as 神田 himself stated in 2016

## 旧 PASONA (5 stages) — 神田 2016 book-first publication

5-stage **P - A - So - N - A** structure. The boundary between
Agitation and Solution is loose; "So" is a legacy notation merging the
first two letters of Solution.

| Stage | Name | Stage mission (3–5 lines) |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | Problem (問題) | Articulate the reader's pain or challenge. Describe pain points in the reader's own words — even ones the reader has not yet verbalized — to trigger the recognition "this is about me." |
| **A** | Agitation (扇動 / 煽り) | Describe the worsening, loss, or missed opportunity if the problem is left unaddressed. Elevate Problem to "a problem that demands immediate action." |
| **So** | Solution (解決策) | Present the resolution principle (why / how it works) **and** the specific product/service **in the same stage** — a combined notation. This is the characteristic compression point of 旧 PASONA. |
| **N** | Narrow down (絞り込み) | Present scarcity of target, quantity, time window, and price. A double narrowing: "this solution is for you, and it's available only now." |
| **A** | Action (行動) | Present a concrete CTA (button copy, phone number, sign-up steps). Design for one-click completion with no hesitation. |

**Applicable character count**: 1,500–5,000 characters.
**Applicable media**: DM, email, short-to-mid sales letters, flyers,
telemarketing trial scripts.
**Characteristic**: the So combination makes persuasion dense but
skips intermediate empathy / evidence — suited for media where readers
already hold problem awareness.

## 新 PASONA (6 stages) — 神田 2016『稼ぐ言葉の法則』

6-stage **P - A - S - O - N - A** structure. Two canonical changes
from 旧: A (Agitation) is replaced by **Affinity (親近感)**, and So
(combined) is separated into **S (Solution) + O (Offer)**.

| Stage | Name | Stage mission (3–5 lines) |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | Problem (問題) | Same as 旧 PASONA. Describe the reader's pain in the reader's words. |
| **A** | **Affinity (親近感 / 共感)** | "I struggled with the same thing." "I understand how you feel." Stand alongside the reader. Replacing 旧 Agitation (fear appeal) with an empathy-based opening is canonical change #1. |
| **S** | Solution (解決策) | Present the resolution principle. Logical explanation of "why this approach works." Does not yet detail the specific product/service. |
| **O** | Offer (提案) | Present the specific product/service, price, guarantees, and bonuses. The back half of 旧 So made independent — canonical change #2. |
| **N** | Narrow down (絞り込み) | Target / quantity / time scarcity. Same as 旧 PASONA. |
| **A** | Action (行動) | Concrete CTA. Same as 旧 PASONA. |

**Canonical change rationale**:

- **Change #1 (A: Agitation → Affinity)**: short-term CVR optimization
  through fear appeal damages completion rate and LTV — per 神田's own
  reflection. Affinity means "running alongside the reader" with a
  foundation of reassurance, not anxiety.
- **Change #2 (So → S / O separation)**: separating principle (Solution)
  from concrete product (Offer) enables a two-step leap — "buy-in to
  the principle → anticipation of the product" — yielding smoother
  persuasion flow in mid-to-long text.

**Applicable character count**: 3,000–10,000 characters.
**Applicable media**: LP (landing page), newsletter series, white paper,
proposal documents triggering resource requests.

## PASBECONA (9 stages) — 神田・衣田 2021『コピーライティング技術大全』

9-stage **P - A - S - B - E - C - O - N - A** structure. Inserts
**B (Benefit) - E (Evidence) - C (Contents)** between 新 PASONA's S
(Solution) and O (Offer), canonically ensuring persuasion depth for
long copy.

| Stage | Name | Stage mission (3–5 lines) |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | Problem (問題) | Same as 新 PASONA. Articulate the reader's pain. |
| **A** | Affinity (親近感) | Same as 新 PASONA. Empathy-based opening. |
| **S** | Solution (解決策) | Resolution principle. Same as 新 PASONA. |
| **B** | **Benefit (利得)** | Depict how the reader's life changes after resolution — the ideal future picture. This is "the outcome obtained," not product specs. |
| **E** | **Evidence (証拠)** | Objective backing for Benefit. Authority (expert endorsement), track record (sales / awards), testimonials, statistical data. |
| **C** | **Contents (内容)** | Product/service detail. Composition, usage, included items, support offerings. Spec information is consolidated here. |
| **O** | Offer (提案) | Price, guarantee, bonuses, application terms. Same role as 新 PASONA O. |
| **N** | Narrow down (絞り込み) | Target / quantity / time scarcity. |
| **A** | Action (行動) | Concrete CTA. |

**Logic of B / E / C insertion**:

- **B (Benefit)** grounds Solution's abstraction in the reader's life
  picture. A translation step from "it can be solved" to "this is what
  I can become."
- **E (Evidence)** preemptively addresses doubt about Benefit. In long
  text, readers inevitably pause at "really?" — connecting evidence
  prevents drop-off.
- **C (Contents)** grounds the expectation built by B and E in the
  product's concrete reality. Spec / usage details appear here for the
  first time.
- The insertion order is **abstract (Benefit) → objective (Evidence)
  → concrete (Contents)**; reversing this order raises cognitive load
  and invites drop-off.

**Applicable character count**: 5,000–20,000+ characters.
**Applicable media**: web LP (high-ticket / coaching / BtoB), sales
letters, seminar invitation pages.

**B/E/C attribution uncertainty**: co-authored by 神田 + 衣田順一;
per-stage creditor attribution is unconfirmable from secondary sources.
This standard uses **co-author notation** and avoids per-stage
individual attribution.

## Three-framework applicability matrix (routing)

| Framework | Stages | Characters | Media examples | Persuasion depth | Tone origin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 旧 PASONA | 5 | 1,500–5,000 | DM, email, flyer | thin (So combined) | Agitation (fear appeal) |
| 新 PASONA | 6 | 3,000–10,000 | LP, newsletter, white paper | medium (S/O separated) | Affinity (empathy) |
| PASBECONA | 9 | 5,000–20,000+ | high-ticket LP, BtoB LP, seminar invite | deep (B/E/C inserted) | Affinity (empathy) |

**Selection criteria**:

- Target character count exceeds 5,000 → PASBECONA as default
- 3,000–5,000 range with Affinity-based tone → 新 PASONA
- Short-to-mid text (<3,000) targeting readers with existing problem
  awareness → 旧 PASONA
- Tone that forbids fear appeal (BtoB / medical / finance) → 旧 PASONA
  is not recommended

## Inter-stage flow design principles

Four canonical principles for designing long-form PASONA-family copy:

- **Explicit drop-off points**. Stage transition boundaries (P→A, A→S,
  S→B, etc.) are where readers are most likely to drop off. Use
  connectives, subheadings, and questions to "bridge to the next stage."
  In particular, the B→E boundary in PASBECONA is a critical transition
  where expectation meets evidence.
- **Character-count proportions**. For 旧 PASONA: P:A:So:N:A ≒
  2:2:3:2:1 is the canonical heuristic. For 新 / PASBECONA, O and E
  proportions grow with additional stages; for a 5,000-char PASBECONA,
  P:A:S:B:E:C:O:N:A ≒ 1:1:1:1:2:2:1:0.5:0.5 is the target.
- **Affinity depth criterion**. In 新 / PASBECONA, Affinity should
  receive at least as much space as Problem. Thin Affinity devolves
  into "empathy-sounding phrases" indistinguishable from Problem.
- **Narrow down and Action proximity**. N and A must be adjacent:
  place the CTA immediately after the reader perceives scarcity.
  Inserting another stage between them dissipates urgency.

## Ethics boundary (`persuasion-ethics.md` jurisdiction)

Excessive fear appeal in the Agitation stage (anxiety marketing,
over-use of fear appeals) is outside this canon's scope and is
governed by **`persuasion-ethics.md`**. This standard notes only that
"旧 PASONA's Agitation is tolerable in short-to-mid text, but the
Affinity replacement in long text is 神田's own reflection-derived
canon." Expression restrictions under 景品表示法, 薬機法, and
特定商取引法 are likewise handled on the ethics side.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Citing 1999 as book-first publication**. This is conventional
  wisdom; 2016's『稼ぐ言葉の法則』is the canonical first-publication
  year (drift #4).
- **Dating PASBECONA to 2016**. 2021's『コピーライティング技術大全』
  is the canonical naming year (drift #5). 2016 is 新 PASONA (6 stages).
- **Calling fear-style Agitation "Affinity"**. Affinity is empathy
  and accompaniment, not a softer synonym for Agitation. A paragraph
  ending with "aren't you worried?" is Agitation-leaning. A paragraph
  beginning with "I was the same" is Affinity.
- **Omitting Offer and going from Solution directly to Action**. In
  新 / PASBECONA, omitting Offer means price and guarantee are opaque
  at the CTA, dropping CVR. 旧 PASONA's So covers Offer implicitly,
  but from 新 onward, independent Offer is mandatory.
- **Placing Contents before Benefit in PASBECONA**. The abstract →
  objective → concrete order is canonical; reversing it raises
  cognitive load. Spec-first is an anti-pattern from 新 PASONA onward.
- **Attributing B/E/C stages to 神田 or 衣田 individually**. They are
  co-authored; attribution cannot be separated from secondary sources.
