# Protocol: Six-Dimension Analysis

Six dimensions run **regardless of lens selection**. They form the
structural backbone of every deconstruction. The selected lenses
populate detail; the six dimensions ensure no design layer is missed.

Run each dimension as a focused pass. Record findings before moving
to the next dimension.

## Dimension 1: Audience routing

**Question**: Who reads what, when?

Investigate:

- Who is the **primary** audience? (decision-maker / executor / observer / external)
- Who are the **secondary** audiences? (skim-readers / future-self / handover recipients)
- Who is **excluded** from the audience? (deliberately or by accident)
- What **routing signals** are embedded? (role-tagged sections, executive summaries, "TL;DR for X" blocks)
- How does the artifact handle **mixed-audience** moments? (does it write up vs write down?)

Output: 3–5 bullet observations, including 1 negative-space note ("the
artifact does not address Y audience because…").

## Dimension 2: Creation sequence

**Question**: What is the reading order vs the probable writing order?

Investigate:

- What is the **reading order** the audience experiences?
- What is the **probable writing order** based on internal evidence?
  - Forward references that resolve later → likely written later first
  - Smooth handoffs between sections → written sequentially
  - Inconsistencies in voice / depth → likely written in different sittings
- What was **revised last**? (executive summary, opening line, CTA — these often)
- What was **written first**? (the hard part — usually the proof or the data)

Output: 2–3 reverse-inferences with cited evidence.

## Dimension 3: Source genealogy

**Question**: What existing frameworks were borrowed?

Investigate:

- What **named frameworks** appear? (PASONA / OKR / RACI / 5 Whys / Toulmin / etc.)
- What **unnamed but recognizable** frameworks appear? (any structure with N sections that maps to a known canon)
- What **vocabulary** is borrowed from a specific tradition? ("blast radius" → SRE; "habit loops" → Duhigg; "circle of competence" → Munger)
- What **prior artifacts** are this descended from? (boilerplate? template? earlier version?)
- Are the borrowings **acknowledged or hidden**?

Output: A genealogy table with 3–7 entries:

| Borrowed from | How it appears | Acknowledged? |
|---|---|---|
| Cialdini | "social proof" framing in §3 | No (used as common knowledge) |
| Toulmin | claim-grounds-warrant in argument | Yes (cited footnote) |

## Dimension 4: Rhetorical structure

**Question**: How does it persuade?

This dimension *coordinates* with the lens passes — if you picked
`lens-rhetoric` or `lens-persuasion`, this dimension is where their
findings consolidate.

Investigate:

- What is the **opening hook**? (curiosity / scarcity / authority / social proof / pain)
- What is the **emotional arc**? (where does tension peak? where does relief land?)
- What is the **closing move**? (CTA / synthesis / cliffhanger / open question)
- Where is the **strongest claim**? (often centered or near the end)
- Where is the **weakest claim**? (often opening or in fine print)

Output: A 1-paragraph rhetorical structure summary + a 3-row table:

| Move | Where | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | line 1–3 | Curiosity gap |
| Climax | §4 | Authority + social proof stack |
| Closing | §6 | Personal CTA |

## Dimension 5: Design patterns

**Question**: What recurring techniques appear?

Investigate:

- **Repetition patterns**: same phrase / structure across sections (signals deliberate reinforcement)
- **Contrast patterns**: before/after, with/without, traditional/AI-augmented
- **Numbering patterns**: 3-step / 5-step / 7-tip — pattern itself is a meaning carrier
- **Visual patterns**: tables / blockquotes / asides — does the form reinforce the content?
- **Voice patterns**: where does "we" become "you"? where does instruction become invitation?

Output: 3–5 detected patterns, named, with examples from the artifact.

## Dimension 6: Negative space

**Question**: What is deliberately omitted?

This is the **most critical** dimension. What is *not* there is
information.

Investigate:

- **Missing genre moves**: if the artifact is a proposal, is the risk section present? If a rollout doc, is the failure-recovery section present?
- **Unsaid assumptions**: what foundational claim is treated as common knowledge but is not actually shared?
- **Excluded audiences**: who is *not* addressed who logically should be?
- **Avoided topics**: which obvious questions are not posed?
- **Quiet cases**: which counter-examples or rebuttals would the author have to acknowledge but doesn't?
- **Tonal omissions**: is there warmth where there should be? coldness where there should be?

Output: A "negative-space register" with 3–7 entries:

| What's missing | Significance |
|---|---|
| Risk section | Skipping reduces credibility but speeds adoption |
| Cost discussion | Avoided to delay budget conversations |
| Counter-examples | Acknowledging would weaken the central claim |

**Rule**: every deconstruction report must include at least 3
negative-space observations. If you find fewer, you have not looked
hard enough.
