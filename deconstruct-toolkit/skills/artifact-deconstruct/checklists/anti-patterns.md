# Anti-patterns Checklist

Run this self-check **before** delivering the deconstruction report.
A failing check requires revision, not just a mention.

## Output anti-patterns

### ❌ "Summary, not deconstruction"

- BAD: "This document covers A, B, C"
- GOOD: "This document deliberately starts with C to create tension before A"

**Check**: Does the report describe what *was decided*, not what *was written*?

### ❌ "Surface listing without depth"

- BAD: "Uses social proof"
- GOOD: "Uses social proof via 60% industry adoption claim. Verifiable. 🟢 transparent. Triggers Cialdini #3."

**Check**: For every detected mechanism, is there a specific citation + ethical position?

### ❌ "Single lens tunnel vision"

- BAD: Only running `lens-persuasion` on a multi-doc playbook
- GOOD: Combining `lens-genre + 6-dimension full pass`

**Check**: Did lens selection follow the protocol? Was at least 1 secondary lens considered (or explicitly skipped with reason)?

### ❌ "Missing the negative space"

- BAD: Only analyzing what IS written
- GOOD: Including 3+ "what's deliberately omitted and why"

**Check**: Does the report include at least 3 negative-space observations? (Mandatory minimum.)

### ❌ "No ethical position"

- BAD: Just describing persuasion mechanisms neutrally
- GOOD: Always classifying 🟢/🟡/🔴/⚫

**Check**: If `lens-persuasion` or `lens-ux` was applied, does every detected mechanism carry an ethical position?

### ❌ "Replicable lessons too abstract"

- BAD: "Use social proof" / "Be authoritative"
- GOOD: "Stack 3 social proof types (logo wall + count + named testimonial) above the fold to compound trust"

**Check**: Are the 5 replicable lessons specific enough that the reader could apply them to their own next artifact?

### ❌ "Bottom line missing or vague"

- BAD: "This is a good document"
- GOOD: "🟢 transparent rollout doc with all 6 canonical moves; distinctive choice is **segmented action by role**"

**Check**: Does the bottom line name (a) ethical position, (b) genre integrity, (c) the most distinctive design choice?

## Process anti-patterns

### ❌ Skipping lens selection

**Check**: Was Step 2 (lens selection) explicitly run? Or did the analysis go straight to applying lenses?

### ❌ Skipping six-dimension analysis

**Check**: Were all 6 dimensions covered in the report? Six runs regardless of lens choice.

### ❌ Reading lens files at the wrong time

**Check**: Lens reference files should be read **only in Step 4**, after lens selection. Reading them earlier biases the selection.

### ❌ Missing primary-source anchor

**Check**: For each lens applied, is the primary source acknowledged (Barthes / Burke / Toulmin / Goffman / Lakoff / Swales-Bhatia / Cialdini / Brignull / Norman-Nielsen)? Even a single citation is enough.

## Pre-delivery checklist (run all)

```
- [ ] Identified artifact type explicitly
- [ ] Justified lens selection (why these, not others)
- [ ] All 6 dimensions covered
- [ ] At least 3 negative-space observations
- [ ] At least 5 replicable lessons (specific, not abstract)
- [ ] At least 1 weakness or warning
- [ ] Ethical position assigned (if persuasion / UX lens used)
- [ ] Bottom-line one-liner present (with ethical position + distinctive choice)
- [ ] No pure summary statements (every observation is design-decision focused)
- [ ] Primary-source anchor present for each applied lens
```

If any item fails, **revise before delivering**. Do not deliver a partial deconstruction.
