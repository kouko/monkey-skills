# Hegelian Dialectics — Additional Cases

Extended examples for reference. Use these as inspiration when guiding
users through dialectical analysis.

## Education: Authority vs Student Voice

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thesis | Hierarchical education system: teachers hold authority, students follow curriculum |
| Antithesis | Authority suppresses critical thinking; students disengage when they have no voice |
| Synthesis | Participatory governance: institutional structure maintained with student councils and feedback loops |

Source: [Understanding Hegel's Dialectical Method — PolSci Institute](https://polsci.institute/western-political-thought/hegel-dialectical-method-thesis-antithesis-synthesis/)

## Economics: Planned Economy vs Free Market

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thesis | Socialist planned economy: state controls production for equality |
| Antithesis | Planning can't respond to local needs; innovation stalls without market signals |
| Synthesis | Mixed economy: market mechanisms with state intervention and social safety nets |

## Open Source vs Proprietary Software

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thesis | Open source: transparency, community, freedom |
| Antithesis | Sustainability crisis: no revenue model, maintainer burnout, security responsibility without compensation |
| Synthesis | Open core / source-available models: core is open, enterprise features fund development (e.g., GitLab, Sentry) |

## Remote Work vs Office Work

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thesis | Remote work: flexibility, no commute, global talent pool |
| Antithesis | Isolation, communication overhead, culture erosion, harder mentoring |
| Synthesis | Intentional hybrid: remote-first with periodic in-person gatherings designed for what remote can't do (bonding, brainstorming, onboarding) |

## Agile vs Waterfall

| Phase | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Thesis | Waterfall: plan everything upfront, execute sequentially |
| Antithesis | Requirements change; late integration reveals problems; users see the product too late |
| Synthesis | Not "do sprints instead" (that's compromise). Synthesis: continuous delivery with just-enough upfront design — spec what you know, iterate what you don't |

**Key insight**: Most "Agile transformations" are compromises (waterfall with standups),
not syntheses. True synthesis reframes: the question isn't "plan vs iterate" but
"what needs planning and what needs iteration?"
