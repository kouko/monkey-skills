# Ikigai Skill

**English** | [日本語](README.ja.md) | [繁體中文](README.zh-TW.md)

Analyze any project, product, or career through the 4-axis Ikigai framework
to find its purpose and diagnose gaps.

## The Four Axes

| Axis | Japanese | Meaning | Key Question |
|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | 好きなこと | What you love | Does this spark genuine passion? |
| 2 | 得意なこと | What you're good at | Do you have the skills to execute? |
| 3 | 世界が求めること | What the world needs | Is there real demand? |
| 4 | 報酬を得られること | What you can be paid for | Is it financially viable? |

## Method Type

Framework-driven analysis (not dialogue-driven like Socratic method).
Agent guides the user through each axis sequentially, then diagnoses
the intersection point or identifies missing axes.

Key principle: Ikigai lives at the intersection of ALL four axes.
When one or more axes are missing, specific symptoms emerge.

## Gap Diagnosis

| Missing Axis | Result | Symptom |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| What you love | Emptiness | Profitable and skilled, but no fulfillment |
| What you're good at | Helplessness | Passionate but unable to deliver results |
| What the world needs | Isolation | Skilled and passionate but no one cares |
| What you can be paid for | Instability | Loved and needed but financially unsustainable |

## Applicable Domains

| Domain | Axis 1 (Love) | Axis 2 (Skill) | Axis 3 (Need) | Axis 4 (Pay) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|--------------|
| Startup | Founder passion | Team capabilities | Market demand | Revenue model |
| Career | Daily motivation | Professional strengths | Industry demand | Salary/compensation |
| Product | User delight | Technical feasibility | User pain points | Business viability |
| Side Project | Weekend motivation | Existing skills | Audience size | Monetization path |

## Cultural Origin

Ikigai originates from Japanese culture, roughly translating to
"a reason for being." The concept gained international attention
through Dan Buettner's research on Okinawan longevity. The 4-axis
Venn diagram model, while a Western interpretation, provides a
practical framework for aligning passion, skill, demand, and viability.
