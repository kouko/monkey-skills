# Hansei Skill

Conduct blame-free retrospection after projects, decisions, or events
to extract structural insights and concrete next steps.

## The Four Phases

| Phase | Japanese | Question | Purpose |
|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| Confirm Facts | 事実確認 | What actually happened? | Separate facts from interpretation |
| Introspection | 内省 | What was MY role? | Examine personal blind spots without blame |
| Learning | 学び | What structural lesson emerges? | Go beyond "try harder" to systemic insight |
| Next Step | 次の一歩 | What one small action prevents recurrence? | Concrete, controllable, measurable action |

## Method Type

Phase-driven process (sequential stages building on each other).
Four phases in strict order: facts first, then introspection, then
structural learning, then one concrete action.

## Key Distinction from Western Postmortem

| | Western Postmortem | Hansei |
|--|-------------------|--------|
| Focus | What happened? Who was responsible? | What did I not see? What was my blind spot? |
| Tone | Analytical, objective | Humble, personal |
| Output | Action items for the team | One small step I can take tomorrow |
| Blame | Distributed or assigned | Not applicable — self-reflection only |

## Shallow vs Deep Learning

| Shallow | Deep |
|---------|------|
| "Pay more attention" | "Add a checklist so attention isn't required" |
| "Consult earlier" | "Define decision points that trigger automatic consultation" |
| "Allow more time" | "Quantify uncertainty explicitly in estimates" |
| "Improve communication" | "Build regular sync points into the process" |
