# team-mental-model

Surface each team member's mental model + negotiate convergence on fundamental values + sustain via measurable leadership-energy proxies (cadence / active-listening / value-revisitation). Not one-shot offsites — continuous-input requirement.

## When to use

- Team mental-model drift: "we used to just *get* it" / decisions taking 3x longer / code-and-design reviews now contentious despite individual excellence rising.
- Post-merger or post-acquisition alignment where `cld-overlay` has already found a straddle between groups and the integrated team needs to sustain convergence.
- Individually-excellent-but-team-slow signal: hired stars from FAANG / competitors, velocity dropped, "we need more team-building" framing keeps surfacing without measurable improvement.

## How to invoke

`/systems-thinking-toolkit:team` or via the router `/systems-thinking-toolkit:stt`.

## What you get

- Per-member causal loop diagrams (CLDs) of "what causes our team to succeed," compared side-by-side to locate divergent S/O labels on fundamental-value nodes.
- A co-authored values-convergence statement that every participant recognizes as their own (not a memo broadcast).
- Four observable leadership-energy proxies tracked over ≥ 1 quarter: cadence count (≥ 1 / week, team-self-initiated), active-listening minutes (≥ 15 min / hr), value-revisitation count (≥ 1 / week), and optional symbol-and-narrative refresh.

## Boundaries

- Psychological safety is a binding constraint — if team members fear speaking up, fix safety first (Edmondson). Surfacing without safety produces performative agreement, not genuine mental models.
- NOT for remote / distributed / async-first cultures — the energy-pumping metaphor assumes co-presence; Sherwood's "shared dinners over years" pattern pre-dates the 2020 hybrid-work transition and has no async-native operational definition.
- NOT a one-shot offsite or annual all-hands — Sherwood explicitly contrasts Nelson's continuous years of shared dinners with one-shot pep talks (the Coopers & Lybrand 100-partner anti-case).
- NOT for short-term task forces, cognitive-diversity-by-design teams (red team, devil's advocate, audit), or as a substitute for hiring / firing decisions.
- Period-dated examples: Nelson's Band of Brothers at Trafalgar (c18, the gold-standard exemplar) and Coopers & Lybrand 100-partner case (c19, the exhortation anti-pattern) are Sherwood's canonical illustrations.
