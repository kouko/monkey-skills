# Team Mental Model — Past Application Cases

Three calibrating cases for the `team-mental-model` skill: Nelson's
Band of Brothers at Trafalgar (c18, the gold-standard high-performing-
team exemplar), the Coopers & Lybrand 100-partner anti-case (c19, the
exhortation anti-pattern), and the theater-vs-dinner couple at n=2
(c17, the small-n boundary case where the inward `team-mental-model`
protocol collapses onto the same artifact as the outward `cld-overlay`
protocol).

Read all three before prescribing a team intervention — they encode
the Nelson energy-pump exemplar, the exhortation anti-pattern that
falsifies any "more speeches will fix it" instinct, and the n=2
interpersonal case showing the protocol's inward / outward boundary.

## Case 1 — Nelson's "Band of Brothers" at Trafalgar (c18)

- **Problem**: Build a fleet whose captains can execute audacious
  tactical innovation without continuous orders from the flagship in
  the chaos of battle.
- **Methodology applied**: Nelson personally selected each captain,
  then invested years in shared dinners, shared briefings, repeated
  value-and-tactics conversations. Each captain knew not only *what*
  Nelson would do but *why*. Continuous energy investment — not
  one-shot — sustained over years before any battle.
- **Conclusion**: At Trafalgar, the fleet executed unprecedented
  maneuver (breaking the line) with minimal signaling because mental
  models were aligned in advance. The order survived chaos because
  the open system had been kept open by years of leadership-energy
  throughput.
- **Outcome**: Sherwood's gold-standard high-performing-team exemplar.
  Shared mental models as emergent property of sustained leadership-
  energy investment — *years* of continuous flow, not an offsite. Cite
  this case as the calibration point for what "continuous" means in
  Step 5 of the execution protocol: weekly+, sustained for quarters
  (Nelson sustained for years), not annual.

## Case 2 — Coopers & Lybrand 100-Partner Anti-Case (c19)

- **Problem**: Professional-services firm with 100 partners —
  individually excellent, collectively unable to coordinate
  strategically. Repeated exhortations about teamwork did not change
  behavior.
- **Methodology applied**: Sherwood's diagnosis — mental models had
  drifted independently; no continuous energy investment in surfacing
  or aligning them. Senior leadership had substituted speeches and
  values workshops for sustained CLD-level convergence work.
- **Conclusion**: Exhortation does not substitute for energy
  throughput. A pep talk is a one-shot input to an open system that
  needs continuous flow. The team scored well on the proxy-zero
  baseline: cadence proxy = 0, active-listening minutes = 0,
  value-revisitation count = 0. All three primary proxies at zero =
  team is functionally closed regardless of stated commitment.
- **Outcome**: Cautionary anti-pattern. Sherwood explicitly contrasts
  with Nelson (Case 1) — same observable structure (group of high-
  performing individuals) but opposite outcome because the leadership-
  energy investment was missing. This is the case to cite when a
  senior leader proposes "one big all-hands" or "another values
  workshop" instead of sustained weekly+ practice. It is also the
  case to cite when arguing for the four observable proxies as the
  falsifiability bar — a Coopers-style leader will *believe* they are
  doing leadership while the proxies are all at zero.

## Case 3 — Theater-vs-Dinner Couple (c17) — n=2 boundary case

- **Problem**: A couple argues every Friday — one wants theater, the
  other dinner. Compromise alternation satisfies neither.
- **Methodology applied (inward harmony frame, this skill)**: Even at
  n=2, mental-model alignment is the binding constraint. Surface-level
  negotiation without CLD-level surfacing just rotates which partner
  is unhappy. The "team" is two people; the convergence-negotiation
  move applies in compressed form. The four observable proxies
  collapse to a single relational signal at n=2 (cadence = how often
  the couple genuinely revisits shared values; active-listening = how
  often each partner surfaces the other's CLD rather than advocating
  their own).
- **Conclusion**: Surface the underlying drivers (need for shared
  cultural experience vs need for intimate conversation time) and the
  surface dispute resolves into a values-causality conversation about
  what each partner believes makes the relationship succeed.
- **Outcome**: Interpersonal-scale validation of the inward harmony
  move. This case is deliberately the same artifact under both lenses
  — at n=2 the outward (`cld-overlay`) / inward (`team-mental-model`)
  boundary dissolves and the same diagrams serve both protocols. When
  the case appears at organizational scale (≥ 2 groups or a multi-
  person team), the protocols separate cleanly; this is the small-n
  collapse case that shows the underlying machinery is the same
  mental-model-surfacing move. At n=2 the energy-pump role is
  symmetric (both partners pump for each other); at organizational
  scale it requires a designated leader (Step 6 halt condition).
