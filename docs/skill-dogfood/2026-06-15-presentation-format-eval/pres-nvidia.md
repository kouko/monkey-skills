# Should I trim my Nvidia position?
## Summary
Nvidia's data-center business delivered extraordinary Q3 FY2025 results — $22.6B (+112% YoY), GAAP GM 74.6% — driven by Hopper AI buildout and a deep CUDA moat (4M+ devs). Blackwell claims 30x inference over H100 but initial ramp compresses GM into low-70s before yields recover. Bull case intact on fundamentals, but three compounding risks — hyperscaler concentration (~40–45% DC revenue from 4 clouds who also build custom silicon), export controls (China ~20–25%→mid-single-digit), valuation 35–38x fwd P/E — mean thin margin of safety. Whether to trim is a function of position sizing, cost basis, horizon, not a verdict the data alone delivers.
## Findings
- **Core DC momentum exceptional: $22.6B (+112% YoY), GM 74.6% (+18.5pp)** *(high)*
- **CUDA moat: 4M+ devs, dominant training stack** *(high)*
- **Export controls structurally reduced China DC ~20–25%→mid-single-digit; H20 licensing risk remains** *(high control / medium share)*
- **Blackwell 30x inference vs H100; ramp guides GM to low-70s** *(medium)*
- **Hyperscaler concentration double-edged: 4 customers ~40–45%, all building custom silicon; AMD MI300X at Azure/Meta, $5B 2024** *(medium)*
- **Valuation decompressed from >45x to ~35–38x fwd FY2026** *(medium)*
- **Sovereign AI double-digit % DC revenue per mgmt** *(low)*
## Caveats
Not financial advice; depends on cost basis/tax/concentration/horizon. China share analyst-estimated. 30x is Nvidia config. Hyperscaler silicon medium-to-long-term. Valuation late-2024 snapshot. Sovereign AI single blog.
## Open Questions

- Controls expand to H20?
- 35–38x supported by FY2027 estimates?
