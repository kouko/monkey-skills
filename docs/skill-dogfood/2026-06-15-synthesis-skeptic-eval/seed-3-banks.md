## ARM A — SINGLE-PASS SYNTHESIS
SUMMARY: US regional bank stocks offer a real yield premium — KRE constituents averaged ~3.8% versus the S&P 500's ~1.3% — and capital buffers (CET1 averaging 11.4%) look solid on paper. However, the sector's three-year total return of roughly −10% through end-2024 shows that dividend income has not compensated for price erosion, meaning total return has lagged broad equities despite the yield advantage. Two structural overhangs persist: HTM unrealized losses of $180–220B that could constrain capital flexibility, and CRE office concentrations at some lenders exceeding regulatory warning thresholds. Dividend cuts have occurred (KeyCorp halved its payout in 2023), so income is not uniform across the sector. Holding makes sense only if you accept credit-cycle volatility and are selective by institution rather than treating the sector as a homogeneous income play.
