## Items

### 1. 72-hour notification window

**Lock**: OPEN_QUESTION — no specific hour-count is currently in force for the 2025 amendments

**條文 (Article 12 paragraphs 1–4)**:
> 第 12 條
> 公務機關或非公務機關知悉所保有之個人資料被竊取、竄改、毀損、滅失或洩漏時，應通知當事人。（第一項）
>
> 前項情形符合一定通報範圍者，公務機關或非公務機關應通報下列機關：一、公務機關：向主管機關及依第二十一條之一第一項規定收受其實施情形之機關通報。二、非公務機關：向主管機關通報。主管機關受理通報後，並轉知其目的事業主管機關。（第二項）
>
> 第一項情形，公務機關或非公務機關應採取即時有效之應變措施，防止事故之擴大，及記載相關事實、影響、已採取之因應措施，並保存相關紀錄，以備主管機關查驗。（第三項）
>
> 前三項應通知或通報之內容、方式、時限與通報範圍、應變措施、紀錄保存及其他相關事項之辦法，由主管機關定之。（第四項）

**Current implementing regulation** (施行細則 Art. 22, last updated 2016):
> 本法第十二條所稱適當方式通知，指**即時**以言詞、書面、電話、簡訊、電子郵件、傳真、電子文件或其他足以使當事人知悉或可得知悉之方式為之。

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=12` — Art. 12, no specific hour count; delegates to implementing regulations
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050022&flno=22` — 施行細則 Art. 22 says "即時" (immediately), not a numbered-hours window. This regulation has NOT been updated since 2016
- Mondaq LST&C (Tier C) states "within 24 hours" for subject notification — this appears to interpret "即時" as 24 hours, but this is NOT verbatim law text
- No primary source confirms a "72-hour" standard (that language appears to be training-data bleed from EU GDPR's Art. 33)

**Confidence**: LOW — The statutory text explicitly defers the specific timeline ("時限") to PDPC sub-regulations under Art. 12 Para 4, which have NOT been published as of 2026-05-12. The old 施行細則 says "即時" without an hour count. Law firm commentary interprets this as "24 hours" for subject notification; 72-hour GDPR language should NOT be imported.

**Phase 2 implication**: The legal-incident-response runbook MUST NOT hard-code "72 hours." Instead, the template should use "即時" (immediately) as the current operative standard, with a note that PDPC will issue a specific 時限 办法 once the amendment takes effect. The notification-to-regulator trigger (Art. 12 Para 2) also depends on "一定通報範圍" which requires PDPC sub-regulations — currently undefined.

### 4. Cross-border transfer restrictions

**Lock**: LOCKED

**條文 (Article 21)**:
> 非公務機關為國際傳輸個人資料，而有下列情形之一者，中央主管機關得限制之：
> 一、涉及國家重大利益。
> 二、國際條約或協定有特別規定。
> 三、接受國對於個人資料之保護未有完善之法規，致有損當事人權益之虞。
> 四、以迂迴方法向第三國（地區）傳輸個人資料規避本法。

**Additional finding (Mondaq LST&C Tier-C)**:
> Government cloud services face strict restrictions: mainland Chinese cloud providers and ICT products are prohibited; data centers cannot be located in mainland China, Hong Kong, or Macao.

**Note on adequacy decisions**: Taiwan's 個資法 does NOT adopt the EU GDPR "adequacy decision" model. There is no whitelist of safe destination countries baked into the statute. Restrictions are imposed case-by-case by the competent authority when any of the four conditions are met. The primary criterion (款三) is whether the receiving country lacks "完善之法規" — but this is assessed by PDPC, not by the exporter unilaterally.

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=21` — Art. 21 four conditions
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=41` — criminal liability for circumventing cross-border restrictions

**Confidence**: HIGH — Article text is clear; the four restriction conditions are verbatim from the law

**Phase 2 implication**: DPA and privacy policy templates should state the Art. 21 four-condition framework for cross-border transfers, NOT the GDPR SCCs/adequacy model. For any template that covers mainland China transfers, add the government-cloud prohibition as a hard note. GDPR adequacy for Taiwan (EU → TW direction) is a separate matter not covered by 個資法.

### 5. Minor protection — age threshold and parental consent

**Lock**: OPEN_QUESTION — 個資法 contains NO dedicated minor protection provisions; falls back to general civil law

**Key facts**:
- 個人資料保護法 (all 56 articles including 2025/11 amendments) contains **no age threshold**, **no parental consent requirement**, and **no dedicated minor protection article**
- The search of 施行細則 (33 articles) also found no minor-specific provision
- No "未成年" or "未滿" language appears in the PDPA framework
- By implication, consent by minors falls under 民法 general rules:
  - Under 7 (未滿7歲): no legal capacity; guardian must consent
  - 7–20 (限制行為能力人, 滿7歲未滿18歲 per 民法 Art. 12 as amended in 2023): partial capacity; may require legal representative's consent or ratification for acts outside daily life
  - Over 18: full legal capacity

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawAll.aspx?pcode=I0050021` — confirmed no dedicated minor articles
- 民法 pcode B0000001 (not fetched in this run; referenced from general legal knowledge)
- Mondaq comparative guide: "The PDPA does not contain dedicated minor protection sections"

**Confidence**: LOW (on specifics) — HIGH (on the OPEN_QUESTION conclusion itself). There is no 個資法-specific answer; 民法 rules apply by default.

**Phase 2 implication**: Privacy policy template should reference 民法 行為能力 rules for minors. The template should include a note that for services targeting children under 12 or under 18, the platform should consider adding voluntary age-gating + parental consent flows as best practice, even though the law does not mandate them. Do NOT fabricate a specific age threshold from the PDPA.

### 6. Third-party SDK disclosure (cookies / pixels / analytics)

**Lock**: PARTIAL — cookies are personal data; specific SDK/pixel disclosure format not mandated in law text

**Key facts**:
- Art. 2 Para 1 defines 個人資料 broadly as any data that can "directly or indirectly identify" an individual — this covers cookies and device identifiers used for tracking
- Art. 8 requires telling data subjects about: (1) collector's name, (2) collection purpose, (3) data categories, (4) usage period/region/recipients/methods, (5) data subject rights, (6) consequences of refusal
- Mondaq comparative guide (Tier C): "cookies and similar online identifiers are regarded as personal data under Taiwanese law"
- No specific PDPA article mandates a separate "cookie consent banner" or SDK list in a privacy notice
- The disclosure of third-party SDKs/pixels flows from Art. 8 Para 1 款4 ("利用之…對象") — third-party recipients (analytics vendors, ad platforms) must be disclosed if they receive personal data

**條文 (Article 8 Para 1)**:
> 公務機關或非公務機關依第十五條或第十九條規定向當事人蒐集個人資料時，應明確告知當事人下列事項：
> 一、公務機關或非公務機關名稱。
> 二、蒐集之目的。
> 三、個人資料之類別。
> 四、個人資料利用之期間、地區、**對象**及方式。
> 五、當事人依第三條規定得行使之權利及方式。
> 六、當事人得自由選擇提供個人資料時，不提供將對其權益之影響。

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=8` — Art. 8 告知義務
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=2` — Art. 2 definition of 個人資料 (broad)
- Mondaq LST&C (Tier C): "cookies and similar online identifiers are regarded as personal data"

**Confidence**: MED — The legal basis is clear (Art. 8 款4 requires disclosing recipients/"對象" which includes analytics/ad vendors receiving tracking data). However, there is no PDPA-specific equivalent to ePrivacy / Cookie Law mandating an explicit cookie banner or per-SDK list. The practical standard for what "對象" disclosure looks like in a privacy policy is a regulatory practice question, not settled in statute text.

**Phase 2 implication**: Privacy policy template should include a section disclosing: (a) data categories from cookies/pixels, (b) third-party recipients (by category, e.g., "advertising platforms," "analytics services"), (c) whether the data is used for profiling/targeted advertising. A cookie-consent banner is best practice but NOT legally required by 個資法 alone. No need to enumerate individual SDK names if categories are described.

### 7. Controller vs processor liability

**Lock**: LOCKED — 個資法 uses "entrustment" (委託) model, not GDPR controller/processor split

**Key facts**:
- 個資法 Art. 2 definitions: **no definition of "控制者" (controller) or "處理者" (processor)**. The framework uses "公務機關" vs "非公務機關" and "委託者/受託者" (entrusting party / entrusted party)
- The liability allocation rule is in the general framework: the entrusting party (委託者) retains full liability for the entrusted party's actions
- Mondaq comparative guide (Tier C): "any entity entrusted by a public or non-public agency to collect, process or use personal data is, **within the scope of the PDPA, deemed to be the same as the entrusting agency.** Consequently, outsourcing or delegation does not exempt the principal agency from its statutory responsibilities."
- Art. 29 (non-public agency civil liability): liability is negated only if "能證明其無故意或過失" (no intent or negligence proven)
- Art. 28 Para 3 (court-assessed damages per person per incident): NT$500–NT$20,000
- Art. 28 Para 4 (aggregate cap for same cause of action): NT$200,000,000

**條文 (Articles 28–29, liability framework)**:
> 第 28 條 第三項：如被害人不易或不能證明其實際損害額時，得請求法院依侵害情節，以每人每一事件**新臺幣五百元以上二萬元以下**計算。
> 第 28 條 第四項：合計最高總額以**新臺幣二億元**為限。
> 第 29 條：非公務機關…違反…負損害賠償責任。但能證明其**無故意或過失**者，不在此限。

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=2` — Art. 2 definitions (no controller/processor)
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=28` — civil liability + damages
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=29` — non-public agency liability
- Mondaq LST&C (Tier C): "deemed to be the same as the entrusting agency"

**Confidence**: HIGH — This is settled law; the 2025/11 amendments did not change the liability framework or introduce GDPR-style controller/processor terminology.

**Phase 2 implication**: DPA (Data Processing Agreement) template should NOT use GDPR-style "Controller" / "Processor" / "Sub-Processor" terminology without explanation. Instead, use "委託方" (entrusting party) and "受託方" (entrusted party). The entrusting party bears the same statutory liability regardless of delegation. Include a clause that the 受託方 must comply with PDPA obligations as if it were the 委託方.

### 9. Required disclosure items in privacy notice (個資告知事項)

**Lock**: LOCKED

**條文 (Article 8 Para 1 — 蒐集時告知義務)**:
> 公務機關或非公務機關依第十五條或第十九條規定向當事人蒐集個人資料時，應明確告知當事人下列事項：
> 一、公務機關或非公務機關**名稱**。
> 二、蒐集之**目的**。
> 三、個人資料之**類別**。
> 四、個人資料利用之**期間、地區、對象及方式**。
> 五、當事人依第三條規定得行使之**權利及方式**。（查詢、閱覽、複製、補充、更正、停止蒐集/處理/利用、刪除）
> 六、當事人得自由選擇提供個人資料時，**不提供將對其權益之影響**。

**Exceptions to disclosure** (Art. 8 Para 2 — 得免為告知之情形):
When: (a) law permits waiver; (b) collection necessary for statutory duties; (c) notification would impede official functions; (d) notification would harm public interest; (e) subject already knows; (f) non-commercial collection without apparent harm.

**Art. 9 — indirect collection** (data not provided directly by subject):
> 公務機關或非公務機關…蒐集非由當事人提供之個人資料，應於處理或利用前，向當事人告知個人資料**來源**（plus the Art. 8 Para 1 items）

**Data subject rights** (Art. 3, cannot be pre-waived):
> 一、查詢或請求閱覽。二、請求製給複製本。三、請求補充或更正。四、請求停止蒐集、處理或利用。五、請求刪除。

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=8` — Art. 8
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=9` — Art. 9
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=3` — Art. 3

**Confidence**: HIGH — This is unchanged, long-standing law; not affected by which 2025/11 articles are effective or not.

**Phase 2 implication**: Privacy policy template MUST contain all six Art. 8 Para 1 items plus data source (Art. 9) if indirect collection is used. The "rights and method" section must enumerate all five Art. 3 rights with a contact method (typically: send written request to [DPO email/address]). This is the most settled, actionable section for SP3.

### 10. Data subject notification obligations (個人通知，非主管機關通報)

**Lock**: PARTIAL — obligation confirmed; specific timeline is "即時" (immediate), not an hour count

**Key facts**:
- Art. 12 Para 1 obligation: "知悉…時，**應通知**當事人" (must notify data subjects when *aware* of a breach)
  - Trigger: **知悉** (becomes aware), not "確認" (confirms) or any specific investigation period
  - Events: stolen (竊取), altered (竄改), destroyed (毀損), lost (滅失), or disclosed (洩漏)
- 施行細則 Art. 22: notification must be "**即時**" (immediately), by any reasonably effective method
- Notification content: Art. 22 Para 3 of 施行細則 — must include (a) **facts of the breach** and (b) **response measures already taken**
- If cost is prohibitive, public notice (internet/news media) is permissible

**條文 (施行細則 Art. 22)**:
> 本法第十二條所稱適當方式通知，指**即時**以言詞、書面、電話、簡訊、電子郵件、傳真、電子文件或其他足以使當事人知悉或可得知悉之方式為之。
> …但需費過鉅者，得斟酌技術之可行性及當事人隱私之保護，以網際網路、新聞媒體或其他適當公開方式為之。
> 依本法第十二條規定通知當事人，其內容應包括個人資料被侵害之**事實**及已採取之**因應措施**。

**Note**: The 2025/11 amendments to Art. 12 Para 4 will override the 2016 施行細則 Art. 22 once the new PDPC sub-regulations are published. Until then, "即時" + two-item content standard remains the operative rule.

**Sources**:
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050021&flno=12` — Art. 12
- `https://law.moj.gov.tw/LawClass/LawSingle.aspx?pcode=I0050022&flno=22` — 施行細則 Art. 22 (current operative standard)

**Confidence**: MED — Trigger and content are locked; specific hour count is not locked (awaiting PDPC sub-regulations).

**Phase 2 implication**: The incident-response runbook should structure the 個人通知 step as: "Upon becoming aware (知悉) of a breach → immediately (即時) notify affected data subjects" including: (a) what happened (事實), (b) response measures taken (因應措施). Use "即時" as the standard. Once PDPC sub-regulations take effect, update with specific timeline.
