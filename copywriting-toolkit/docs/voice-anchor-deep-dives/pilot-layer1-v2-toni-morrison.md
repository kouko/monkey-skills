### Toni Morrison — lyrical inheritance (EN | Q2 toward-Q3, author)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Transmit Black American memory as heritable cadence — sentences that carry ancestor-presence into the reader's ear through sermonic rhythm, vernacular compression, and matrilineal intimacy, so a fact about a body becomes a fact about a people.

**Native critical read**:
- "the ancestor" (Morrison, "Rootedness: The Ancestor as Foundation," 1984 — "timeless people whose relationships to the characters are benevolent, instructive, and protective")
- "the presence of an ancestor" as craft requirement distinguishing Black literature from "city values" writing (Morrison, "Rootedness," 1984)
- "oral quality" — Morrison: "my writing expects, demands participatory reading… the affective and the cognitive experience must be invited in" (Paris Review, *Art of Fiction No. 134*, 1993)
- "the vitality of language" — Nobel Lecture 1993: "We die. That may be the meaning of life. But we do language. That may be the measure of our lives."
- "sermonic / call-and-response structure" (Henry Louis Gates Jr., on Morrison's syntactic inheritance from Black church sermon)
- "discredited knowledge" — Morrison's term ("Rootedness") for ancestral register she refuses to sanitise into "universal" English

## Prose mechanics
- Open in medias res with declarative fragment that sounds spoken, not written: subject + predicate no scene-setting ("124 was spiteful."). No establishing paragraph
- Deploy **appositive cascade** — stack 2-4 renamings of a noun separated by commas, each rename deepening rather than listing
- Let Black vernacular enter unmarked — no italics, no "dialect" flags, no translator's distance. A grandmother's speech and narrator's speech share one register
- Use **sermonic triad**: three parallel clauses, the third breaking rhythm by length or reversal ("Not to need permission for desire — well now, that was freedom."). One per passage, never two
- Anchor abstract claims with body-part specificity (hands, feet, neck, mouth, hair) before abstraction — never "grief", always "grief that lived in the hollow of her collarbone"
- Summon ancestor by naming absent elder in present tense within the sentence that describes the living ("the baby's ghost," "Pilate who had no navel")
- Permit second-person plural ("we," "our") to emerge from third-person narration for one beat, then recede — Nobel-Lecture move, enfolds reader into communal memory
- End paragraphs on monosyllabic word or short sentence — percussive closure that leaves breath ("It was not a story to pass on.")

## Examples
- "124 was spiteful. Full of a baby's venom." (*Beloved*, 1987, opening)
- "She is a friend of my mind. She gather me, man. The pieces I am, she gather them and give them back to me in all the right order." (*Beloved*, 1987, Paul D / Sixo's description of the Thirty-Mile Woman)
- "It was not a story to pass on." (*Beloved*, 1987, closing coda — repeated three times, sermonic triad)
- "The North Carolina Mutual Life Insurance agent promised to fly from Mercy to the other side of Lake Superior at three o'clock." (*Song of Solomon*, 1977, opening)
- "You wanna fly, you got to give up the shit that weighs you down." (*Song of Solomon*, 1977, Pilate)
- "In that place, where they tore the nightshade and blackberry patches from their roots to make room for the Medallion City Golf Course, there was once a neighborhood." (*Sula*, 1973, opening)
- "Quiet as it's kept, there were no marigolds in the fall of 1941." (*The Bluest Eye*, 1970, opening)
- "We die. That may be the meaning of life. But we do language. That may be the measure of our lives." (Nobel Lecture, 1993)

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM lyricism collapses into decorative parallelism — triadic clauses everywhere, body-parts sprinkled as ornament, "she carried her grief in her hands" pastiche untethered from ancestor-presence. Reads as museum-Black-voice: cadence without the communal addressee that makes the cadence mean
- **Mitigation**: "Name one specific ancestor in-grammar per passage; no triad without earned rhythm"
