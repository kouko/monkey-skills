### Phoebe Waller-Bridge — Fleabag fourth-wall (EN | Q3 extreme, screenwriter)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Build conspiratorial intimacy by turning reader into the only confidant, then detonate that intimacy with a confession or grief-beat the reader wasn't braced for. The fourth wall is not a trick — it is the entire love affair; breaking it is how the speaker flirts, deflects, and eventually tells the truth.

**Native critical read** (5):
- "She's a seductress narrator — she's trying to get the audience to fancy her, and then she has to earn back their trust." (Waller-Bridge, BAFTA Screenwriters' Lecture, April 2019)
- "Complicity is the engine. The camera-look makes you the accomplice — so when she turns, you've already been in the crime." (Waller-Bridge, *Fleabag: The Scriptures* foreword, Faber 2019)
- "Filthy, funny and devastating — a dirty joke that suddenly asks you to hold its hand." (Lucy Mangan, *The Guardian* S2 review, March 2019)
- "Weaponised charm." (Sian Cain profile, *The Guardian*, March 2019)
- "She uses the aside the way Shakespeare used the soliloquy, except she's flirting with you instead of thinking out loud." (Vulture S2 critical register)

## Prose mechanics (8)
- **Comic-devastation pivot within 2 sentences**: set up with deflective joke, swerve to grief/confession on next beat. Never telegraph the turn — no "but honestly" / "the truth is" bridge
- **Deflection-as-confession**: the joke IS the admission. If the line is funny, the grief underneath must be exact; if the grief is named, the joke must not soften it
- **Second-person aside ≤1 per paragraph**: the camera-look works because it is rationed. Over-use collapses it into quirk
- **End on the flinch, not the explanation**: cut the line on "Sorry." / "This is a love story." / the unfinished clause. No interpretive tag follows
- **Clinical register for bodily / sexual / shameful content**: flat vocabulary — the comedy is the flatness, not the word choice
- **Apology as recurring punctuation**: "Sorry." lands as deflection, punchline, and grief-marker — let the same three-letter line do three jobs
- **Catalogue escalation into self-indictment**: pile adjectives in a long list, then indict yourself at the end (`greedy, perverted, selfish… who can't even call herself a feminist`)
- **No therapy vocabulary**: avoid "trauma", "processing", "healing", "holding space", "self-care"

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM defaults into *constant* direct-address asides ("Hi, reader!" "Between us…" "You and I both know…"), turning fourth-wall break into twee tic; pairs every vulnerable line with immediate comic deflection in SAME sentence, which reads as sitcom cadence, not Waller-Bridge. Register collapses when confession and joke are served on same beat instead of across two beats
- **Mitigation** (≤15 words): "One aside per paragraph; confession and joke on separate beats; end on the flinch"
