### Mailchimp Voice & Tone (EN | Q3 center)

## Voice direction

**What this register achieves**: Peer-expert translator voice — demystifies technical work without condescending, maintains warmth without saccharine, stays humor-disciplined (no jokes in error / money / compliance / unsubscribe). Since 2021 Intuit acquisition, framework evolved to "Expert Absurdist" — convention-subverting but never at clarity's expense.

**Native critical read** (5):
- "Expert Absurdist" (post-2021 Intuit brand-character framing, contentdesign.intuit.com/voice-tone/mailchimp/ — "Expert ingenuity × Absurdist subverts the expected")
- "Prefer winking to shouting / wry over farcical / subtle over noisy" (humor-calibration triad, styleguide.mailchimp.com/voice-and-tone/)
- "Clear / Useful / Friendly / Appropriate" (4 content-quality axes — clarity > entertainment always, writing-principles page)
- "Translators" (self-identity — demystify without inflating, anti-enterprise-ese)
- "Freddie doesn't talk" (mascot-silence rule, GitHub content-style-guide — brand voice stays unified to copywriter register, not mascot register)

## Prose mechanics (8)

1. **Contractions always** — "They give your writing an informal, friendly tone" (grammar-and-mechanics page) — never "do not" / "cannot" in flow copy
2. **Em-dashes without spaces for asides** — `text—aside—text`; semicolons minimized; prefer em-dash or new sentence
3. **Exclamation points sparingly, NEVER in error messages** — explicit ban
4. **Emoji infrequently and deliberately; NEVER in error / money / compliance flows**
5. **Lowercase always: website, internet, online, email** — anti-corporate capitalization
6. **Affirmative framing over negative** — "Remember to…" not "Don't forget to…"
7. **Humor-suppression contexts** — NO humor in error states / money flows / private-info / compliance shutdowns / unsubscribe (Kate Kiefer Lee canon, "keep the comedy out of your microcopy")
8. **Anti-grandiose bans** — exaggeration / "powerful" / "seamless" / "effortless" / enterprise-ese / B2B-speak / patronizing language; Mailchimp self-identifies as "Translators"

## Examples (6 verbatim product copy)

- "Oops, something went wrong. Please try again later." (general form error — canonical template: plain apology + clear next step, NO emoji, NO joke, NO euphemism)
- "High five!" (post-campaign-send celebration — short affirmation + Freddie visual carries warmth; copy stays terse)
- "We had to shut down your account because you were spamming." (compliance — subject + verb + reason, no softening, no euphemism)
- "Your campaign is on its way." (send confirmation — reassurance over celebration)
- "This is your moment of glory." (pre-send confirm screen — warm-theatrical, winks doesn't shout)
- "David admires his parents, Oprah, and Justin Timberlake." (Oxford-comma example sentence used in public style guide — peer-wink register even in a grammar rule)

## Don't / Over-mimic

- **Failure mode**: Modern LLMs default to Mailchimp-ish baseline (contractions + plain English + warmth) automatically — style guide is in training data. LLMs FAIL on: (a) humor-suppression in error / money / compliance (LLM defaults to "soften with whimsy"); (b) Expert Absurdist convention-subversion (LLM defaults to convention-safe warmth); (c) anti-grandiose word bans (LLM slips into "powerful" / "seamless"); (d) Freddie-doesn't-talk (LLM personifies mascots — Duolingo-owl drift).
- **Mitigation** (≤15 words): "Cite Mailchimp for humor-suppression / anti-grandiose / Expert Absurdist — NOT generic warmth"
