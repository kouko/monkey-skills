## § 1. Visual-dominant campaigns (do-not-emulate list)

These campaigns are canonical in advertising history but **cannot serve as voice anchors** because copy alone represents <20% of the communicative work. Voice emulation from these sources produces hollow pseudo-versions.

If a brief cites one of these ("我們要 Benetton 感的文案"), the pipeline should **reframe** rather than attempt emulation — reply with what IS emulable (the underlying register) and what is not (the visual/directorial execution).

### 1.1 Benetton / Oliviero Toscani (1984-2000)

- **Type**: Fashion brand campaign run
- **Why visual-dominant**: Toscani is a PHOTOGRAPHER, not a copywriter. Every canonical campaign piece (dying AIDS patient / priest kissing nun / bloody newborn / death-row inmates) carries message through photography + placement. Headline is always "United Colors of Benetton" — 4 words, brand tagline not copy
- **What to preserve**: emulating the visual transgression paradigm; recognizing anti-establishment brand-positioning register
- **What's NOT emulable**: Toscani's provocation works because the PHOTO is unflinching; 4-word tagline alone doesn't carry it
- **Primary source**: *United Colors: Benetton* (Taschen, 1996); Toscani *Die Werbung ist ein lächelndes Aas* (Mannheim, 1996)

### 1.2 Thai Life Insurance / TrueMove H (Ogilvy Bangkok, 2011-2014)

- **Type**: Long-form emotional TVC
- **Canonical pieces**: "Silence of Love" (2011), "Giving" (TrueMove H, 2013), "Unsung Hero" (Thai Life, 2014)
- **Why visual-dominant**: Cinematic long-form (3-5 min), dialogue-minimal, emotional power from casting + score + pacing + lived-experience storytelling. Written copy = 5-word closing card ("Giving is the best communication" / "Believe in good")
- **What to preserve**: emotional-narrative structure model for long-form brand film
- **What's NOT emulable**: the closing-card-alone gives zero voice signature; LLM emulating "Thai voice" from these produces generic moral aphorisms
- **Primary source**: Cannes Lions Bronze (2014); The Drum "World's Best Ads" feature

### 1.3 Apple "1984" single spot (Chiat/Day, 1984)

- **Type**: Single TVC
- **Why visual-dominant**: Ridley Scott directorial; 95% of impact lives in dystopian visual dynamic + hammer-throw symbolism. Spoken copy = 30 seconds VO ("On January 24th, Apple Computer will introduce Macintosh...")
- **What to preserve**: manifesto-ambition positioning for tech launch
- **What's NOT emulable**: copy alone is event announcement, not voice-bearing
- **Note**: Apple "Think Different" (1997-2011) IS a voice anchor and remains in active inventory. "1984" single spot is excluded.
- **Primary source**: Cannes Grand Prize 1984; Smithsonian NMAH collection

### 1.4 Guinness "Surfer" (AMV BBDO, 1999)

- **Type**: Single TVC
- **Why visual-dominant**: Jonathan Glazer directorial; voice lives in combination of white-horses visual crescendo + orchestral crescendo + 30-sec wait-narrative. VO lines ("He waits. That's what he does." / "Here's to waiting.") are contemplative bridges, not stand-alone voice
- **What to preserve**: patience-premium argument structure
- **What's NOT emulable**: short VO lines reproduced standalone are generic
- **Primary source**: Cannes Gold Lion Film 1999; D&AD Black Pencil

### 1.5 Old Spice "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" (W+K, 2010)

- **Type**: TVC series
- **Why visual-dominant**: Isaiah Mustafa's performance + deadpan delivery carries 60%+ of comedy; absurdist scene transitions carry another 20%. Written lines ("Hello, ladies...") read flat without vocal cadence
- **What to preserve**: surrealist-absurd male-beauty category subversion
- **What's NOT emulable**: script-only emulation produces generic deadpan
- **Primary source**: Cannes Grand Prix Film 2010; W+K case archive
