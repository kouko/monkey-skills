## Cluster 1: Neta Technique Academic Grounding

### Canonical primary sources

- **Lakoff, G. & Johnson, M.** (1980) *Metaphors We Live By*. University
  of Chicago Press. **Foundational.** Conceptual-metaphor theory:
  metaphor is cognitive structure (cross-domain mapping), not
  ornament. Anchors Technique 4 (Cross-domain Mapping).
- **McQuarrie, E. F. & Mick, D. G.** (1996) "Figures of Rhetoric in
  Advertising Language." *Journal of Consumer Research*, 22(4),
  424–438. Oxford UP / ACR. **Canonical.** Taxonomy of four rhetorical
  operations: **repetition / reversal / substitution / destabilization**
  split across schemes vs tropes.
- **Ott, B. L. & Walter, C.** (2000) "Intertextuality: Interpretive
  Practice and Textual Strategy." *Critical Studies in Media
  Communication*, 17(4), 429–446. Three strategies: **parodic
  allusion / creative appropriation / self-reflexive reference.**
  Explicitly claims parody depends on reader's prior knowledge —
  grounds Technique 3 (Subcultural Capital).
- **McQuarrie, E. F. & Phillips, B. J.** (2008, eds.) *Go Figure!*.
  Extension to visual rhetoric.
- **Dery, M.** (1991) "Subvertising: The Billboard Liberation Front's
  Hit and Run Media Art." — subvertising as documented rhetorical
  operation grounding Technique 1 (Reversal); EN industry vernacular
  includes "subvertising" and "culture jamming."

### Humor theory anchors

- **Suls, J.** (1972) "A two-stage model for the appreciation of
  jokes and cartoons." In Goldstein & McGhee (eds.) *The Psychology
  of Humor*. Academic Press. **Canonical** incongruity-resolution
  model.
- **Raskin, V.** (1985) *Semantic Mechanisms of Humor*.
  Reidel/Springer. Script-based Semantic Theory of Humor (SSTH);
  later extended to GTVH (Attardo & Raskin 1991).
- **Ritchie, G.** (1999/2004) *The Linguistic Analysis of Jokes*;
  "Developing the Incongruity-Resolution Theory." Formalizes Suls.
- **McGraw, A. P. & Warren, C.** (2010) "Benign Violations: Making
  Immoral Behavior Funny." *Psychological Science*, 21(8),
  1141–1149. **Canonical.** Three conditions: violation + benign
  + simultaneous.

### Subcultural capital anchor

- **Thornton, S.** (1995) *Club Cultures: Music, Media and Subcultural
  Capital*. Wesleyan UP. Bourdieu extension. "Being in the know" as
  form of cultural capital. Grounds Technique 3 (Subcultural Capital);
  current JP frontline practitioner term is 界隈消費 (kaiwai shōhi).
- **Bourdieu, P.** (1984/1979) *Distinction: A Social Critique of the
  Judgement of Taste*. Harvard UP. Foundation for cultural capital.
- **Spence, M.** (1973) "Job Market Signaling." *Quarterly Journal of
  Economics*, 87(3), 355–374. Signaling theory primary source.
- **Pullum, G. K.** (2003) / **Whitman, G.** (2004) "snowclone"
  (phrasal-template linguistics). Grounds Technique 2 (Substitution);
  JP SNS native expression is 大喜利 (oogiri) culture.

### JP academic literature

- **東京家政大学「広告における『しゃれ』・『だじゃれ』の多用に関する
  一考察」**(CiNii Research). JP academic treatment of pun/ダジャレ in
  advertising.
- **呂晶 (北海道大学)「日本語広告表現の語用論的研究：形式と機能に
  着目して」**— pragmatic study.
- **辻大介「言語行為としての広告」**— speech-act framing.
- **「日英語の広告言語表現に関する認知言語学的分析」**(東洋大学)
  — cognitive-linguistic (Lakoff-lineage) JP+EN ad analysis.
- **Walter Dill Scott** (1903) *The Theory of Advertising*.
  Long-standing 広告心理学 chair tradition (関西大学 etc.).

No single JP authoritative monograph on "パロディ広告" emerged;
handled case-by-case within 広告心理学 / 認知言語学.

### Attribution drifts (cluster 1)

- **Drift #20 (cluster 1)**: "Hitchon 1991 — Adaptation of Lakoff and
  Johnson's Theory of Metaphor to Advertising Language" — this title
  is informal / dissertation phrasing. The actually-published 1991
  ACR paper is **Hitchon, J. C. (1991) "Effects of metaphorical vs.
  literal headlines on advertising persuasion." *Advances in
  Consumer Research*, 18, 752–753.** Cite this shorter ACR proceedings
  paper; do NOT cite the "Adaptation..." title as a standalone work.
- **Drift #21**: 「次元降下 (jigen kōka)」JP vernacular — borrows from Liu Cixin's
  *The Three-Body Problem* (三体, 2008) SF universe where 降維打擊
  is a physics-SF concept. Team repurposes the term as a copy-craft
  label; this is NOT rhetoric-canon terminology and must be
  transparently flagged.
- **Drift #22**: McQuarrie & Mick's "operations" are about rhetorical
  **figures**, not explicitly about "neta injection." Team's
  mapping (reversal ↔ Technique 1, substitution ↔ Technique 2) is
  interpretive, not a direct quote.

## Cluster 3: Cringe Risk + Legal Grounding

### Primary sources — copyright + fair use

- **17 U.S. Code § 107** — fair use four factors. Text confirmed via
  Cornell LII. https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/107
- **Campbell v. Acuff-Rose Music, 510 U.S. 569 (1994)** — U.S.
  Supreme Court. **Canonical** for transformative-use doctrine; parody
  as fair use. Opinion by Souter. Confirmed via U.S. Copyright Office
  + Cornell.
- **Louis Vuitton Malletier S.A. v. Haute Diggity Dog, LLC, 507 F.3d
  252 (4th Cir. 2007)** — parody trademark dual-message test
  (communicates it IS the original + it is NOT). Confirmed via Justia.
- **Japan 著作権法 Article 32** — 引用 (quotation) permission.
  Judicial doctrine layers: **主従関係** (main-subordinate relationship)
  + **明瞭区別性** (clear distinction). Anchored in モンタージュ写真
  事件 最高裁 昭和55年 (1980) and subsequent case law.

### 景品表示法 2024 ステマ告示

- **Enforcement: 2023-10-01** via 消費者庁 official page title.
  https://www.caa.go.jp/policies/policy/representation/fair_labeling/stealth_marketing/
- **Two-prong test** grounded in 景品表示法 5条3号 告示 + 2023-03-28
  運用基準:
  1. 事業者が表示内容の決定に関与している (brand-influenced content)
  2. 一般消費者が事業者の表示であることを判別困難 (consumer cannot
     identify as brand content)
- **Brand is the liable party, NOT the creator/influencer.**
- Penalty: 消費者庁 措置命令 + public disclosure; repeat violations
  can escalate to criminal liability under 景表法.

### Documented cringe marketing failures

- **McDonald's #McDStories (2012-01)** — brand-hashtag hijacked
  into customer complaints within hours. Primary: NYT, BuzzFeed.
- **DiGiorno #WhyIStayed (2014-09)** — DiGiorno's pizza tweet on a
  domestic-violence survivor hashtag; public apology required.
  Primary: NYT.
- **Pepsi Kendall Jenner "Live for Now" (2017-04)** — protest-
  imagery parody read as trivializing social justice movements;
  pulled within 24h. Primary: Reuters, NYT.
- **ペヤング 異物混入 crisis + SNS mishandling (2014)** — JP case;
  SNS response intensified backlash. Primary: J-CAST.
- **Academic analysis**: Kucuk, S. U. (2019) *Brand Hate:
  Navigating Consumer Negativity*. Palgrave Macmillan. Taxonomy of
  brand backlash.

### Subcultural capital + signaling anchors

- **Spence, M.** (1973) "Job Market Signaling." *QJE* 87(3), 355–374.

### Safety gate dimension scoring (recommendation)

**Traffic-light per dimension + hard legal vetoes** (not composite
numeric). Legal/ステマ risk is non-fungible with taste risk —
failing legal threshold cannot be offset by passing taste threshold.

| Dimension | Scoring | Threshold |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Cringe index | Red / Yellow / Green (subjective judgment with stated reasoning) | Red = NEEDS_REVISION |
| Copyright / trademark risk | **HARD VETO** (fair use / 著作権法32条 applicability) | Any unmitigated red = NEEDS_REVISION (non-fungible) |
| ステマ risk (JP brand-paid UGC-like content) | **HARD VETO** (brand-influence + identifiability test) | Any unmitigated red = NEEDS_REVISION |
| 圈層 match (in-group fit) | Traffic-light | Red = NEEDS_REVISION; Yellow = PASS_WITH_NOTES |
| Timeliness (meme currency) | Traffic-light | Red = NEEDS_REVISION; Yellow = PASS_WITH_NOTES |

### Attribution drifts (cluster 3)

- **Drift #23**: "Transformative use" — sometimes attributed to
  Campbell (1994). Concept was articulated earlier by **Judge Pierre
  N. Leval** in (1990) "Toward a Fair Use Standard," *Harvard Law
  Review*, 103(5), 1105–1136. Campbell adopted Leval's formulation;
  Leval predates Campbell by 4 years.
- **Drift #24**: "主従関係 / 明瞭区別性" — these are judicial
  doctrine layers on 著作権法 32条, not literal statutory wording.
  Avoid citing them as "32条の要件".
