## Cluster A: FCB Grid + Halliday SFL Register Theory

### A-1. Vaughn — FCB Grid (CONFIRMED)

- **Vaughn, R.** (1980) "How Advertising Works: A Planning Model."
  *Journal of Advertising Research*, 20(5), 27–33.
  DOI 10.1080/00218499.1980.12518790.
- **Vaughn, R.** (1986) "How Advertising Works: A Planning Model
  Revisited." *JAR*, 26(1), 57–66.
  DOI 10.1080/00218499.1986.12467874.
- **Two axes**: Involvement (high/low) × Think/Feel (processing mode).
  Four quadrants: Informative / Habit formation / Affective /
  Self-satisfaction.
- **Verdict on Think/Feel → Reason↔Emotion mapping**: GROUNDED but as
  plausible extension. Vaughn's Think/Feel classifies *consumer
  cognitive processing toward products*, not message style. Mapping
  it to message-voice Reason↔Emotion is a widely used practitioner
  extension, but not a verbatim Vaughn citation.

### A-2. Halliday — Systemic Functional Linguistics (CONFIRMED)

- **Halliday, M.A.K.** (1978) *Language as Social Semiotic*.
  Edward Arnold, London. **Foundational.**
- **Halliday & Hasan** (1985) *Language, Context, and Text*.
  Deakin UP / OUP 1989. ISBN 0194371549. **Canonical register text.**
- **Halliday & Matthiessen** (2004) *An Introduction to Functional
  Grammar*, 3rd ed. Hodder Arnold.
- **Register has 3 dimensions**: Field (activity), **Tenor** (participant
  relationships, power, solidarity, formality), Mode (medium).
- **Verdict on Tenor → Authority↔Affinity mapping**: PARTIALLY GROUNDED.
  Tenor canonically contains Power + Solidarity as *orthogonal*
  sub-dimensions (Poynton 1985 later formalized Power, Contact,
  Affective Involvement). Our Authority↔Affinity axis **compresses**
  these into one continuum — this compression is team synthesis.

### A-3. Combined FCB + SFL for Brand Voice (NOT FOUND)

No published paper combines Vaughn's FCB Grid with Halliday's SFL
register theory for brand voice positioning. Adjacent work:
- Kitchen, Tourky & Dean (2022) "Remembering the FCB Grid."
  *Journal of Advertising*, 51(3) — revisits FCB for social media;
  no SFL.
- SFL applied to advertising texts exists (register analysis of ad
  copy), but not cross-walked to FCB Grid.

**This combination is original team synthesis.** The standard must
disclose this explicitly.

### A-4. Drift Corrections

- **Drift #25**: Our Voice Quadrant's axes are **grounded in** Vaughn
  and Halliday, but the 2-axis combination is **this team's synthesis**,
  not a pre-existing published framework. Must flag in standard.

## Cluster B: Brand Archetypes — Contested Framework

### B-1. Mark & Pearson — Canonical Source (CONFIRMED)

- **Mark, M. & Pearson, C. S.** (2001) *The Hero and the Outlaw:
  Building Extraordinary Brands Through the Power of Archetypes*.
  McGraw-Hill. ISBN 978-0071364157. **Practitioner canonical.**
- **12 archetypes** grouped into 4 motivational quadrants: Innocent,
  Explorer, Sage, Hero, Outlaw, Magician, Everyman, Lover, Jester,
  Caregiver, Creator, Ruler.
- Explicitly framed as brand-facing adaptation of Jungian archetypes.

### B-2. Jung — Academic Origin (CONFIRMED)

- **Jung, C. G.** (1919) "Instinct and the Unconscious." *British
  Journal of Psychology*, 10(1), 15–23. First formal introduction of
  archetype concept.
- **Jung, C. G.** (1959) *The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious*.
  Collected Works Vol. 9, Part I. Bollingen Series XX. Princeton UP.

### B-3. Peer-Reviewed Critiques (REQUIRED for contested framework)

- **Neher, A.** (1996) "Jung's Theory of Archetypes: A Critique."
  *Journal of Humanistic Psychology*, 36(2), 61–91. **Canonical
  critique.** No scientific basis for heritable transmission of
  complex symbolic patterns.
- **Roesler, C.** (2023) "Revisiting Carl Jung's archetype theory."
  *Biosystems*. Modern genetics/neuroscience still lack evidentiary
  grounding for original archetype concept.
- **Xara-Brasil, Hamza & Marquina** (2018) "The meaning of a brand?
  An archetypal approach." *Revista de Gestão*, 25(2), 142–152.
  Empirical test (537 respondents, 4 countries) — original 12-way
  taxonomy **reduces to 4 clusters** under consumer-perception data.
- **Dias & Dias** (2022) "Exploring the changing role of brand
  archetypes in customer-brand relationships." *Business Horizons*.
  Analysis of 2,400+ brands — **single-archetype doctrine empirically
  refuted**; strong brands leverage multiple archetypes.

### B-4. Empirical Status Assessment

- Jung's substrate (collective unconscious) lacks genetic/neuroscience
  support (Neher 1996; Roesler 2023).
- Mark & Pearson's 12-category taxonomy fails to reproduce under
  empirical data (Xara-Brasil 2018 reduces to 4; Dias & Dias rejects
  single-archetype).
- **Framework is practitioner-driven, not empirically validated.**

### B-5. Standard Usage Rule

- Cite as **practitioner heuristic** with wide industry adoption
- **Do NOT build gate criteria** solely on archetype conformance
- Pair with stronger primary sources (Halliday SFL, FCB Grid)
- Use as voice-ideation prompt, NOT as quality gate
