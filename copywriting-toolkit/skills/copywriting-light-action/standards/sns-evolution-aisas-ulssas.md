# SNS-Era Consumer Behavior Evolution — AIDMA → AISAS → SIPS → ULSSAS

Tier 2 standard: references `short-form-catchcopy-canon.md` for the
AIDMA baseline and `persuasion-psychology-anchor.md` for Schwartz
awareness levels. This standard traces the evolution of consumer
behavior models from pre-internet AIDMA through SNS-era ULSSAS,
documenting each model's canonical source, stage definitions,
structural innovations, and — most critically — their implications
for how a copywriter should write differently in each era.

The progression is not replacement but accumulation: AIDMA remains
valid for specific contexts even in the ULSSAS era. The copywriter's
job is to identify which model best describes the target audience's
behavior and write accordingly.

## Primary Sources

### AIDMA

- Samuel Roland Hall (1924) *Retail Advertising and Selling*.
  McGraw-Hill. **canonical** for the 5-stage formalization
  (Attention - Interest - Desire - Memory - Action).
- Already documented in `short-form-catchcopy-canon.md` §Primary
  Sources. This standard cross-references rather than duplicates.

### AISAS

- 秋山隆平・杉山恒太郎 (2004)『ホリスティック・コミュニケーション』
  宣伝会議. ISBN 978-4883350940.
  **canonical** for AISAS (Attention - Interest - Search - Action -
  Share). Published as a **book**, not a 電通報 article.
- 電通 trademark registered June 2005.
- Dual-verify JP sources: MarketingPedia, 日経クロステック.

### SIPS

- 佐藤尚之 [さとなお] (2011-01-31) サトナオ・オープン・ラボ
  announcement.
  **canonical** for SIPS (Sympathize - Identify - Participate -
  Share & Spread). 佐藤 is a 電通 alumnus (pen name さとなお).
- Dual-verify JP sources: 電通報 coverage, marketing blogs.

### ULSSAS

- 飯髙悠太 (2019-08-29)『僕らはSNSでモノを買う』
  **ディスカヴァー・トゥエンティワン**. ISBN 978-4799325292.
  **canonical** for ULSSAS (UGC - Like - Search1 - Search2 -
  Action - Spread). 飯髙 was ホットリンク CMO at time of
  publication.
- Dual-verify JP sources: Hottolink official blog, MarkeZine.

## Critical Attribution Corrections

- **Drift #11: ULSSAS canonical year is 2019, not 2015.** Some
  secondary sources date ULSSAS to 2015 (ホットリンク concept
  origin). The canonical year is **2019** (book publication).
  The concept may have crystallized during 2018-19 at ホットリンク,
  but the 2019 book is the citable canonical source.

- **Drift #12: ULSSAS publisher is ディスカヴァー・トゥエンティワン,
  NOT すばる舎.** Multiple secondary sources cite the wrong
  publisher.

- **Drift #13: AISAS originates from a book, NOT a 電通報
  article.** The canonical source is 秋山・杉山 (2004)
  『ホリスティック・コミュニケーション』(宣伝会議). Citations
  attributing AISAS to a 電通報 article are incorrect.

## Timeline and structural evolution

```
1924  AIDMA ─── Hall (McGraw-Hill)
        │        passive audience, memory-dependent
        │
2004  AISAS ─── 秋山・杉山 (電通/宣伝会議)
        │        active searcher, Share as new stage
        │
2011  SIPS ──── 佐藤尚之 (電通 alumnus)
        │        empathy-driven, purchase optional
        │
2019  ULSSAS ── 飯髙悠太 (ホットリンク/ディスカヴァー)
                 UGC-flywheel, circular not linear
```

The evolution follows a clear vector:
**passive → active-searcher → empathy-driven → UGC-flywheel**

Each successor does not invalidate its predecessor — it describes
a different audience behavior pattern that emerged with new media
technology.

## AIDMA (1924) — baseline

Already documented in `short-form-catchcopy-canon.md`. Key points
for this standard's comparative purpose:

| Stage | Name | Behavior |
|-------|------|----------|
| A | Attention | Notice the ad |
| I | Interest | Become interested |
| D | Desire | Want the product |
| M | Memory | Remember the ad/product |
| A | Action | Purchase |

**Structural characteristic**: linear funnel, one-directional,
memory-dependent (assumes time gap between exposure and purchase).
No peer influence, no search behavior, no sharing.

**Still valid for**: BtoB long purchase cycles, TVCM FMCG
(fast-moving consumer goods), 店頭販促 (in-store promotion),
low-digital demographics, print/radio/outdoor advertising
targeting audiences with limited internet behavior.

## AISAS (2004) — the active searcher

| Stage | Name | Behavior | Innovation vs AIDMA |
|-------|------|----------|---------------------|
| A | Attention | Notice the ad | Same as AIDMA |
| I | Interest | Become interested | Same as AIDMA |
| S | **Search** | Actively search for information | **New**: replaces Desire+Memory with active information-seeking |
| A | Action | Purchase | Same as AIDMA |
| S | **Share** | Share experience with others | **New**: post-purchase behavior feeds back into others' Attention |

**Key structural innovation**: the consumer is no longer passive.
After Interest, they **search** (Google, review sites, comparison
sites) before deciding. After Action, they **share** (blogs,
review posts), creating a feedback loop.

**電通 trademark** (June 2005) means AISAS is formally owned
terminology. Use with appropriate attribution.

**Copywriting implication**: copy must survive the Search stage.
Claims made in the Attention/Interest stages will be verified by
the consumer. Unsubstantiated claims → search reveals the gap →
purchase abandoned. Cross-reference with `persuasion-ethics.md`
§表示規制.

## SIPS (2011) — empathy replaces attention

| Stage | Name | Behavior | Innovation vs AISAS |
|-------|------|----------|---------------------|
| S | **Sympathize** | Feel empathy with the message/brand | **Replaces Attention**: the entry point is emotional resonance, not noticing |
| I | **Identify** | Confirm the empathy is genuine ("this is really for me") | **New**: self-verification stage between empathy and action |
| P | **Participate** | Engage (like, comment, share, attend) — purchase is ONE form of participation, not the only one | **Reframes Action**: purchase is optional; participation is the metric |
| S | **Share & Spread** | Share the participation with others | Extended from AISAS Share |

**Key structural innovation**: 共感 (empathy/sympathy) replaces
Attention as the entry point. In a feed-based social media
environment, users don't "notice ads" — they resonate (or don't)
with content that appears in their stream. Purchase is demoted
from the goal to one of many participation forms.

**佐藤尚之's framing**: SIPS describes behavior in "social media
native" contexts where brand-consumer relationships are
peer-mediated, not advertiser-mediated.

**Copywriting implication**: the copywriter's goal shifts from
"capture attention → drive to purchase" to "create resonance →
enable participation." Copy must be empathy-first, not
benefit-first. This aligns with 新 PASONA's Affinity stage and
小霜's instinct-driven approach (both start from empathy/desire
rather than problem/fear).

## ULSSAS (2019) — the UGC flywheel

| Stage | Name | Behavior | Innovation vs SIPS |
|-------|------|----------|---------------------|
| U | **UGC** (User Generated Content) | Others' posts, reviews, photos trigger awareness | **New entry point**: awareness comes from peers, not brands |
| L | **Like** | Like/react to the UGC | Social signal that amplifies UGC reach |
| S1 | **Search 1 (SNS)** | Search within social platforms for more UGC | **Split Search**: social-first search (Twitter/Instagram/TikTok) |
| S2 | **Search 2 (Web)** | Search on web (Google, review sites) for verification | Traditional web search as verification layer |
| A | **Action** | Purchase | Same as predecessors |
| S | **Spread** | Post own UGC about the experience | **Closes the loop**: new UGC feeds back to U stage |

**Key structural innovation**: **circular flywheel, not linear
funnel**. The Spread stage generates new UGC, which becomes the
U stage for the next consumer. The brand's role shifts from
"creating the message" to "seeding conditions for UGC generation."

**Dual search (S1 + S2)**: consumers search social platforms first
(for authentic peer experiences) and web second (for official
information and price comparison). This dual-search pattern means
copy must be optimized for both discovery contexts.

**飯髙's framing**: in the ULSSAS era, the most effective
"advertising" is not advertising at all — it's facilitating the
conditions under which users create and share content about the
product.

**Copywriting implication**: the ULSSAS-era copywriter writes
**UGC-triggering seeds**, not final persuasion pieces. Copy
should be:
- **Quotable**: short enough to be screenshot-shared or quoted
  in tweets
- **Opinion-provoking**: triggers "I agree/disagree" responses
  that generate discussion UGC
- **Remixable**: contains hooks that users naturally adapt to
  their own context (templates, fill-in-the-blank structures)
- **Search-surviving**: discoverable in both SNS and web search
  contexts

## Model applicability matrix

| Model | Era | Entry point | Purchase role | Feedback loop | Best fit |
|-------|-----|-------------|---------------|---------------|----------|
| AIDMA | Pre-internet | Attention (notice) | Goal | None | BtoB, TVCM, print, 店頭, low-digital |
| AISAS | Search era | Attention | Goal | Share → others' Attention | E-commerce, comparison-heavy, review-dependent |
| SIPS | Social era | Sympathize | One form of participation | Share & Spread → others' Sympathize | Brand community, lifestyle, value-driven |
| ULSSAS | UGC era | UGC (peer content) | One stage in flywheel | Spread → new UGC (circular) | SNS-native, D2C, experience products, viral |

### Selection criteria for copywriting

- **Target audience is AIDMA-pattern**: older demographics,
  B2B with long decision cycles, offline-primary touchpoints
  → write for attention capture and memory retention
- **Target audience is AISAS-pattern**: actively comparing
  products online, reading reviews before purchase
  → write for search-survivability and share-worthiness
- **Target audience is SIPS-pattern**: engages with brands
  through social media, values brand alignment with personal
  identity
  → write for empathy-first resonance and participation
  enablement
- **Target audience is ULSSAS-pattern**: discovers products
  through peer UGC, trusts peer content over brand content
  → write UGC-triggering seeds, not traditional persuasion copy

**Mixed-model reality**: most campaigns target audiences with
mixed behavior patterns. A campaign may need AIDMA copy for TV
spots, AISAS copy for the landing page, and ULSSAS copy for
social media. The copywriter should identify the dominant model
per touchpoint and adapt accordingly.

## Integration with other copywriting-team standards

### With `short-form-catchcopy-canon.md`

The short-form canon already references AIDMA and briefly mentions
AISAS/ULSSAS. This standard provides the full model definitions
and copywriting implications that the short-form canon summarizes.

### With `long-form-pasona-canon.md` and `long-form-extended-frameworks.md`

PASONA-family frameworks (and QUEST/PASTOR) are primarily
**AIDMA-to-AISAS era** tools — they assume the copy IS the
persuasion vehicle. In SIPS/ULSSAS contexts, these frameworks
still apply to the landing page, but the copy that gets people
TO the landing page operates under different rules (empathy-first,
UGC-triggering).

### With `kosimo-instinct-analysis.md`

小霜's instinct-driven approach aligns naturally with SIPS
(empathy-first entry) and ULSSAS (instinct-triggering content
that generates organic sharing). The 本能 lens is particularly
useful for writing UGC-triggering seeds — copy that activates
unconscious recognition ("this is exactly how I feel") naturally
generates share/spread behavior.

## Ethics boundary

The ULSSAS model's emphasis on UGC-triggering raises specific
ethical considerations:

- **Stealth marketing (ステルスマーケティング / ステマ)**: writing
  copy designed to appear as organic UGC while being brand-paid
  is a violation of both 景品表示法 and this team's ethics
  standards. UGC-triggering means creating conditions for genuine
  UGC, not fabricating fake UGC.
- **Review manipulation**: writing copy that incentivizes fake
  reviews or manipulated ratings violates `persuasion-ethics.md`.
- **Viral-first, truth-second**: writing intentionally
  controversial or misleading copy to trigger sharing violates
  義 (小霜's ethical foundation) even if it generates UGC.

Cross-reference: `persuasion-ethics.md` §ステマ告示 for
the 2023 景品表示法 amendment on stealth marketing.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Citing AISAS as originating from a 電通報 article**. Canonical
  source is the 2004 book『ホリスティック・コミュニケーション』by
  秋山・杉山 (drift #13).
- **Citing ULSSAS as a 2015 concept**. Canonical year is 2019
  (book publication) (drift #11).
- **Citing すばる舎 as ULSSAS publisher**. Canonical publisher is
  ディスカヴァー・トゥエンティワン (drift #12).
- **Treating AIDMA as "obsolete"**. AIDMA remains valid for
  specific contexts (BtoB, TVCM FMCG, low-digital audiences).
  Each model describes a behavior pattern, not a historical era.
- **Treating ULSSAS as a linear funnel**. ULSSAS is a circular
  flywheel — Spread → new UGC is the defining structural
  innovation. Drawing it as a straight line misses the point.
- **Writing traditional persuasion copy for ULSSAS contexts**.
  In UGC-flywheel contexts, the copy's job is to trigger sharing,
  not to close the sale. A perfectly structured PASONA piece may
  generate zero UGC if it's not quotable, opinion-provoking, or
  remixable.
- **Ignoring the dual-search pattern (S1 + S2)**. ULSSAS consumers
  search social platforms FIRST, then web. Copy optimized only for
  web search (SEO) may miss the SNS search discovery path entirely.
- **Fabricating UGC to simulate ULSSAS behavior**. This is stealth
  marketing (ステマ), not ULSSAS-era copywriting. The model
  describes organic behavior; the copywriter's job is to facilitate
  it, not fake it.
- **Applying SIPS/ULSSAS models to BtoB long-cycle purchases
  without adaptation**. BtoB purchase decisions involve multiple
  stakeholders, budget cycles, and procurement processes that
  SIPS/ULSSAS don't model. Use AIDMA or AISAS as the base model,
  with SIPS/ULSSAS considerations for the social-proof layer only.
