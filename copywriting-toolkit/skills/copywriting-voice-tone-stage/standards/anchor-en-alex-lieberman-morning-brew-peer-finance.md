### Alex Lieberman — Morning Brew peer-finance register (EN | Q3 center, named founder-writer recast)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Translate Wall-Street-Journal-grade financial news into a register a 25-year-old reads voluntarily at 6am — irreverent, punny, bullet-dense, and parenthetically self-aware, without sacrificing factual load or ticker-level specificity.

## Native critical read
- "serious-but-fun financial news" (Lieberman, Businessweek profile 2019; stated mission since founding)
- "the Wall Street Journal for Gen-Z" (NYT, Business section profile of Morning Brew 2020)
- "smart but not stiff" (Lieberman, Businessbarista tweet / podcast interview recurring phrase)
- "pun-forward subject line" (Brew internal edit convention, surfaced in Lieberman's Founder's Journal Substack 2021 retro on newsletter craft)
- "the Friday Puzzle / trivia closer" (Brew reader-facing nickname for the signature sign-off beat)

## Prose mechanics
- Open every edition with a dated-news hook followed by a one-line pun or wink — the pun earns the right to the rest of the paragraph. No cold open, no formal lede.
- Headline formula: `{Company/Index}: {punny verb-phrase}`. Prefer verbs that double as finance slang (Tanks, Pops, Jumps, Bleeds, Cooks) over neutral "Announces / Reports".
- Bullet everything three items or more. Bullets are sentence-fragments, not full sentences — start with a noun or a verb, not "The company".
- One parenthetical aside per ~150 words, voiced as narrator-to-reader ("(yes, really)", "(we checked)", "(insert Succession reference here)"). The aside does the emotional labor the main clause refuses.
- Number-plus-context formula: never cite a figure without a one-clause comparison. `$3.2B — or roughly one Twitter acquisition` beats `$3.2B in revenue`.
- Native-ad integration uses the fixed header "Together with {Sponsor}" then maintains Brew voice (same pun cadence, same bullet logic) — no tonal seam between editorial and sponsor block.
- Close with a trivia question, a chart-of-the-day, or a one-line "Bottom Line" that re-states the thesis in Gen-Z idiom.
- Pop-culture reference density: roughly one per section. TV (Succession, Office), memes, sports — not literary. Avoid references older than the median reader (no Boomer canon).
- Sentence length: financial clause short (8-14 words), aside even shorter (3-8 words). Never exceed 25 words without a dash, parenthesis, or bullet break.

## Examples
- "Good morning. It's Monday, which means yesterday was Sunday, which means some of you are nursing a hangover. We've got you." (Morning Brew daily, edition open, 2019)
- "Markets: The S&P 500 notched its fourth straight record close because apparently nothing can stop this thing. Not a trade war. Not an inverted yield curve. Not Mercury in retrograde." (Morning Brew daily, Markets section, late 2019)
- "WeWork's IPO: An Adam Neumann Short Story" (Morning Brew subject line, September 2019, pre-pulled S-1 week)
- "Together with Robinhood. Commission-free trading is cool. You know what's cooler? Not losing your shirt. Robinhood's new learning hub wants to help with that second part." (Morning Brew native-ad block, 2020)
- "Bottom line: Boeing's having the kind of year that makes you want to take the train." (Morning Brew daily, Bottom Line closer, 2019 737 MAX coverage)
- "Netflix added 15.8 million subscribers last quarter — or roughly the population of the Netherlands, minus a few people who still use DVDs." (Morning Brew daily, earnings recap, Q2 2020)
- "The Fed cut rates. Again. At this point Jerome Powell is just handing out rate cuts like Oprah hands out cars." (Morning Brew daily, Fed recap, 2019)
- "Building a media company in 2015 was either brave or stupid. The jury's still deliberating." (@businessbarista / Lieberman, Twitter, 2020)

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM defaults into what the industry now calls "Brew voice" — pun-stacked lede, bulleted everything, parenthetical in every sentence, ironic Gen-Z sign-off. The template has been copied across 1440, The Hustle, Milk Road, Future Party, Chartr — so "playful finance newsletter" collapses into a single homogenized register with no specificity. Secondary failure: punning over content gravity (pun on layoffs, pun on deaths, pun on bank failures) — Lieberman's Brew stayed restrained on genuine loss; copycats do not.
- **Mitigation**: "One pun per section max; no pun on layoffs / death / failure; keep figures anchored."
