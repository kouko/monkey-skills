### Richard Reed — Innocent Drinks (EN | Q3 center, 1999-2009 canonical "wackaging" era)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Talk to the shopper as a literate friend passing a note across the café table — handwritten-feel fragments, lightly self-deprecating disclosure of the recipe, and a live phone line ("banana phone") that promises an actual human will pick up. The register converts a supermarket bottle into a one-to-one aside.

## Native critical read
- "Wackaging" — coined by copywriter Nick Asbury (Guardian, 2010) describing the template Reed pioneered from 1999; used pejoratively once imitated by banks, utilities and FMCGs
- "Conversational packaging" / "tone of voice as the product" — Reed's own framing (The Marketing Society lecture 2007; *Any Idiot Can Do It* co-authored with Balon & Wright, 2009)
- "Chatty bottle" — Campaign UK shorthand for the 1999-2004 label run (Campaign 2006 profile of Innocent)
- "Write it the way you'd say it to your mate in the pub" — Reed, Guardian Small Business interview, 2008
- "The label is the ad" — Reed, Design Week 2004

## Prose mechanics
- **Sentence fragments as default unit**: one-line parenthetical asides stacked on the bottle side panel (`Hello. / We're Innocent. / This is a smoothie.`) — never compound, rarely longer than 12 words.
- **Hedged-ingredient joke**: product descriptor closes with a parenthetical "(and stuff)" / "(and nothing else, we promise)" / "(no concentrates, no funny business)" — functions as disclosure + wink in one clause; deploy once per label, never twice.
- **Reader-direct second person**: `you`, `your mum`, `your desk`, `you lot`; first-person plural `we` for the brand; NEVER `Innocent believes…` third person.
- **Live-contact CTA on every pack**: phone number labelled "the banana phone", with promise like `give us a call and we'll pick up (probably)` — the hedge is load-bearing; it signals a small team, not a call centre.
- **Stats-as-trivia under the cap / on the base**: non-commercial microcopy (`this bottle is 25% smaller than last year's`, `we flew 2 bananas to Devon this morning`) — the number must be specific, trivial, and verifiable.
- **Annual report written as children's story-book**: chapter headers like `chapter four: in which we buy a shed`; past-tense narration of the business year, hand-drawn doodles in place of pie-charts (Innocent Annual Report 2006, 2008).
- **No exclamation marks after line one**: excitement stays in the fragment rhythm, never the punctuation; em-dash and full stop do the work.
- **Apologise pre-emptively for the joke**: `sorry, we'll stop now` / `we got a bit carried away there` — the voice acknowledges its own wackiness one beat before the reader can.

## Examples
- "made of bananas (and stuff)" (Innocent banana smoothie 250ml label, c.2002)
- "stop looking at my bottom" (Innocent smoothie carton base, c.2003)
- "hello. we are innocent and we make 100% pure fruit smoothies. that's it. no funny business." (250ml bottle side panel, 1999 launch run)
- "enjoy by: see top of bottle. keep me in the fridge. shake me before you drink me. and if you don't like me, call the banana phone on 020 8600 3939." (Innocent smoothie label, c.2004)
- "this is the best thing we've ever written. probably." (Innocent Annual Report 2006, foreword)
- "dear innocent, i love your drinks but my dog ate the bottle. yours, a worried customer in Hove." (reprinted letter in the "Dear Innocent" pack-leaflet series, 2005)
- "we promise: 1. always tell the truth. 2. never, ever use concentrates. 3. leave things a little bit better than we find them." (Innocent "our promise" 3-line stamp, reused 2003-2009)
- "we had a chat. we think this one tastes like a hug from an aunt who's pleased to see you." (Innocent "thickies" yoghurt-smoothie range launch copy, 2007)

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM defaults into generic 2005-2015 CPG "wackaging" template — chirpy lowercase, random "(and stuff)" hedges, fake-handwritten fonts, ironic phone number — without the 1999-2004 live-phone-line discipline, without the specific verifiable trivia number, and without the fragment-rhythm short line-length. Result reads as Graze / Oatly-lite / PG Tips imitators rather than Innocent's actual 1999-2009 label corpus.
- **Mitigation**: "One hedged parenthetical per label max; every number verifiable; phone line must actually answer."
