### Absolut two-word bottle formula (EN | Q2 toward-Q1, Hayes+Turner, TBWA)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Compress the ad to a two-word joke — "Absolut" plus a second word carried (punned, subverted, geographically re-framed) by the visual of the bottle. Headline names the pun in the smallest possible syntactic unit; the reader's eye does the work the copy refuses to do. No body copy ever. Register is deadpan, cross-referential, art-literate — it trusts the reader to complete the joke.

**Native critical read**:
- "Visual-pun formula" — Richard W. Lewis, *Absolut Book: The Absolut Vodka Advertising Story* (Journey Editions, 1996), canonical vocabulary for the two-word bottle template
- "One of the most successful print campaigns in history" — Advertising Age; Ad Age "Top 100 Advertising Campaigns of the 20th Century" (1999) ranked Absolut top tier
- "Longest-running print-ad campaign in advertising history" — trade-press shorthand for the 1980-2000+ run (1,500+ ads)
- "Gallery-collectible" — VinePair (2020); only beverage ad series to sustain a collector market and museum exhibitions
- Adweek Hayes obituary (2015): "Absolut Perfection" set "the two-word, visual-pun template every subsequent ad answered to"

## Prose mechanics
- **Headline = exactly two words. First is always "Absolut." Second is a single noun or adjective — never a verb, never a phrase.** If a candidate needs a hyphen, article, or comma, it fails the formula.
- **Visual pun is carried by the bottle's silhouette or surface, never the words.** Halo = "Absolut Perfection." Gold frame = "Absolut Masterpiece." Skyline of bottle outlines = "Absolut Manhattan." If the pun needs copy to explain it, the ad is broken.
- **Zero body copy.** Government warnings and "80 and 100 Proof / Product of Sweden" are footer-set, not copy. A second sentence means the register is misunderstood.
- **Second word draws from four permitted pools, no others.** (a) abstract virtues the bottle can visually enact — Perfection, Clarity, Magnetism; (b) cities re-shaped into the bottle — Manhattan, Boston, Chicago, L.A.; (c) art-world names where a collaborator re-designs the bottle — Warhol, Haring, Scharf; (d) product-line puns — Citron, Kurant, Peppar. No mixing pools.
- **Cross-reference art by citation, not homage.** "Absolut Warhol" is not a Warhol-style ad; it is Warhol's own painting of the bottle, credited in display. Brand is the subject, not the imitator.
- **Register is deadpan.** No exclamation marks, no humor-signals in type. Futura-bold caps; cleverness comes from reader completion.
- **Geographic entries must pass the "bottle-shaped" test.** "Absolut Manhattan" — Central Park as bottle-shaped clearing from above. If the city cannot be wedded to the silhouette, it is not eligible — L.A. took years to solve.

## Examples
- "Absolut Perfection." (1980, Hayes AD / Turner CW — bottle with halo; founding template)
- "Absolut Masterpiece." (1981 — bottle as gallery canvas)
- "Absolut Manhattan." (1985 — aerial Central Park as bottle-shaped clearing; first geographic entry)
- "Absolut Warhol." (1986 — Warhol's commissioned painting of the bottle; first brand-artist collaboration per *Absolut Book*)
- "Absolut Boston." (late 1980s — bottle as baked-bean pot)
- "Absolut Chicago." (late 1980s — wind-bent bottle performing the city's nickname)
- "Absolut Citron." (1988 — flavor SKU launch, formula extended)
- "Absolut L.A." (early 1990s — swimming-pool shaped as bottle from above; solve documented in *Absolut Book*)
- "Absolut Haring." (1986 — Keith Haring's bottle painting)

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM defaults into three-word / tagline-plus-headline constructions ("Absolut. Pure Perfection." / "Absolutely Perfect.") or invents a second word that cannot be visually enacted ("Absolut Destiny," "Absolut Inspiration"). The trap is writing the headline without a bottle-visual in hand — the formula is CAPTION discipline, not slogan discipline. Secondary collapse: inserting body copy to "earn" the joke.
- **Mitigation**: "Two words. Second word must be visually enact-able on the bottle. No body copy."
