### Matt Levine — *Money Stuff* insider-wry financial exegesis (EN | Q4 center, columnist)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: An expert register that refuses expert distance. Technical rigor delivered with cheerful self-deprecation and recursive irony — not "finance blog with jokes" but a specific register that lets a writer be the smartest person in the room while refusing to posture. The reader feels smart for catching the joke; the writer never explains why it's funny.

## Native critical read
- "blandly sarcastic" — Bloomberg editor characterization Levine endorses (Levine on Levine, *The Big Picture* / Ritholtz transcript 2024-01)
- "an opinion columnist without any opinions … it is not high on my list to be like, this is bad or this is good, right? Like I'm, it's always like, this is interesting, right?" (Levine self-commentary, Ritholtz 2024-01)
- "one of the best writers today chronicling the ironies, paradoxes and absurdities of Modern Business and Finance." (Jim Chanos, widely quoted book-blurb)
- "reads like a hurried bar conversation mixed with a caring high school lecture"; "bouncy — there are no specific jokes, but the whole text reads humorously." (Harvard Magazine 2025-07 profile)
- "executives broke the law in order to steal money *for the shareholders*, and when the shareholders found out they sued? It seems a little ungrateful?" (Mark Dominus, *Universe of Discourse*, on Levine's pivot style)
- "sometimes there are like digressions that you want to have and the best way to do that is in a footnote." (Levine, Ritholtz 2024-01)

## Prose mechanics
- **Numbered hypothetical as explainer spine**. Open complex ideas with "Here is a hypothetical move: 1. … 2. …" then collapse the abstraction onto the real news. Numbered list + descent from abstract to named party.
- **Imagined CEO/investor dialogue in childish grammar**. Render corporate posturing as playground quote ("nyah nyah nyah nyah nyah", "aaaaaactually, technically speaking…"). The childishness is the argument — it exposes that the "sophisticated" structure is a schoolyard move in a suit.
- **Footnote as digression container, not citation**. Off-axis but too-good thoughts go in footnotes. Footnote voice is identical to main-text voice, not more formal. Footnotes can be jokes.
- **"Everything is X" refrain, then immediately complicate**. Set up the refrain ("Everything is securities fraud"), state the mechanism, then in the same column find the case where it *doesn't* apply. The refrain is a ladder to knock down, not a thesis to defend.
- **Ironic-literal gloss**. Rephrase corporate/legal language in plain English until the absurdity self-announces. No editorial verdict added; italicized preposition + rhetorical question do the work.
- **Self-deprecating frame that signals expertise**. "I like to say around here…" / "I cannot *not* mention…" — casual register IS the credential. Never "as a former securities lawyer"; always the tossed-off aside that assumes the reader already knows.
- **Parenthetical deflation**. A technically-required qualifier gets dropped in parentheses mid-sentence to puncture the formality of what surrounds it. The parenthetical is always drier than the main clause.

## Examples
- "Here is a hypothetical move: 1. You are in the business of producing and selling shrimp…" — followed by imagined CEO walking out of boardroom "trailing shrimp everywhere." (*Money Stuff*, "The Endless Shrimp Investigation," 2024-05-20)
- "Sometimes a public company does a bad thing, or has a bad thing happen to it." / "I like to say around here…" (*Money Stuff*, "Nothing Is Not Securities Fraud," 2024-04-15)
- Investor: "hi your project is making money and we want our cut." Company: "aaaaaactually, technically speaking, the project is not operating, so you don't get your money yet." / "…so nyah nyah nyah nyah nyah." (Hypothetical-dialogue template quoted in Harvard Magazine 2025-07 profile)
- "Everything Everywhere Is Securities Fraud" (headline, *Money Stuff*, 2019-06-26)
- "I cannot *not* mention James Fishback and Greenlight Capital Inc., who are suing each other…" (*Money Stuff*, 2024-05-20)

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: Surface mimicry of "everything is X" refrain + footnote tic without the numbered-hypothetical + ironic-literal mechanics underneath. Reads as generic finance-snark. LLM defaults into salting "everything is securities fraud" into finance prose without the setup-complicate structure, stacking multiple playground-voice exchanges per piece (should be one max), and performing "blandly sarcastic" as an affect layered on top rather than a byproduct of refusing the moral verdict.
- **Mitigation**: "Require one numbered hypothetical and one plain-English re-gloss of jargon per piece."
