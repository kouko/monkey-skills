### John Schoolcraft — Oatly Activist / Typewriter Register (EN | Q2 toward-Q3 + Q3 center)

## Voice direction
**What this register achieves**: Turn FMCG packaging into a reader's-digest-of-a-manifesto — small-print activism dressed as self-deprecating ramble — so the consumer feels they caught the brand being honest instead of being sold to. Antithesis does the heavy lifting ("like milk, but made for humans"); the rest of the carton disarms with typewriter-aesthetic confession that admits the copy exists to fill white space. Irreverence earns the right to scold the food industry on the next panel.

## Prose mechanics
- **Antithesis-as-tagline, once per piece max**: one sentence carries the whole thesis against the category. Template: "It's like X, but [unexpected qualifier that reframes X as absurd]." Never two in a row — second antithesis kills the first.
- **Typewriter register = monospace-compatible sentences**: run-on permitted but only when the run-on is the joke (the "empty space" confession). Avg sentence length bimodal: 4-8 words (slogan panel) or 40-70 words (confessional side panel). No 15-25 word middle — that sounds like a brand.
- **Self-deprecating meta-frame**: copy acknowledges itself as copy. "Before we wrote this sentence, there was a lot of empty space…" The reader is flattered for reading; the brand is embarrassed for writing. Deploy once per surface, never twice.
- **Small-print activism opposite a dumb joke**: the carton's "serious" panel (climate numbers, food-system critique) only earns its weight because the adjacent panel is silly. Never run two earnest panels back-to-back — the register collapses into NGO voice.
- **Second-person address in the CEO-letter / IFAQ form**: "Do I need a permit to carry oatmilk?" The Q is absurd; the A is flat deadpan. Absurdity lives in the question, not the answer.
- **Forbid adjective stacking**: "creamy, delicious, plant-based, sustainable" is forbidden. Pick zero or one. Oatly's register is noun-and-verb; adjectives signal capture by marketing.
- **Enemy-named activism, always a category not a competitor**: "Big Dairy", "the food industry", "the dairy lobby". Never a named brand — naming a brand sounds like attack ads; naming a category sounds like journalism.
- **Lowercase sentence-start inside headlines permitted; ALL CAPS reserved for one-line antithesis**: "WOW NO COW" / "IT'S LIKE MILK, BUT MADE FOR HUMANS" are the only caps moves. Everywhere else: sentence case, typewriter-plain.
- **"So?" / "I guess" / "really" as register-tells**: ending a confession panel on an undercutting particle ("…I guess.") is the Schoolcraft signature. Don't overdo — once per panel.
- **Profanity is a quarterly-report lever, not a garnish**: "f**k milk" / "fucking fearless" works because the rest of the body is restrained. If every third sentence swears, the register becomes Liquid Death (adjacent Q3-extreme, different anchor).
- **Never use "journey", "mission", "community", "movement"**: Schoolcraft's rule-in-practice. These words are where food-industry-adjacent brands cargo-cult his activism and lose the register.

## Don't / Over-mimic
- **Failure mode**: LLM produces Oatly-shaped slop — lowercase typewriter voice + run-on side-panel + one forced antithesis + "Hey [Industry], show us your [thing]" wheat-paste cargo-cult. The widely-imitated 2018-2023 DTC register (Liquid Death, Ugly Drinks, Olipop, Recess, Magic Spoon adjacent) borrows the form without the antithesis-earning-the-scold discipline. Secondary failure: activism-without-silliness (pure NGO voice) or silliness-without-activism (pure quirky-brand voice) — Schoolcraft's register requires BOTH on adjacent panels, never in the same sentence.
- **Mitigation** (15 words): "Pair one earnest-activist panel with one self-deprecating panel; never merge. No adjective stacks."
