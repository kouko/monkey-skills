# Mid-Form Copywriting — BEAF Canon

Tier 2 standard: defines the **BEAF (Benefit - Evidence - Advantage -
Feature)** framework as canon for mid-form copy — product pages,
in-store POP, and presentation materials in the range of tens to
hundreds of characters. BEAF is a widely adopted ordering convention in
Japanese EC practice (楽天市場 / Amazon JP). No identifiable canonical
author is confirmed from primary sources, so it is positioned as an
**"industry-standard EC practice framework."**

## Primary Sources

- **宣伝会議 AdverTimes (2022)** *BEAFの法則とは — 商品ページで使える
  コピーライティング術*. https://www.advertimes.com/ (site search:
  "BEAFの法則"). Formalizes the 4-stage definition and Benefit-first
  ordering.
- **ferret-plus (2020)** *BEAFの法則 — 商品ページ制作で覚えておきたい
  文章構成*. https://ferret-plus.com/ (site search: "BEAF"). Provides 4-stage
  nomenclature and application examples for 楽天 / Amazon product pages.
- **innovation.co.jp urumo blog** *セールスコピー BEAF の法則*.
  https://www.innovation.co.jp/urumo/ (site search: "BEAF"). Dual-verify
  source for B→E→A→F ordering.
- **楽天市場出店者向け教学資料 / Amazon JP セラーセントラル ヘルプ** —
  official documentation recommending Benefit-first ordering for product-
  page composition. https://seller.rakuten.co.jp/ /
  https://sellercentral.amazon.co.jp/
- **Author attribution uncertainty**: no primary source identifying
  BEAF's canonical author (who coined and systematized it) has been
  found in the secondary-source cluster. Treated as an established
  JP EC industry practice framework, labeled as **"industry-standard
  framework."** Canonical textbook is unconfirmed.
- **Relationship to FAB (Feature-Advantage-Benefit)**: FAB is a classic
  3-stage model from US sales training (Xerox, etc.), ordered Feature →
  Advantage → Benefit. BEAF is the **reverse direction + Evidence
  insertion** as a 4-stage model, understood as the JP EC adaptation
  of FAB.

## Framing — positioning of the mid-form layer

BEAF targets **tens to hundreds of characters** as a mid-form
framework, differentiated from PASONA-family (long form) and
キャッチコピー (short form) by two characteristics:

- **Functional persuasion**: while PASONA-family guides via a
  "empathy → urgency → action" psychological flow, BEAF follows
  a "what this product brings you → evidence → competitive advantage
  → specs" objective-persuasion flow. The axis is on functional
  explanation, not psychological guidance.
- **Benefit-first ordering**: on product pages where readers scan,
  the opening must present "what good change happens for me" — or the
  reader scrolls past. Order violation (Feature-first) invites
  drop-off.

## BEAF's 4-stage definitions

| Stage | Name | Stage mission | Typical phrasing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B** | **Benefit (利益)** | Depict how the reader's life improves — the concrete post-purchase change — in the very first sentence. Show the "achieved state" that buying the product enables, and stop the reader's eye. | "You can become ~", "Free yourself from ~" |
| **E** | **Evidence (証拠)** | Objective data backing Benefit: awards, third-party reviews, customer testimonials, test results. Preemptively addresses doubt about Benefit. | "Winner of 〇〇 award", "Review score 4.8/5.0", "Clinically proven to ~" |
| **A** | **Advantage (優位)** | Unique strengths compared to competitors. Not just "any product in this category" but "why this one." Feature-comparison tables and proprietary technology introductions. | "2× the industry average in ~", "Only product in the category with ~" |
| **F** | **Feature (特徴)** | Objective baseline information: specs, ingredients, size, color, materials, included items. Serves as the specification list. | "Capacity 500ml / Ingredients: ~ / Weight: ~g" |

**Canonical ordering rationale**: Benefit → Evidence → Advantage →
Feature beats the reverse order (F→A→E→B) for three reasons:

- **Optimized for scan reading**: product pages are skimmed. Without
  Benefit at the top, scrolling does not pause.
- **Interest → conviction → differentiation → confirmation cognitive
  flow**: readers' questions follow "attractive? → really? → how is it
  different? → what exactly is it?" — BEAF matches this sequence.
- **Psychological weight distribution of purchase decisions**: Feature
  is post-decision confirmation material with weak pre-decision
  persuasion. Benefit drives the decision.

## Importance of Benefit-first ordering

BEAF's foremost canonical claim is the **"give the sugar first, then
give the proof"** ordering. Product pages that lead with Feature fail
on two counts:

- **Readers do not self-translate Feature into Benefit.** "500ml" does
  not spontaneously evoke "covers a full day's hydration in your commute
  bag." The writer must pre-translate Feature → Benefit.
- **A Feature-only product page becomes an "ad that doesn't seem to be
  selling."** A spec listing feels neutral and "not trying to sell,"
  and psychologically fails to generate purchase motivation.

## Character-count range and use cases

**Character count**: tens of characters (in-store POP) to hundreds
(product-page body text). Beyond 1,000 characters, consider 新 PASONA /
PASBECONA as the boundary.

**Typical use cases**:

- 楽天市場 / Yahoo!ショッピング / Amazon JP product-page body text
- In-store POP (supermarkets, electronics retailers, bookstores)
- Presentation / exhibition booth handout materials
- B2B product catalogs — one page per product
- EC site product-listing-page summary text

## Demarcation from PASONA-family

BEAF and PASONA-family differ **at the root of their persuasion
approach**:

| Axis | BEAF | PASONA-family |
|---|---|---|
| Persuasion type | Functional persuasion (objective) | Psychological guidance (subjective) |
| Starting point | Benefit (outcome picture) | Problem (problem awareness) |
| Urgency | Not used | Explicitly stated via Narrow down |
| Empathy | Not used | Affinity (新/PASBECONA) |
| Action | Explicit CTA not needed (purchase button adjacency is natural) | Explicit stage required |
| Character range | Tens to hundreds | 1,500–20,000+ |
| Media | Product page / POP | LP / newsletter / sales letter |

**Selection criteria**:

- Product page / POP / catalog single-product description → BEAF
- LP / newsletter / sales letter → PASONA-family
- Product page that wants to tell "an additional story" → hybrid
  composition with BEAF as the main axis and Problem + Affinity
  subsections inserted

## Differences from FAB (a common industry confusion)

**FAB (Feature - Advantage - Benefit)** is a classic 3-stage model
from US sales training, frequently confused with BEAF. The precise
differences:

| Axis | FAB | BEAF |
|---|---|---|
| Stages | 3 | 4 |
| Order | Feature → Advantage → Benefit | Benefit → Evidence → Advantage → Feature |
| Evidence | None | Present (stage 2) |
| Origin | US sales training (Xerox, etc.) | JP EC practice |
| Starting-point philosophy | Product-centric (explain from Feature) | Customer-centric (depict from Benefit) |

**Canonical caution**: secondary sources that explain BEAF in "FAB
order" are incorrect and must not be adopted. BEAF is **FAB reversed
+ Evidence added** — the two must not be swapped in memory.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Starting from Feature**. Canonical ordering violation of BEAF.
  Spec listing at the top means the reader does not pause scrolling.
- **Omitting the Evidence stage**. Benefit alone looks like "ad
  exaggeration." Objective backing (awards, reviews, data) is essential.
- **Implementing BEAF in FAB order**. F → A → B and F → A → E → B are
  different things. Swapping Evidence and Benefit positions is a
  canonical violation.
- **Filling Advantage with subjective adjectives**. "High quality" and
  "the best" are not Advantage; concrete comparative figures / technology
  / certifications against competitors are Advantage.
- **Writing Benefit as a Feature paraphrase**. "It's convenient because
  it holds 500ml" is merely Feature translation. "Covers a full day's
  hydration in your commute bag" — that level of concreteness is
  Benefit.
- **Forcibly adding a CTA stage to BEAF**. BEAF itself has no Action
  stage; the product page's purchase button serves as Action. Forcibly
  appending a CTA stage blurs the boundary with PASONA.
