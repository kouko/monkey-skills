# D3 scoreboard — Did You Get It Right? (book self-check)

> Source: McChesney/Covey/Huling/Thele/Walker (2021), *The 4 Disciplines
> of Execution* 2nd ed., Simon & Schuster, p 251.

The official FranklinCovey 8-item self-check from Chapter 14 ("Applying
Discipline 3 — Did You Get It Right?"). Every freshly-built or
freshly-revised scoreboard SHOULD pass all eight before being treated as
done. Verbatim wording preserved.

## The 8 questions (verbatim, team scope)

- [ ] 1. Has the team been closely involved in creating the scoreboard?
- [ ] 2. Does the scoreboard track the Team WIG, lag measures, and lead measures?
- [ ] 3. Is there a full explanation of the WIG and measures along with the graphs?
- [ ] 4. Does every graph display both actual results and the target results (Where are we now? Where should we be?)?
- [ ] 5. Can the team tell at a glance on every measure if they're winning or losing?
- [ ] 6. Is the scoreboard posted in a highly visible location where the team can see it easily and often?
- [ ] 7. Is the scoreboard easy to update?
- [ ] 8. Is the scoreboard personalized — a unique expression of the team?

## Personal-scope lens (solo adaptation)

For solo / personal scope, the same 8 questions apply with two
substitutions — "team" → "you" and "Team WIG" → "WIG". The book
questions above remain the canonical wording; the personal-scope
reading below is a lens on the same checklist, not a rewrite.

- 1 → Have **you** been closely involved in creating the scoreboard? (i.e. it is yours, not borrowed / templated wholesale)
- 2 → Does the scoreboard track the **WIG**, lag measure, and lead measures?
- 3 → Is there a full explanation of the WIG and measures along with the graphs?
- 4 → Does every graph display both actual results and the target results (Where are you now? Where should you be?)?
- 5 → Can **you** tell at a glance on every measure if you're winning or losing?
- 6 → Is the scoreboard posted in a highly visible location where **you** can see it easily and often?
- 7 → Is the scoreboard easy to update?
- 8 → Is the scoreboard personalized — a unique expression of **your** practice / role / context?

## Use

- Coach-mode (`personal-design.md`, `team-lead-design.md`) — run after
  the draft scoreboard is sketched; any `[ ]` left unchecked is a
  return-to-bench signal before declaring D3 done.
- Audit-mode (`audit-mode.md`) — run as the final canonical
  verification gate after the per-element disposition + minimum-viable
  redesign, before output.
- Member-mode (`member-read.md`) — informational only; member doesn't
  own redesign. Failed items become escalation talking-points, not
  self-fixes.
