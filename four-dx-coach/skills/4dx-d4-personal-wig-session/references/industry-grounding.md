# Industry-experience grounding for 4dx-d4-personal-wig-session

Supplements the book's WIG Session prescription with the broader meeting-science
and product-flow literature on **why** weekly cadence works (and when it
backfires). The book asserts the cadence is sacred; these sources give the
mechanism, dosage, and the cadence-vs-flow tradeoff that the book elides —
particularly important when adapting a team-meeting ritual to a solo user.

## Meeting science — why short, agenda-disciplined, weekly meetings outperform

- **Steven G. Rogelberg, *The Surprising Science of Meetings: How You Can Lead
  Your Team to Peak Performance* (Oxford University Press, 2019, ISBN
  978-0-19-068921-6, 180 pp.)** — synthesizes ~15 years of empirical
  meeting-science research (field surveys, lab studies, planted-confederate
  experiments) on >5,000 employees. Three findings bear directly on the WIG
  Session: (1) shorter time-boxes raise per-minute effectiveness — 25-min
  default beats 60-min default by Parkinson dynamics; (2) agenda discipline
  (a single explicit purpose per meeting) is the single largest predictor of
  perceived effectiveness; (3) "meeting recovery syndrome" — most workers
  need 30-60 min to re-enter focused work after a meeting — argues for
  *fewer, shorter, agenda-tight* meetings, exactly the WIG Session shape.
  Won the Humboldt Prize for meeting-science research.
  ([Oxford University Press product page](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-surprising-science-of-meetings-9780190689216),
  [Personnel Psychology review (Wiley, 2021)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/peps.12475))

- **Patrick Lencioni, *Death by Meeting: A Leadership Fable About Solving the
  Most Painful Problem in Business* (Jossey-Bass, 2004, ISBN
  0-7879-6805-6)** — names the four-meeting taxonomy that the WIG Session
  occupies a slot in: **Daily Check-In** (~5 min), **Weekly Tactical**
  (45-90 min), **Monthly Strategic** (2-4 hr), **Quarterly Off-Site Review**.
  The WIG Session is structurally a Weekly Tactical with a stricter
  single-WIG focus and shorter 20-30 min budget. Lencioni's three-part
  Weekly Tactical structure (lightning round → progress review →
  real-time agenda) maps almost cleanly onto 4DX Account → Review → Plan.
  This grounds the WIG Session in a recognized meeting taxonomy rather than
  treating it as a 4DX invention.
  ([Lencioni Weekly Tactical Guide (O'Reilly)](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/death-by-meeting/9780787968052/pr10.html),
  [Schilder publishing PDF excerpt](https://www.donnaschilder.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Four-Meetings_Patrick-Lencioni.pdf))

## Cadence-vs-flow tradeoff (Reinertsen) — the solo-user adaptation gap

- **Donald G. Reinertsen, *The Principles of Product Development Flow: Second
  Generation Lean Product Development* (Celeritas Publishing, 2009, ISBN
  978-1-935401-00-1)** — Principle F6 (**Cadence Principle**): "Cadence is
  the use of a regular predictable rhythm within a process." Reinertsen
  shows mathematically that *fixed cadence transforms unpredictable events
  into predictable ones* and *prevents variability from accumulating in
  sequential processes* — the deep mechanism the book asserts but doesn't
  explain. But Reinertsen also flags the tradeoff the book ignores: cadence
  is overhead-positive only when batch size and feedback latency are
  reasonably matched. For domains with very long lag-measure feedback loops
  (multi-month R&D, drug discovery, novel writing) the weekly cadence
  becomes theatrical — Reinertsen's framework would predict this exact
  failure mode, which the book treats as an edge case rather than a
  structural prediction.
  ([Chapter 1 PDF (lpd2.com)](http://lpd2.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/ReinertsenFLOWChap1.pdf))

## What 4DX practitioners (especially solo users) learn from these sources

The book asserts the WIG Session is sacred and does so on practitioner
authority. Rogelberg gives the *empirical* warrant: short + agenda-tight +
weekly is the meeting shape that survives meeting-science scrutiny. Lencioni
gives the *taxonomic* warrant: the WIG Session is a Weekly Tactical with one
extra constraint (single WIG focus), not a novel ritual; this lets solo users
borrow Lencioni's three-part structural template and Rogelberg's
time-boxing dosage with confidence. Reinertsen gives the *mechanistic*
warrant — fixed cadence dampens variability — and crucially the *limits*:
when the lag's natural feedback period is much longer than a week, the
ritual decouples from its mechanism and reverts to ceremony. For solo users
this is the most important safeguard the book under-serves: a writer with a
12-month novel WIG running weekly sessions on lead-measure compliance is
probably running theatre, not 4DX. The Cystic Fibrosis Center pattern
(commit to *experiments*, not outcomes) is the documented workaround, but
the Reinertsen framing makes the workaround principled rather than
ad-hoc — adapt the question from "did the lead move" to "did you run the
experiment", and lengthen cadence if even the experiment-cycle is
sub-weekly impossible.
