# 4DX Discipline 2 — Team Lead Measure Facilitation (Team-Leader Scope)

## R — Reading

> "The lead measures with the most impact come from a collaboration between
> the leader and the frontline team. ... The leader can veto an idea they
> believe will not work, but they cannot dictate what the final lead
> measures should be."
>
> — McChesney et al., Chapter 3

---

## I — Interpretation

A team-leader who picks lead measures alone gets compliance, not commitment.
A team-leader who fully delegates loses strategic alignment with the Team
WIG. The book's answer is a structured collaboration: leader provides the
WIG and holds the line on quality; team provides the leverage points
because they know the operation.

The facilitation has four moving parts the leader must hold simultaneously:

1. **WIG as anchor.** The Team WIG must already be defined as
   *From X to Y by When*. Leads built on a vague WIG are noise; halt and
   redirect upstream if the WIG is not yet crisp.
2. **Brainstorm wide, then narrow ruthlessly.** More candidates → higher
   quality. The book's caution: "the more you try to do, the less energy
   you have to give to any one thing." Cap at 2-3 leads; 2 is ideal, 3 is
   the maximum.
3. **Two-axis test as the gate.** Every surviving candidate must be both
   *predictive* of the Team WIG AND *influenceable* by THIS team without
   significant dependence on another team. Failing either axis disqualifies
   completely — not "interesting but…", just out.
4. **Veto, don't dictate (Rule 3, Ch 7).** When the team proposes a bad
   lead, the leader rejects it ("this won't move the WIG because…") — but
   does NOT author the replacement. Silence is the leader's tool. The team
   must produce the next candidate.

Two structural confusions to surface loudly. First, **a battle is not a
lead measure** (Ch 7 explicit caution): sub-goals like "improve arrival
experience" are lag measures on a smaller scale, not lead measures. Second,
**a task is not a measure**: "complete the cache rewrite" is one-shot;
"% of requests served from cache, weekly" is a measure that drives
behavior across the whole quarter.

Knowledge-work caveat: most book exemplars are operational (hotel valet,
shoe retail, surgical tray audits). For knowledge-work teams, the two-axis
test still applies but candidate-discovery may need the Five Optimizing
Questions and longer search; resist the temptation to copy retail patterns.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: Opryland Hotel — 75 functionally-diverse teams under 3 Key Battles
- **Problem**: Primary WIG of guest satisfaction 42→55 (top-box). After
  Key Battles were chosen, each of 75 frontline teams had to discover
  their own lead measures under a Team WIG (e.g. bellstand: luggage
  delivery 106 min → 12 min).
- **Methodology applied**: Each team-leader facilitated brainstorm + two-
  axis filter with their team. Bellstand team chose lead measures around
  delivery routing and staging — operational, influenceable by every
  bellman, predictive of luggage time. Leaders veto'd lag-masquerading-as-
  lead candidates (e.g. "guest satisfaction score for our area" — that's
  the lag).
- **Conclusion**: Leader-team collaboration produced leads no top-down
  process would have surfaced — operational specificity came from the
  frontline.
- **Outcome**: Top-box satisfaction beat target — 42 → 61 in nine months
  across all 75 teams.

### Case 2: Surgical team — verbal confirmation 17% lift
- **Problem**: Existing lead "audit surgical tray before every surgery"
  was already in place but plateaued. The clinical-lead facilitated
  refinement with the team rather than imposing a new lead.
- **Methodology applied**: Team brainstormed quality-axis enhancements;
  proposed "audit + complete a verbal confirmation of every procedure"
  — small qualitative tweak, team-authored, leader veto'd vaguer
  proposals like "communicate better in the OR" (failed influenceability
  + measurability).
- **Conclusion**: Quality-based lead added on top of the existing
  frequency-based lead; both passed the two-axis test.
- **Outcome**: Perioperative-incident reduction lifted by 17%
  attributable to the verbal-confirmation lead.

### Case 3: Front-desk team — 7-foot eye-contact rule, 38% lift
- **Problem**: Front-desk lead "warmly welcome every guest" was vague
  (failing measurability), producing inconsistent guest experience.
- **Methodology applied**: Team-leader facilitated team's redefinition;
  team proposed "make eye contact and greet within 7 feet of arriving
  guest" — specific, influenceable by every clerk, predictive of
  satisfaction. Leader veto'd softer rewordings ("be friendly").
- **Conclusion**: Specific behavioral lead replaced vague aspiration.
- **Outcome**: 38% lift on the relevant satisfaction sub-measure.

## A2 — Future Trigger ★

### When will the user need this skill?

1. Team-leader has a defined Team WIG (in From X to Y by When form) and
   needs to run a working session with their team to choose 2-3 lead
   measures — and is unsure how to facilitate without dictating.
2. Team-leader is about to pre-write the lead measures themselves "to
   save the team time" — needs to be redirected to facilitation, not
   imposition.
3. Team-leader's first attempt produced 6+ candidate leads; the team
   couldn't narrow because everything seemed equally important.
4. Team has proposed a candidate lead that the leader senses is wrong
   (lag-masquerading-as-lead, gated by another team, vanity metric like
   "more meetings") — leader needs the veto-don't-dictate language.
5. Team is split: half says "we control X", other half says "no, X is
   gated by team B" — leader needs to mediate the influenceability
   debate without dictating.
6. Team proposed "complete the cache rewrite by Q4" as a lead — the
   leader needs to surface that this is a TASK (one-shot deliverable),
   not a MEASURE (sustained leveraged behavior or small outcome).

### Language signals (user phrasings that should activate)

- "How do I run a lead-measure session with my team without just telling
  them the answer?"
- "My team brainstormed 12 candidate leads — how do I help them narrow
  to 2-3?"
- "One of my engineers proposed [X] but I think it's gated by another
  team — how do I push back without shutting them down?"
- "Should I prepare draft lead measures before the team meeting?"
- "My team can't agree on what we can actually influence — they're
  arguing about scope."
- 「team で lead measure を一緒に決めたい。どう facilitate すればいい？」
- 「メンバーが提案した先行指標が、本当に効くか自信ない。どう veto する？」
- 「6 個候補が出たが、2-3 に絞れない。どう narrow する？」
- 「先行指標とサブゴール（小目標）の違いを、team にどう説明する？」
- 「帶 team 一起找 lead measure，但我不想直接給答案，怎麼引導？」
- 「成員提的指標感覺不對，但我又不想 dictate，怎麼 veto？」
- 「team 在吵某個指標到底我們控不控得到，怎麼仲裁？」

### Distinction from neighboring skills

- vs. `4dx-d2-personal-lead-measure-discovery`: that skill coaches a SOLO
  user discovering leads for their own personal WIG. This skill coaches
  a team-LEADER facilitating a TEAM's discovery — adds team-dynamics
  layer (buy-in, member disagreement, vanity-lead policing,
  veto-not-dictate language) and forbids the leader from authoring the
  leads themselves. If the user is solo, redirect there.
- vs. `4dx-d1-member-team-wig-comprehension`: member-side skill for a
  team member who has been HANDED leads and needs to derive their slice.
  This skill is for the leader who is FACILITATING the team's selection
  in the first place. If the user is a member describing inherited
  leads, redirect there.
- vs. `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` / `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection`:
  those skills cover Team WIG selection (the lag). This skill assumes
  the Team WIG already exists and covers ONLY the lead-measure
  discovery beneath it. If the user can't yet state their Team WIG in
  one sentence, redirect to the WIG cascade skill first — leads built
  on a vague WIG are noise.
- vs. `4dx-d4-team-wig-session-lead`: D4 is the weekly review cadence
  AFTER leads are chosen. This skill is the one-time discovery that
  picks them.
- vs. `4dx-meta-team-xps-evaluation`: XPS audits whether existing leads
  are being executed (cadence, fulfillment, lead performance, lag). This
  skill chooses the leads in the first place.

## E — Execution

When this skill activates, the agent coaches the team-leader through a
facilitation protocol — never authoring leads on the team's behalf, and
explicitly preventing the LEADER from doing so either.

1. **Team WIG gate check**
   - Ask: "State your Team WIG in one sentence — From X to Y by When."
   - Completion criterion: leader produces a single sentence with current
     state, target state, deadline.
   - Halt condition: if the leader can't, STOP and redirect to
     `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` (Team WIG cascade from a Primary WIG) or
     `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection` (single-team Primary WIG).
     Do NOT proceed.

2. **Leader-stance briefing (anti-dictate inoculation)**
   - Brief the leader on the four facilitation rules BEFORE the session:
     (a) you do not pre-write the leads; (b) you can veto, you cannot
     dictate; (c) silence is your tool — when team is stuck, do NOT fill
     it with your own answer; (d) cap at 2-3 leads, with 2 as ideal.
   - Ask: "Which of these four will be hardest for you?" — surfaces the
     leader's specific failure-mode (most common: filling silence).
   - Completion criterion: leader names their personal-risk rule and
     commits to a specific behavior to counter it (e.g. "when team is
     silent for 60s I will ask 'what's a non-obvious option?' rather
     than offer one").

3. **Facilitate brainstorm — wide, no filtering (5-10 candidates)**
   - Coach the leader to ask the team: "What daily or weekly behaviors,
     if you did them consistently, would drive [Team WIG]? Don't filter
     — list as many as you can."
   - Push for counter-intuitive options: "What's something nobody else
     would think of? What's the equivalent of 'measure children's feet'
     for our work?"
   - Halt condition: if a team member proposes a one-shot task (e.g.
     "ship the cache PR"), the leader names it: "that's a deliverable,
     not a measure — what's the underlying behavior we'd track every
     week?" Re-add as a measure or drop.
   - Halt condition: if a team member proposes a vanity lead ("more
     meetings", "send more emails") — the leader veto's with reason:
     "what's the causal chain to the WIG?" If no chain, drop.
   - Completion criterion: ≥5 candidate behaviors on the board, with
     the team (not the leader) authoring each.

4. **Two-axis test — applied as a team**
   - For each candidate, the team scores together:
     - **Predictive (1-5)**: "If we did this consistently for 6 weeks,
       how confident are we the WIG moves? State the causal chain in
       one sentence."
     - **Influenceable (1-5)**: "Can THIS team move this measure THIS
       week WITHOUT permission from another team?"
   - Halt condition (mediation): if the team disagrees on
     influenceability ("we own X" vs "no, gated by team B"), the leader
     mediates by asking "what fraction of X do we directly control?
     Score it as that fraction × 5." If <60% controllable, the lead
     needs to be re-scoped to the controllable portion or dropped.
   - Completion criterion: every candidate has both scores + a
     one-sentence causal chain for predictive.

5. **Drop anything <4 on either axis (leader vetoes; doesn't replace)**
   - Anything ≤3 on predictive: noise. Drop.
   - Anything ≤3 on influenceable: rainfall-class. Drop.
   - When dropping, the leader names WHY ("this fails predictive — no
     causal chain to WIG") but does NOT propose a replacement.
   - Completion criterion: only 4-5★ candidates remain on both axes.

6. **Narrow to 2-3, prefer one frequency (small outcome) + one quality (leveraged behavior)**
   - Coach the leader to ask the team: "Pick 2-3 — at most. Ideally one
     small outcome (a weekly result with method-latitude) and one
     leveraged behavior (a specific action performed consistently)."
   - Halt condition: if the team picks >3, the leader forces a cut: "more
     than three recreates the whirlwind — which one drops?" The TEAM
     decides which drops.
   - Completion criterion: 2-3 named lead measures, team-authored.

7. **Operational definition (team writes; leader proofreads)**
   - For each selected lead, the team writes:
     - What exactly counts as "done" (specific enough that any team
       member can apply without asking).
     - How and when it gets logged (and who logs it).
     - Weekly target number.
   - Leader's role: proofread for vagueness. If a definition is vague,
     leader veto's it ("this isn't specific enough — another team
     member couldn't apply it") and the team revises. Leader does NOT
     write the revision.
   - Completion criterion: each lead has a definition any team member
     could apply unaided.

8. **Causal-chain commitment (team writes one paragraph)**
   - Team writes (collaboratively): "If we hit our lead targets every
     week for 6 weeks, here's why we expect [Team WIG] to have moved
     by [Y] …"
   - This is a falsifiable forecast — anchor for D4 weekly review.
   - Completion criterion: written paragraph linking lead activity →
     WIG movement with explicit mechanism, signed off by the team
     (not just the leader).

9. **Output**
   - 2-3 named lead measures, each with team-authored operational
     definition + weekly target + team-authored causal-chain rationale.
   - Hand off to `4dx-d3-personal-scoreboard` (or future
     `4dx-d3-team-scoreboard`) for visual artifact, and to
     `4dx-d4-team-wig-session-lead` for weekly review cadence.

## B — Boundary ★

### Do NOT use this skill in:

- **No defined Team WIG yet** — leads need a lag to be predictive of.
  Redirect to `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` /
  `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection`.
- **Solo personal-WIG context** — the user is alone, no team. Redirect
  to `4dx-d2-personal-lead-measure-discovery`. Member-side leads (user
  is a team-member with a WIG handed down) → redirect to
  `4dx-d1-member-team-wig-comprehension`.
- **Multi-team / matrix context** — when the user actually leads several
  teams that need cascading separate lead-measure sessions, this is
  out of scope; treat each team as one application of this skill, and
  use `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` to handle the cross-team alignment.
- **Stroke-of-pen WIGs** — if the Team WIG is achieved by a single
  decision (sign the contract, reorg, hire) there's no behavioral
  pattern to discover. Lead measures apply only to behavioral-change
  WIGs.
- **Knowledge-work with months-long feedback loops** (genuine R&D,
  drug discovery, regulatory cycles) — weekly leads may become
  theatrical. Apply the two-axis test rigorously and shorten the loop
  by one decomposition layer, or honestly admit the lead-to-lag chain
  doesn't fit a weekly cadence.

### Author-warned failure modes (from cluster CEs)

- **Lag-masquerading-as-lead at team scale (CE-01 + Ch 7 caution)** —
  the team proposes a sub-WIG ("improve arrival experience") as a lead.
  Sub-WIGs are lag measures on a smaller scale. Veto with: "that's a
  lag — what's the daily behavior that drives it?"
- **Predictive but not influenceable, gated by another team (CE-02 +
  CE-16)** — the team proposes "decrease infra outage rate" but
  outage rate is mostly driven by another team's pager. Veto and
  re-scope to the controllable portion ("our service's error budget
  consumption", "PRs without rollback").
- **Lead-data-too-hard-so-skipped (CE-17)** — team gives up on tracking
  a high-leverage lead because data collection is awkward. Author
  warning ("pay the price, P-11"): if you skip tracking, you've
  abandoned D2. The leader must hold this line — the lead's awkwardness
  is not grounds to drop it; only failing the two-axis test is.
- **Compliance without commitment (CE-15)** — team accepts whatever
  the leader implies; outwardly compliant, internally not bought in.
  The 4-week tell: leads green, lag flat, team affect flat. If observed,
  re-run this skill — the original session was leader-dictated even if
  it didn't feel that way.
- **Leader hijacks the team's work (CE-23, "Marcus pattern")** — under
  pressure, the leader starts personally executing the lead measure
  ("I'll just do the cold calls myself this week") rather than
  coaching the team. This is a D4 failure mode but its root cause is
  often a D2 facilitation that didn't actually transfer ownership.
- **Lead performed but not at sufficient quality (CE-21)** — frequency
  count met but quality hollow ("we made 50 cold calls" — but didn't
  follow the script). Pairing one frequency-based + one quality-based
  lead (Step 6) exists precisely to catch this; if the team picks two
  frequency-based, the leader vetoes the second and asks for a quality
  pair.
- **Goodhart / measure-gaming (CE-24, book gap)** — at team scale,
  gaming becomes social as well as individual; teams collectively
  rationalize gamed numbers. Defend by re-checking the causal chain
  every 4 weeks: if leads are green but lag is flat, the lead is being
  gamed or was wrong. Industry-experience addendum below.

### Author's blind spots / period limitations

- **Operational-work bias** — author's exemplars are heavily operational
  (hotel, retail, surgery, plant safety). Knowledge-work teams (data
  platform, research, design, content) need the two-axis test applied
  more carefully — the influenceability axis tends to be fuzzier
  (work depends on cross-team review, etc.) and the predictive axis
  has longer feedback loops. Resist copying the retail pattern; let
  the team derive their own.
- **High-context-culture peer dynamics** — 4DX assumes a low-context,
  high-direct-feedback culture where peer veto is socially safe. In
  high-context settings (JP/ZH/KR enterprise), public veto can read as
  face-loss. The leader may need to run pre-session 1:1s to surface
  candidates privately before the group narrowing, then merge.
- **Authority-assumption** — assumes the leader has clear veto authority
  on the WIG side. Weaker in flat / partnership / holacracy settings;
  in those, the "veto" must be reframed as a quality gate the team
  collectively enforces, not a leader prerogative.

### Easily-confused neighboring concepts

- **Battles vs lead measures (Ch 7 explicit)** — a battle is a sub-WIG
  (lag); a lead measure is a daily behavior that drives the Team WIG.
  Surface this loudly: most first-time lead-measure sessions confuse
  these two.
- **Tasks vs lead measures** — "ship the cache PR by Friday" is a task
  (one-shot deliverable). "% of requests served from cache, weekly" is
  a lead measure (sustained). Tasks live in commitments (D4); leads
  live in this skill.
- **OKR Key Results** — KRs in OKR-trained teams are often a mix of
  lag and lead. If team has KRs already, ask which are lag (results)
  and which are lead (driving behaviors). Run this skill against the
  WIG, not the KR list.

### Member-misfire patterns (re-anchor to leader scope)

- **Member as facilitator** — if the user is a team-MEMBER who's been
  asked to "drive the lead-measure session" because the leader
  delegated, this skill still applies but with reduced veto authority.
  Re-frame: "you're hosting; you can name when a candidate fails the
  two-axis test, but the actual veto-on-strategic-fit belongs to your
  leader. Bring 2-3 candidates that all pass; let the leader weigh in
  before lock-in." If the leader has fully abdicated, escalate
  privately first.
- **Member experiencing inherited leads** — if the user is a team
  member trying to make sense of leads chosen without them, redirect
  to `4dx-d1-member-team-wig-comprehension`.
- **Single-team leader vs leader-of-leaders** — this skill is for a
  team-leader whose team is the frontline. Leaders of multiple teams
  (leaders of leaders) should NOT have lead measures at their level
  (P-10) — they have XPS instead. Redirect to
  `4dx-meta-team-xps-evaluation` if the user manages other leaders.

### Industry-experience addendum

The book treats team buy-in as the natural output of running the
methodology. Industry research adds three preconditions
(citations: `references/industry-grounding.md`):

- **Psychological safety** (Edmondson 1999/2012) — without it, teams
  give the leader the answer they think the leader wants, producing
  CE-15 compliance. Diagnostic: in Step 3, did >1 junior member
  volunteer a counter-intuitive candidate? If only senior voices
  spoke, safety is the bottleneck.
- **Trust → conflict → commitment chain** (Lencioni 2002) —
  veto-don't-dictate debate requires Stage 2 (productive conflict).
  Below it, Step 4 mediation collapses into silence ("artificial
  harmony") or personalized argument.
- **Evidence-based management** (Pfeffer & Sutton 2006) —
  copy-pasted leads ("Stripe does X") inherit case-study
  survivorship bias compounded by FranklinCovey selection bias.
  Always two-axis-test against THIS team's WIG.

Cross-ref for Goodhart/gaming (Wells Fargo / Phoenix VA / Atlanta
Public Schools): see `4dx-d2-personal-lead-measure-discovery` — cases
generalize to team scale; not duplicated here.
