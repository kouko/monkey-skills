## 1. Edmondson — Psychological safety as precondition for honest discovery

**Primary citation**:
- Edmondson, A. C. (1999). "Psychological Safety and Learning Behavior
  in Work Teams." *Administrative Science Quarterly*, 44(2), 350-383.
  DOI: 10.2307/2666999. Foundational empirical study.
- Edmondson, A. C. (2012). *Teaming: How Organizations Learn, Innovate,
  and Compete in the Knowledge Economy*. San Francisco: Jossey-Bass.
  ISBN 978-0-7879-7093-2. Book-length synthesis.

**Verification**:
- 1999 ASQ paper indexed at https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.2307/2666999
  and on the author's HBS faculty page
  (https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=2959).
- 2012 *Teaming* listed at HBS faculty publications
  (https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=42298) and indexed
  in Semantic Scholar
  (https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/a2658cf010a4dc32e96c4cdc0f62491dcd0e0552).

**Why this skill needs it**: 4DX's Step 3 (brainstorm wide) and Step 4
(two-axis scoring) only produce honest output if team members feel safe
proposing counter-intuitive candidates and disagreeing publicly with
the leader's instincts. Edmondson's 1999 study found psychological
safety predicted team learning behavior independent of leader
effectiveness. The book's silent-leader rule ("don't fill silence with
your own answer") presupposes safety; without it, silence becomes the
team waiting for the leader to take back the pen. *Teaming* (2012)
extends this from stable teams to flexible cross-functional groupings —
relevant for product/platform teams that re-form per quarter.

**Diagnostic mapped to the skill**: in Step 3, count how many team
members propose at least one counter-intuitive candidate. If only the
1-2 most senior voices speak, the bottleneck is psychological safety,
not facilitation skill. Run the skill anyway, but expect the resulting
leads to inherit the safety gap (CE-15 compliance-without-commitment
will surface 4 weeks later).

## 2. Lencioni — Trust → conflict → commitment chain for veto debate

**Primary citation**:
- Lencioni, P. (2002). *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team: A Leadership
  Fable*. San Francisco: Jossey-Bass. ISBN 978-0-7879-6075-9.
- Anniversary edition (Wiley 2022) preserves original 5-stage model;
  2002 edition is the SoT for the canonical sequence.

**Verification**:
- Wiley product page (https://www.wiley.com/en-us/9780787960759).
- Reviewed in *Research in Hospitality Management* 11(3), 2021
  (https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/22243534.2021.2006940),
  confirming 2002 / Jossey-Bass / ISBN 0787960756.
- Stage sequence (absence of trust → fear of conflict → lack of
  commitment → avoidance of accountability → inattention to results)
  is the canonical model; corroborated across Wikipedia + Wiley
  marketing copy + multiple reviews.

**Why this skill needs it**: This skill's Step 4 (mediation when team
disagrees on influenceability) and Step 5 (leader veto's a candidate
without authoring replacement) both REQUIRE Stage 2 (productive
conflict) to function. If the team is at Stage 1 (no trust) or Stage 0
(unspoken hostility), Step 4 collapses into either silence ("artificial
harmony" — Lencioni's term) or personalized argument. The skill's
veto-don't-dictate move only produces commitment (Stage 3) downstream
if conflict (Stage 2) is healthy upstream.

**Diagnostic mapped to the skill**: in Step 4, when the team is asked
to score influenceability, watch the highest-status quiet member. If
they don't push back on the most-senior voice's score, the team is
operating below Stage 2 conflict. The lead measure that emerges will
be the most-senior voice's preference dressed as consensus — which is
the very failure mode (CE-15) the veto-don't-dictate rule was designed
to prevent.

## 3. Pfeffer & Sutton — Evidence-based skepticism on copy-pasted leads

**Primary citation**:
- Pfeffer, J., & Sutton, R. I. (2006). *Hard Facts, Dangerous
  Half-Truths, and Total Nonsense: Profiting from Evidence-Based
  Management*. Boston: Harvard Business School Press.
  ISBN 978-1-59139-862-2.

**Verification**:
- Stanford GSB faculty publications page
  (https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/books/hard-facts-dangerous-half-truths).
- Author's site (https://jeffreypfeffer.com/books/hard-facts-dangerous-half-truths-and-total-nonsense/).
- HBSP product page (https://hbsp.harvard.edu/product/8622-PDF-ENG).
- *Globe and Mail* best-business-book of 2006.

**Why this skill needs it**: 4DX's Ch 3 famously advises "look at
what others who solved similar goals did differently" (P-12, 80/20
leverage analysis). Naïvely applied, this becomes "Wegmans does X, so
let's do X" — importing a case-study lead without testing it against
THIS team's predictive chain. Pfeffer & Sutton's central argument is
that benchmarking-by-anecdote ignores survivorship bias and base rates;
evidence-based management requires testing whether the imported
practice is *causally* relevant in the local context. Combined with the
acknowledged FranklinCovey selection bias in the source book itself
(see BOOK_OVERVIEW Critical phase: "all ~4,000 case studies are
clients who paid for implementation services"), the implication is
clear: every imported lead candidate must clear the two-axis test
against THIS team's WIG before adoption.

**Diagnostic mapped to the skill**: in Step 4, if a team member
justifies a candidate primarily by external precedent ("Stripe does
this", "Toyota does this", "the book says…"), the leader runs the
two-axis test EXTRA rigorously and asks the team to articulate the
causal chain in their own context. External precedent is permissible
as a search prompt (broaden the candidate pool) but never as the
predictive evidence (only the local two-axis test counts).

## How these three sources stack with the source book

| Layer | Source | Provides |
|---|---|---|
| Methodology | 4DX Ch 3 / 7 / 13 | Two-axis test + veto-don't-dictate + brainstorm-narrow protocol |
| Precondition: voice | Edmondson 1999/2012 | Psychological safety predicts honest candidate proposal |
| Precondition: debate | Lencioni 2002 | Trust → conflict → commitment chain enables productive veto |
| Precondition: skepticism | Pfeffer & Sutton 2006 | Evidence-based filter on imported case-study leads |

The 4DX book treats team buy-in as the natural output of running the
methodology. These sources collectively show that buy-in is the output
only when voice + debate + evidence-skepticism preconditions are
already in place. When they're not, the skill still runs but produces
CE-15 compliance-without-commitment as the silent failure mode.
