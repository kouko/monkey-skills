# 4DX Meta-Team Strategy Triage — Should YOUR team adopt 4DX?

## R — Reading

> "The first mainly requires a stroke of the pen; the second is a breakthrough, requiring a change in behavior."
>
> — McChesney et al., *Chapter 1: The Real Problem With Execution*

> "The 4 Disciplines are a matched set, not a menu of choices."
>
> — McChesney et al., *Chapter 1*

> "Begin by populating the left and right sections of the Strategy Map with the stroke-of-the-pen aspects … as well as the key metrics of your whirlwind."
>
> — McChesney et al., *Chapter 6: Choosing Where to Focus*

---

## I — Interpretation

Before a team leader installs 4DX across their team, two questions must clear: (1) is the team's headline goal even a 4DX-shaped problem? (2) is the team itself the right shape to actually run all four disciplines together?

The book's three-bucket triage carries directly to team scope. **Stroke-of-the-pen** outcomes are achievable by the leader's own authority — sign the contract, hire the role, change the rule. **Whirlwind** outcomes are achievable by the team doing its existing work a bit better. **Behavioral-change** outcomes require many people on the team to sustain new daily practices for months; only this third bucket needs 4DX. The Strategy Map (Ch 6) makes the carve-out visual — left column stroke-of-pen, right column whirlwind, middle column behavioral-change zone where the Primary WIG can live.

But team scope adds gates the personal version doesn't have. A team can be the wrong *shape* for 4DX even when the goal is correctly behavioral-change: firefighting roles where reactive IS strategic (ER, customer-support frontline, on-call SRE) — 4DX inverts wrong; teams below 3 (no peer accountability) or above ~12 (cadence becomes report-out theater); teams whose leader has no authority to carve out protected WIG time; single-shot projects with a fixed end-state where project management beats a continuous-cadence operating system; candidate WIGs that violate the team's stated mission and vision (P-45 veto).

The output is a verdict that either greenlights the leader to proceed to team-WIG-selection, or names which gate failed and what to use instead. Refusing 4DX when the team-fit is wrong is the skill's job — not bending the team into 4DX shape.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: Spartanburg Marriott — capital alone failed (correct triage to behavioral-change at team scope)
- **Problem**: A $20M renovation was planned to lift guest satisfaction at an aging Marriott property. Leadership initially treated this as a stroke-of-the-pen capital decision.
- **Methodology applied**: Re-classified the goal: even with the renovation, hitting the satisfaction lag required front-line teams (housekeeping, front desk, F&B) to consistently change daily behaviors over many months — not a single capital-budget approval. Triaged into the *behavioral-change* bucket; installed all four disciplines across team layers.
- **Conclusion**: 4DX was the correct fit at team scope; capital expenditure alone would not have moved the lag. Matched-set rule applied — D1 through D4 installed together, no cherry-pick.
- **Outcome**: The hotel achieved breakthrough satisfaction scores; case became the flagship 4DX example and seeded the 70K-Marriott-leader rollout.

### Case 2: Beverly Walker — Georgia child welfare (mission-critical behavioral change at scale)
- **Problem**: Repeat child-abuse cases stayed flat despite reorgs, hiring, and policy changes across ~20K caseworkers. State leadership had exhausted stroke-of-the-pen levers.
- **Methodology applied**: Walker (foreword author) recognized the residual gap was structural behavior change across many small teams of caseworkers. Reframed as 4DX-shaped: required sustained new daily practices (home-visit protocols, follow-up cadence) — not another reorganization.
- **Conclusion**: When stroke-of-pen options have been spent and the lag is still flat, the residual gap is by definition behavioral. 4DX applicable at team scope, layered up to a Leader-of-Leaders rollout.
- **Outcome**: Repeat child-abuse cases declined; public-sector / mission-driven proof point that the team-scope triage is domain-agnostic.

### Case 3: Comcast Greater Chicago — last-to-first division turnaround
- **Problem**: A division had been ranked last in customer experience among Comcast regions for years. Leadership had cycled through reorgs, training, and scripts without sustained lift.
- **Methodology applied**: Triaged as full-4DX-install. Stroke-of-pen levers (new tools, new policies) had been used; the residual gap was behavioral across hundreds of frontline-team installations. Each frontline team got a Team WIG, lead measures, scoreboard, and weekly WIG Session.
- **Conclusion**: Correct triage to behavioral-change with team-shape adequate (frontline teams in 3-12 range, leader authority over time, cadence sustainable). Matched-set installed, not cherry-picked.
- **Outcome**: Division moved from worst to best in customer experience; demonstrates that 4DX team-triage holds even in already-overworked operational settings when the team-shape gates clear.

## A2 — Future Trigger ★

### When will the leader need this skill?

1. **Pre-rollout sanity check** — Leader has read the book or attended a workshop and is deciding whether to spend a quarter installing 4DX on their team versus picking a lighter framework.
2. **OKR-fatigue rebound** — Leader's team tried OKRs (or quarterly goals, or a balanced scorecard) and lost steam; reaching for 4DX as the next thing, may again be a wrong-shape fit.
3. **Annual-planning fork** — Leader has a list of annual targets handed down and is choosing operating cadence (4DX vs PM vs OKR vs status-quo) for the team.
4. **Methodology-evangelism resistance** — Leader's boss or peer is pushing 4DX; leader wants a defensible "yes / no / not yet" answer rooted in the book's own scope rules, not vibes.
5. **Post-fail re-triage** — Team installed 4DX, it stalled at month 2-3; leader wonders whether the fit was wrong from day one. Re-triage now before re-investing.

### Non-activation signals (do NOT trigger when…)

- User is asking about a **personal / solo goal** (no team) — route to `4dx-meta-personal-strategy-triage`.
- Team has already committed to 4DX and the leader asks *"how do we pick our WIG?"* — route to `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection`.
- Leader-of-leaders rolling out across **multiple teams** — recommend reading book Ch 6-10 directly and use `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` once teams are scoped.
- Asking about a specific discipline (lead measures, scoreboard, WIG session mechanics) — route to the matching D-skill.
- Generic team-strategy question with no 4DX vocabulary and no scope-fit framing — let other decision skills handle it.

### Distinction from neighboring skills

- vs. `4dx-meta-personal-strategy-triage`: that one is solo (one person, one goal); this one is for a leader judging fit for a 3-12 person team. The personal version's anti-patterns (habit / portfolio-bet / creative) are individual-scale; this skill's anti-patterns (team-shape, time-authority, mission-alignment) are organizational.
- vs. `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection`: this skill asks *"is 4DX right for our team?"*; that one assumes 4DX is chosen and helps pick the Primary WIG from candidates.
- vs. `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade`: this skill is single-team scope; cascade is for translating an upper-level WIG down through multiple sub-teams.
- vs. OKR: OKR also works at team scale but emphasizes ambition + transparency over lead-measure / weekly-cadence discipline; if the team needs portfolio kill criteria (which 4DX lacks) or a stretch-vs-commit split, OKR is the lighter fit.

## E — Execution

When this skill activates, the agent acts as a consultant to the leader. Each step asks the leader a clarifying question about THEIR team — not about the leader as an individual. Each step has a completion criterion that decides whether to proceed or route out.

1. **Surface the team and the candidate goal in concrete terms.**
   - Ask: *"How many people on the team, and what's the headline outcome you're trying to drive over the next 6-12 months? One sentence."*
   - Completion criterion: leader has named team size + a goal explicit enough to classify. If goal is vague ("improve performance") follow up with: *"What would 'improved' look like in measurable terms by when?"*

2. **Stroke-of-the-pen check (Ch 1 + Ch 6 Strategy Map left column).**
   - Ask: *"Is there a single decision YOU control — a contract to sign, a hire to make, a tool to buy, a rule to change — that by itself would deliver this outcome? Or does it require many people on your team to sustain new daily practices for months?"*
   - Completion criterion: leader converges on {single-decision / sustained-team-behavior / unsure}.
   - **Halt condition**: if single-decision → return verdict **stroke-of-the-pen, 4DX overkill** (e.g. "if it's 'replace the legacy CRM' — sign the contract; 4DX would be ceremony"). Skill ends.

3. **Whirlwind-handleable check (Ch 6 Strategy Map right column).**
   - Ask: *"Could the team hit this just by doing its existing work a bit better — sharper execution of the same routine, no new categories of action, no new daily practice?"*
   - Completion criterion: leader converges on yes/no.
   - **Halt condition**: if yes → return verdict **whirlwind-handleable, 4DX overkill** ("optimize the existing flow; pull the lever you have"). Skill ends.

4. **Team-shape screen** (the four team-scope traps the personal version doesn't carry).
   - Ask each in turn, only as relevant:
     - *"How many people report to you on this team — directly?"* → if <3 (no peer accountability dynamic) or >12 (WIG Session devolves to report-out), return verdict **wrong-team-shape (size out of 3-12)** with split / consolidate recommendation.
     - *"Is your team's primary purpose reactive — handling whatever comes in (incident response, customer support, ER, on-call)? Or is it proactive — pursuing an outcome?"* → if reactive-primary, return verdict **wrong-team-shape (firefighting role)** — the whirlwind IS your team's strategic value; 4DX inverts wrong. Tighten reactive-quality metrics instead.
     - *"Do you, as the leader, have the authority to carve out protected WIG time on the team's calendar — block ~20% of capacity from incoming demands?"* → if no, return verdict **no-time-authority** — fix the upstream demand-protection problem first; 4DX cannot install on a team whose time is owned by someone else.
     - *"Is this work a single-shot project with a fixed end-state and a known plan (build X, deliver, done)? Or is it an ongoing capability the team is building?"* → if single-shot fixed-plan, return verdict **single-shot-project — use project management not 4DX** (4DX's continuous-cadence operating system is overhead when there's a finite task list with a Gantt).
   - Completion criterion: each gate explicitly checked and either matched (skill ends with redirect) or cleared.

5. **WIG-shape check — From X to Y by When (P-30, P-45 mission-vision veto).**
   - Ask: *"Can the goal be expressed as 'from X to Y by when'? And does pursuing it support your team's stated mission and vision — would your boss and your team's senior leadership endorse it as on-mission?"*
   - Completion criterion: leader either produces a draft From-X-to-Y-by-When that aligns to mission, or admits one or both gates fail.
   - **Halt condition**: if cannot express as From-X-to-Y-by-When → recurring service delivery without a transformative target; 4DX has no entry point. If WIG conflicts with mission/vision → return verdict **mission-misaligned** (P-45 hard rule).

6. **Adoption-curve readiness check (F-28 Models / Not Yets / Nevers).**
   - Ask: *"Of your team, who would you classify as Models (already execute disciplined when given a clear target), Not Yets (capable but inconsistent), Nevers (won't engage no matter what)? Roughly what's the split?"*
   - Completion criterion: leader has a rough split (e.g. 3 / 4 / 1).
   - **Halt condition**: if Nevers majority or no Models, surface the risk — 4DX rollout will degrade to compliance theater. Recommend addressing the team-composition problem before installing the methodology.

7. **Matched-set commitment (P-23 hard rule).**
   - Ask: *"4DX is all four disciplines or none — a Primary / Team WIG, daily lead measures, a visible weekly scoreboard, and a 20-30 min weekly WIG Session you personally lead with member commitments. Are you in for all four, sustained, for at least one quarter?"*
   - Completion criterion: leader explicitly says yes / no.
   - **Halt condition**: if leader wants to cherry-pick (e.g. "just D1 + D4") → flag P-23 (matched set, not menu) and offer either commit-to-all-four or use a lighter framework instead.

8. **Verdict + handoff.**
   - If all gates passed → return verdict **TEAM-APPLICABLE — proceed to `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection`**.
   - In all other branches the redirect verdict is the output; do not bend the team-shape back into 4DX.
   - Completion criterion: a single verdict has been issued from the 6-category set.

## B — Boundary ★

### Do NOT use this skill in:

- **Solo / individual-contributor goals.** NOT for solo / individual contributor — use `4dx-meta-personal-strategy-triage`. The team-shape gates (size 3-12, peer accountability, leader-of-team authority) are nonsensical for one person; the personal version has different anti-patterns (habit / portfolio / creative / on-call) that fit individual scale.
- **Already-committed teams asking how to start D1.** Route to `4dx-d1-team-primary-wig-selection` and `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade`; don't re-gate work that's past this stage.
- **Leader-of-Leaders multi-team rollout.** This skill is single-team scope. For VPs / division leads cascading 4DX across multiple teams, recommend the book's Ch 6-10 directly and `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` once each team is scoped. The triage logic is similar but the actor model + Strategy Map work is multi-team and needs the cascade skill.
- **Specific-discipline questions** ("how do we pick lead measures?", "what goes on the scoreboard?") — route to the matching D-skill.

### Author-warned failure modes

- **CE-06 — Stroke-of-the-pen miscategorization.** Applying 4DX where leader authority alone suffices wastes the team's attention; the book's strongest scope warning. The agent must actively look for "is there a single decision the leader can make that solves this?" — leaders often default to elaborate methodology when the answer is "sign the contract / change the policy".
- **CE-25 — Wrong-WIG-locked-in (no kill criteria).** 4DX has no built-in mechanism to detect that the WIG itself is wrong; teams in exploratory / portfolio modes can spend a 6-month cycle perfecting execution of the wrong goal. Triage must catch this *before* installation — if the team is genuinely betting and unsure which goal is right, OKR's stretch-vs-commit gives kill criteria 4DX lacks.
- **CE-26 — Whirlwind-as-the-strategic-work.** For reactive teams (ER, customer-support frontline, on-call SRE, NOC, incident-response) the whirlwind is not a competitor to strategic work — it IS the strategic value. Manufacturing a 20% sub-WIG that competes with on-call duty inverts the role and degrades the team's actual mission.
- **CE-27 — Knowledge-work feedback loop too long for weekly cadence.** Drug discovery, hardware engineering, deep-research teams operate on multi-month feedback loops; weekly 4DX cadence becomes theatrical. Adjust cadence or pick a different operating system.
- **CE-30 — FranklinCovey selection bias.** All ~4,000 case studies are paying clients; failures are anecdotal. The leader must NOT assume "4DX worked at Marriott so it'll work for our team" — do the team-fit check first, every time.

### Author's blind spots / period limitations

- The book's case studies are dominated by US-anglophone-corporate operational settings (hotels, retail, transport, manufacturing). Knowledge-work, R&D, and creative teams are under-represented; the agent should be more cautious gating these.
- "Public peer commitment" assumes low-context / direct-feedback culture. In high-context cultures (JP, ZH, KR) public peer accountability inside a Team WIG Session can read as face-loss; the book offers no adaptation. The leader may need to soften the WIG Session ritual without removing the matched-set gates.
- The book pre-dates remote-default work normalization. In-person social pressure was the original active ingredient of weekly cadence; for fully-remote teams the WIG Session needs deliberate ritual design (camera-on, scoreboard share, individual commits) to retain the dynamic.
- Team-size bands (3-12) are derived from the book's worked examples and FranklinCovey practice; not from controlled empirical study. Treat as a strong heuristic, not a hard cutoff.

### Easily-confused neighboring methodologies

- **OKR** (John Doerr / *Measure What Matters*) — also team-scale, also From-X-to-Y format; differs in (a) explicit stretch vs commit split with built-in kill criteria 4DX lacks; (b) quarterly grading replaces weekly cadence; (c) less emphasis on lead measures + weekly accountability ritual. Use OKR when the team is in portfolio / discovery mode and needs to kill goals; use 4DX when the goal is correct and the gap is execution discipline.
- **Project management (PMI / Agile / Gantt)** — for single-shot fixed-plan deliverables; 4DX's continuous-cadence operating system is overhead when there's a finite task list with a known sequence.
- **Balanced Scorecard / KPI dashboards** (Kaplan & Norton) — for steady-state performance monitoring across many dimensions; no narrowing-to-one-WIG discipline, no behavioral-change focus.
- **Personal 4DX triage** (`4dx-meta-personal-strategy-triage`) — solo scope; missing the team-shape gates this skill enforces (size, time-authority, firefighting-role, single-shot-project, mission-alignment).

### NOT for: solo / individual contributor

NOT for solo / individual contributor — use `4dx-meta-personal-strategy-triage`. NOT for multi-team Leader-of-Leaders rollout — read book Ch 6-10 directly and use `4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade` once teams are scoped.

### Industry-experience addendum (organizational change-readiness)

The 6-verdict triage catches goal-shape and partial team-shape failures but silently assumes change-readiness. Three gates from the change-management literature, before returning **TEAM-APPLICABLE**. Primary citations in `references/industry-grounding.md`:

- **Kotter, *Leading Change* (HBS Press, 1996/2012)** — ~100 transformations studied; ~70% fail in Stage 1 (urgency) or Stage 2 (guiding coalition) before any methodology is installed. Gate: *is the team's urgency ≥ the leader's?* If no, a WIG installed on a non-urgent team becomes Friday ceremony.
- **Galbraith STAR Model (Jossey-Bass, 2002/2014)** — Strategy / Structure / Processes / Rewards / People; any one misaligned lever degrades performance. The book's gates cover Strategy + partial Structure; F-28 partially covers People. Gate: *do existing reviews and bonuses reward whirlwind firefighting over WIG progress?* If yes, the incentive system defeats the WIG within a quarter.
- **Schein, *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (Jossey-Bass, 1985/2017)** — Artifacts / Espoused Values / Underlying Assumptions; methodology touches only Artifacts; assumptions win silently. Gate: *when a member's Friday WIG commitment conflicts with their boss's Monday request, what wins?* If boss-request always wins, the assumption is "compliance over commitment" — change the assumption first.

If any gate fails, return **TEAM-NOT-YET-READY** instead.
