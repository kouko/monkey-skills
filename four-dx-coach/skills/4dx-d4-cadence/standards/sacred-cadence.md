# Standard: Sacred cadence — never skip; reschedule, never cancel

## Statement

The WIG Session runs **same day, same time, same length, every
week**, regardless of the whirlwind. **Cancel-and-reschedule** within
the same week if absolutely necessary; **never skip**. If the leader
is travelling, sick, or otherwise absent, the session still runs —
delegate the chair, dial in via video, but don't cancel. Skipped
sessions collapse the cadence within 3-4 weeks.

## Source

> Hold WIG Sessions to the same day at the same time in the same place every week (including electronically), regardless of the whirlwind. If you're gone, delegate leadership of the session to another team member.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 15

> The WIG Session is sacred—it takes place every week, even if the leader can't attend.
>
> — *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 5

## Why it matters

Cadence consistency is itself the trust-currency mechanism. The book
asserts this on practitioner authority; primary sources give the
mechanism:

- **Reinertsen, *Principles of Product Development Flow* (2009)** —
  Principle F6 (Cadence Principle): fixed cadence transforms
  unpredictable events into predictable ones and prevents variability
  from accumulating in sequential processes.
- **Edmondson, *Teaming* (2012)** — when the leader holds the cadence
  even under stress, the team learns the discipline is for everyone,
  including the leader. A skipped session signals the discipline is
  optional, conditional on convenience.

The empirical pattern across 4DX case studies (MICARE, Store 334,
Cystic Fibrosis Center): teams that hold cadence rigidly even on hell
weeks see lag movement in 3-6 months; teams that skip "just this
week" because of travel / busyness / firefighting see cadence collapse
within 3-4 weeks and the WIG dies.

Hell weeks are exactly when the session matters most. Skipping during
a hell week is the single highest-leverage way to kill the cadence —
because if it's skippable then, it's skippable always, and the team
quickly learns the rule.

The "cancel-and-reschedule, never skip" rule operationalizes this:
move the time within the same week if absolutely necessary, but the
session has to happen. Delegating the chair to another member when
the leader is out is the team-mode variant; it doubles as
leader-development.

## Application across modes

| Mode | How this standard applies |
|---|---|
| **Solo** | User enforces against themselves. Agent's job in Step 6 (close): "Same time next week." If user signals they want to skip, hold the line: "WIG Sessions are sacred. Cancel-and-reschedule, but don't skip. When this week, what time?" Agent must NOT collude with the skip. The lone failure mode the book under-validates for solo (CE-28: no peer to keep the user honest) makes this enforcement extra-important; if the user falls off entirely, route to `4dx-sustain-personal-momentum-rescue`. |
| **Team-leader** | Leader's primary job. Same day, same time, same place, every week. If leader is travelling: "I'll dial in / [member] will chair — we don't skip." Coach reminder: "Cadence consistency is the trust signal. The team learns the discipline is for everyone, including you, when you hold it under stress." Pre-session check (Step 1) confirms scoreboard updated, time-box set, WIG stated verbatim. |
| **Member-prep** | Indirect — member doesn't set the cadence. But the prep step itself depends on the cadence holding (no point preparing for a session that gets cancelled). If member reports the team's cadence has been broken multiple weeks, surface that and route to `4dx-sustain-personal-momentum-rescue` or escalate to leader. |
| **Member-debrief** | Indirect — member doesn't set the cadence. But **no-mention mode** is one of the five debrief failure modes precisely because skipping the Account silently undermines D4. The cadence runs only if the report-back happens; refusing to report is a personal-level cadence break even when the team session happens. |

Across all four modes, the underlying rule is the same: the cadence is
not optional, not conditional on convenience, not subject to "we'll
skip just this week". The mechanism is sacred *because* skipping
collapses it.
