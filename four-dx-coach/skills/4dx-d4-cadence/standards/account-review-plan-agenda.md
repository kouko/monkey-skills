# Standard: Account → Review → Plan agenda grammar

## Statement

A WIG Session always runs three fixed segments in this order: **Account**
(report on last week's commitments), **Review** (look at the lead + lag
on the scoreboard), **Plan** (make 1-2 new self-chosen commitments for
the coming week). Total time-box: 20-30 minutes. Centre of gravity is
**Plan** (~40%), not Account (~25-30%) or Review (~25%).

## Source

> The standard agenda for the WIG Session has just three items: 1. Account: Report on commitments. 2. Review the scoreboard: Learn from successes and failures. 3. Plan: Clear the path and make new commitments.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 5

Lencioni's *Death by Meeting* (Jossey-Bass, 2004) Weekly Tactical
taxonomy maps almost one-to-one: lightning round → Account; progress
review → Review; real-time agenda → Plan. The 4DX *adds*
whirlwind-exclusion + commit-forward (not report-out) on top.

## Why it matters

The shape is what makes the WIG Session a forcing function rather than
a status meeting. Three failure modes flow directly from breaking the
shape:

1. **Treating session as report-out** — when Account swells past 50%,
   the session becomes a status meeting and lead measures stop moving.
   The book is firm: Plan is the centre of gravity.
2. **Skipping Account when uncomfortable** — the team's signal of
   trust-currency comes from honest reporting of last week's outcome.
   Skipping it (or rushing past misses) silently undermines D4.
3. **Skipping Review** — the scoreboard is the only artifact that can
   tell the team whether their lead measure is actually a lead. Without
   Review, commitments get made against folklore, not signal.

Account fails when story-mode crowds out one-word classification (kept
/ partial / missed). Plan fails when commitments get dictated, vague,
dependency-laden, or inflated past the 1-2 cap.

## Application across modes

| Mode | How this standard applies |
|---|---|
| **Solo** | Agent runs the three segments as peer-witness in one ~25 min session. Account = single-person report on prior commitments. Review = user reads their own scoreboard aloud. Plan = user states 1-2 self-chosen commitments. Plan dominates time. |
| **Team-leader** | Leader holds the three segments. Account is round-robin (leader-first, modeling). Review is collective scoreboard read. Plan is per-member with leader gating. Time budget: ~7-10 / 5-7 / 8-10 min per segment. |
| **Member-prep** | Member prepares only the **Plan** half — the commitment they will voice. Doesn't touch Account/Review (those happen at the live session). Step 5 rehearsal of wording = the verbatim sentence that will land in Plan segment. |
| **Member-debrief** | Member prepares only the **Account** half — the 30-second statement they will deliver. Doesn't touch Review/Plan (those happen at live session). The five-question debrief Q1-Q5 maps to: commitment / outcome / cause / learning / next-step intention — composed into a single Account-segment script. |

A member-prep / member-debrief hand-off pair across one week constitutes
a member's full member-side participation: prep before / Account at
session / debrief after / new prep before next.
