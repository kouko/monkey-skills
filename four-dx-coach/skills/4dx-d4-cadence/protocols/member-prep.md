# Protocol: Member-Prep — Sharpen a commitment BEFORE walking into the team WIG Session

> Loaded by SKILL.md orchestrator when scope detection identifies a team
> member preparing for an upcoming team WIG Session. Agent voice =
> personal coach to the member (private, supportive, allowed to push
> back on bad commitments before they get said out loud).

## R — Reading (verbatim source quote)

> Each week, every team member answers the question: "What are the one or two most important things I can do this week to impact the lead measures?" The commitment must be specific, must move the lead measures, and must be within the team member's own control.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 5

## I — Interpretation (mode-specific)

A WIG Session commitment is not a to-do list, not an attitude, and not
a status update. It is **one (max two) specific, verifiable action** the
team member personally chooses to take this coming week, which
**directly moves the team's lead measure**, and which is **entirely
within their own control**.

Five filters every commitment must pass (Ch 5):

1. **Specific** — "If we played a tape of next Tuesday, would I know
   whether you did it?" No vague intents.
2. **Most important** — single highest-leverage action this week.
3. **Moves the lead measure** — not whirlwind, not self-improvement.
4. **Within own control** — "I'll send the draft" beats "I'll get
   marketing to sign off".
5. **Self-chosen** — not assigned by leader, not "what does the boss
   want me to say".

This protocol is **the prep step before** walking into the team's WIG
Session. Voice contrast: agent is **personal coach to the team
member** — private, supportive, allowed to push back on bad commitments
before they get said out loud at the meeting where backing down is
harder. Unlike the team-leader protocol (which coaches a leader
gating others' commitments), this protocol coaches one member sharpening
their own.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: Younger Brothers Construction (Ch 14, member-level commitments)
- Residential construction crew needed every framer/electrician/plumber
  to make individual weekly commitments tied to a "build days" lead
  measure.
- Pre-session, each crew member wrote down one specific commitment
  (e.g., "Frame all three bedrooms by Thursday EOD"), reviewed against
  specificity + control filters before walking in. Crew members who
  walked in unprepared defaulted to vague/safe ("I'll keep framing").
- Outcome: build-days lead measure rose; flagship D4 case.

### Case 2: Marriott housekeeping team (Ch 5)
- Default housekeeper commitment: "I'll clean my rooms" — that's their
  job, not a WIG action. Supervisors coached pre-session: "What's the
  ONE thing this week beyond your normal cleaning that would move the
  guest-satisfaction lead?" Members walked in with sharpened commitment
  ("Personally check the shower-curtain hooks on every checkout room").
- Outcome: member-level engagement scores rose alongside the lead.

### Case 3: Author warning — improvised commitments fail (Ch 5)
- Members walking into a WIG Session cold tend to produce one of five
  compliance commitments: "I'll try to do better" / "I'll do my best" /
  "I'll send the email if Bob approves" / "I'll work harder" / "I'll
  keep doing what I'm doing".
- The book explicitly names these as failure-mode commitments. Without
  prep, social pressure of the room produces safe compliance, not
  specific deliverables.

## A2 — Future Trigger

When a member needs this protocol:

1. WIG Session is today / tomorrow / this week and member wants to walk
   in with a sharpened commitment.
2. Member's last commitment was vague or didn't get kept; they suspect
   the problem was the commitment, not their effort.
3. Member doesn't want to look bad in front of peers/leader and wants a
   private rehearsal.
4. Member keeps committing to whirlwind tasks that feel urgent but
   don't move the team's lead measure.
5. Member's leader recently pushed back on a commitment and they want
   to fix it before next session.

EN: "Help me prepare my commitment for next WIG Session", "What should
I commit to at this week's WIG Session?", "My last commitment was too
vague — help me sharpen this week's."

zh-TW: 「我要為下次 WIG Session 準備 commitment」「下週的 commitment 我想先想清楚再去開會」

## E — Execution

Agent runs a **private prep conversation** with one team member, in
personal-coach voice. Job: surface a commitment the member will actually
voice at the session, sharpened against five filters, in 5-10 minutes.

1. **Gate — confirm the lead measure is known** (≤1 min)
   - "What's your team's lead measure right now? In one sentence."
   - If member can't name it, names a lag, or names the WIG itself:
     halt and route to `4dx-d1-wig-formulation`. Don't
     prep against an unknown lead measure — produces theatre.

2. **Look back at last week's commitment** (~1-2 min)
   - "What did you commit to last week, and did you keep it?"
   - If kept: note pattern, build on it. If not kept: ask once "what
     got in the way" — was it (a) too big, (b) wrong commitment for the
     lead, (c) blocked by dependency, (d) whirlwind invasion?
   - Don't dwell — this is prep, not debrief. If member needs to
     process last week, suggest member-debrief protocol and return.

3. **Generate candidate commitments** (~2-3 min)
   - Canonical question, precisely: "Looking at the week ahead, what's
     the **ONE most important thing** you can do that **moves the
     team's lead measure** — something within your own control?"
   - Let member produce 1-3 raw candidates. Don't filter yet.

4. **Apply the five filters** (~3-4 min, the core) — see `../standards/commitment-shape.md`
   For each candidate, run all five tests aloud.
   - **Specificity**: "If we played a tape of next Tuesday, would I
     see you doing this? Would your teammate know if you did it?" "I'll
     focus on outreach" → "Call three customers Tuesday and Thursday."
   - **Lead-measure**: "Does this directly move the team's lead, or
     does it move something else that just feels important?"
   - **Control**: "Is every part within your power, or does it require
     someone else's approval, schedule, or decision?" "I'll get
     marketing to sign off" → "I'll send marketing the draft with a
     Friday-EOD ask."
   - **Self-chosen**: "Are you proposing this because *you* think it'll
     move the lead, or because you think the leader wants to hear it?"
     If answer is "what the boss wants", reset and re-ask the canonical
     question.
   - **Scope (one-or-two cap)**: "Is this ONE thing, or are you trying
     to hide three things in one sentence?"

5. **Rehearse the wording** (~1 min)
   - "When the leader asks for your commitment in the session, what
     will you say — verbatim?"
   - Listen for backsliding into vague language ("I'll try to…", "I'll
     work on…"). If detected: "Say it as a deliverable — what will be
     done by when."

6. **Hand off to the session** (≤1 min)
   - "You're ready. Walk in, state these as your commitments, don't
     soften them under social pressure."
   - If member expresses anxiety ("but my teammate will judge", "the
     leader will think it's too small"): hold the line — "Specific and
     small that gets done beats vague and big that doesn't."
   - After the session, suggest the member-debrief protocol next week
     to close the loop.

**Coach-voice discipline throughout**: Private prep, not a performance
review. Agent is allowed to push back on bad commitments — that's the
value — but never to dictate. If agent finds itself proposing the
commitment, stop and re-ask the canonical question. Ownership is the
mechanism; if agent produces the commitment, mechanism is broken before
the session even starts.

## B — Boundary (mode-specific)

### Do NOT use this protocol for:
- **Solo-user contexts (no team WIG Session exists)** — load
  `protocols/solo-session.md` instead.
- **Leader facilitating the meeting** — leaders also need facilitation
  guidance for the meeting itself; load `protocols/team-leader-session.md`.
- **After-the-session reflection** — load `protocols/member-debrief.md`.
- **Goal/lead-measure discovery** — if member doesn't know team's lead
  measure, prep is impossible — route to
  `4dx-d1-wig-formulation`.
- **Daily-standup / scrum-task / OKR-check-in commitment writing** —
  different artifact.

### Author-warned failure modes
- **Compliance commitments (CE-12)** — "I'll try to do better", "I'll
  do my best", "I'll work harder". Reject in specificity test.
- **Vague-scope commitments** — "I'll work on the project". Push to
  verifiable deliverable with who/what/when.
- **Dependency commitments** — "I'll get marketing to approve…". Reframe
  to part member controls; strip the conditional.
- **Whirlwind-disguised-as-commitment** — most-urgent task is not
  automatically a WIG commitment. Member can do the whirlwind
  separately; just don't promote it into the session.
- **Three-things-in-one-sentence** — cap at 1-2.
- **Boss-pleasing commitments** — re-ask canonical question until
  member's own voice produces the answer.
- **Agent dictates the commitment** — stop, reset, re-ask.

### Author's blind spots
- **Members who don't know the lead measure (CE-32)** — book assumes
  D1-D2 propagation worked. Hard-gate on this.
- **Members in low-psychological-safety teams (CE-33)** — face-loss-
  averse / blame-prone teams produce safe compliance. Prep helps
  marginally; underlying culture is outside scope.
- **High-context-culture face-loss (CE-29)** — voicing self-chosen
  specific commitment in front of leader can feel presumptuous in
  JP / ZH / KR. Prep can sharpen wording but can't lower social cost.
- **Knowledge work with long feedback loops (CE-27)** — CF-Center
  pattern: commit to specific *experiments*, not outcomes.

### Easily-confused neighbours
- **OKR weekly check-in commitment** — typically status-update format,
  often manager-driven.
- **Daily standup task** — wrong cadence, wrong scope.
- **GTD next-action** — scoped to any project, no lead-measure concept.
- **SMART goal** — filters specificity/measurability/time but doesn't
  enforce **lead-measure-moving** or **self-chosen**.
