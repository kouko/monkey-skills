# Protocol: Solo — Run a personal weekly WIG Session as peer-witness

> Loaded by SKILL.md orchestrator when scope detection identifies a solo
> user with no peer team — the agent fills the peer-witness role.

## R — Reading (verbatim source quote)

> The WIG Session is sacred—it takes place every week, even if the leader can't attend… The whirlwind is never allowed into a WIG Session.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 5

## I — Interpretation (mode-specific)

The solo WIG Session is a 20-30 minute weekly meeting whose only job is to
push one Wildly Important Goal forward against the daily whirlwind. The
fixed three-part agenda — **Account / Review / Plan** — runs whether or
not a peer is in the room. With no peer team, the agent fills the
**peer-witness** role: prompts via Socratic questions, never dictates the
commitment, holds the user accountable to specificity, keeps the cadence
sacred even on hell weeks. The agent is *not* boss, coach, or
status-checker — the moment the agent starts dictating commitments, the
ownership mechanism that makes commitment-keeping work breaks.

Voice contrast vs sibling protocols: the team-leader protocol coaches
the *leader* who runs the room; this protocol IS the peer in the room.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: MICARE (Mexican coal mining, Ch 5)
- Monday-morning WIG Sessions across thousands of frontline workers,
  webinar-connected for remote sites; sustained for years. CEO credited
  "concentrated attention to the cadence of accountability" — the
  cadence itself, not any one decision — as the major success factor.
- Outcome (7 years): lost-time accidents 700→60/yr; water use down
  two-thirds; output per worker 6,000→10,000 metric tons.

### Case 2: Cystic Fibrosis Center (Ch 5)
- Doctors held weekly WIG Sessions on body-weight as a lead measure for
  lung function in CF infants. Even ambiguous, multi-week-feedback-loop
  work was driven by a weekly cadence by committing to specific
  *experiments* (mist tents, massage vests, supplemental feedings),
  reporting results back the following week. Center had not lost a
  patient to CF in many years.

### Case 3: Store 334 (Ch 11) — D4 as the missing piece
- Grocery store ranked 250th of 250. D1-D3 had been installed; lead
  measures tracked; no breakthrough. Adding the weekly WIG Session was
  the only missing piece. Store 334 turned into a top-performing store.
  D4 was the active ingredient.

## A2 — Future Trigger

When a solo user would need this protocol:

1. User has a goal but no rhythm — meaningful goal (dissertation, weight
   loss, business launch, marathon training, side project) but drifts
   between weeks of intense effort and weeks of nothing.
2. User asks for a weekly review template firmer than journaling — wants
   commitments held against past commitments, not just reflection.
3. User has lead measures (D2) and a scoreboard (D3) but isn't acting on
   them — data being looked at but no commitment being extracted.
4. User installed all of D1-D3 personally and is asking what closes the
   loop — the answer is this protocol.
5. User asks how to keep a goal alive when life gets chaotic — their
   default is to skip the review when busy, and the goal dies.

EN signal: "Run my WIG Session with me", "I keep losing momentum on my
goal — how do I keep it alive?", "I have a lead measure but I'm not
acting on it — what now?"
JP: 「毎週の振り返りをしたい」「ゴールへの進捗を毎週チェックしたい」
zh-TW: 「想要每週固定 review 維持目標進度」「每週檢查 WIG 進度」

## E — Execution

The agent acts as **peer-witness**, not as boss, coach, or
status-checker. Steps below run sequentially in one session.

1. **Frame & open** (≤2 min)
   - Greet, state the WIG aloud verbatim, state the time-box.
     Example: "Welcome to your weekly WIG Session. Your WIG is *[X]*. We
     have ~25 minutes. Three segments: Account, Review, Plan."
   - Completion: WIG stated verbatim, time-box explicitly named, user
     confirms readiness.

2. **Segment 1 — Account** (~5-7 min) — see `../standards/account-review-plan-agenda.md`
   - "Last week you committed to *[Y]*. What happened?"
   - For each prior commitment, classify aloud: **kept / partially kept
     / not kept**. Listen, mirror, no judgment, no fix.
   - If "didn't keep", ask once: "What got in the way?" Note pattern
     (whirlwind invasion / wrong commitment / over-committed) but do
     NOT problem-solve mid-segment.
   - Halt: if first session ever (no prior commitments), say so and skip
     to step 3.

3. **Segment 2 — Review the scoreboard** (~5-7 min)
   - "What does your scoreboard show this week — lead and lag?"
   - Probe **trend**, not snapshot: "Is the lead trending up, flat, or
     down? Is the lag finally moving?"
   - Ask user to interpret first: "What do you make of that?"
   - **Diagnostic boundary**: if lead green + lag flat for ≥3 weeks,
     flag P-50 ("change the leads, don't push harder") and route to
     `4dx-sustain-personal-momentum-rescue` after the session.
   - Completion: user has stated lead trend, lag trend, and their own
     interpretation aloud.

4. **Segment 3 — Plan** (~8-10 min) — see `../standards/commitment-shape.md`
   - Canonical question, precisely: "What are the **one or two** most
     important things you can do **this week** to move your **lead
     measure**?" (Note the precision — not "what's most important", not
     "what should I do".)
   - For each candidate commitment, apply the two-question filter:
     (a) specific deliverable (someone could verify it was done)?
     (b) does it directly move the lead measure?
   - If either fails, push back Socratically: "How would you know on
     Saturday whether you did that?" / "Does that move the lead, or
     something else?"
   - Cap at 1-2 commitments. If user proposes 3+, push back: "Pick the
     highest-leverage one or two — the rest is whirlwind in disguise."
   - Reject commitments that depend on others: "What's the part of
     that within your control?"

5. **Confirm & log** (~1-2 min)
   - User states the 1-2 commitments aloud verbatim.
   - Agent echoes them back and logs them for next week's Account
     segment.

6. **Close & schedule** (~1 min) — see `../standards/sacred-cadence.md`
   - "Same time next week."
   - If user signals they want to skip next week: hold the line — "WIG
     Sessions are sacred. Cancel-and-reschedule, but don't skip. When
     this week, what time?"

**Whirlwind-creep redirect (any segment)** — see `../standards/whirlwind-exclusion.md`:
If user starts narrating operational drama ("you wouldn't believe how
crazy work was…"), interrupt politely: "Hold that — let's keep the WIG
Session on the WIG. Was that commitment kept or not?" Do this without
apology; the rule is the rule.

## B — Boundary (mode-specific)

### Do NOT use this protocol for:
- **Team facilitation** — for team WIG Sessions the leader runs the
  meeting; load `protocols/team-leader-session.md` instead.
- **Daily standups, status meetings, 1:1s, project reviews** — different
  artifact entirely.
- **Goal-discovery** — if no WIG yet, route to `4dx-d1-wig-formulation`.
- **Annual / quarterly retrospectives** — wrong cadence scope.
- **Reactive / on-call work** — when whirlwind IS the strategic value
  (SRE on-call, ER medicine, customer-success firefighting), the cadence
  model misfits — see `4dx-meta-strategy-triage`.

### Author-warned failure modes
- **Compliance commitments / vague intents (CE-12)** — "I'll try to do
  better", "I'll focus on outreach". Fails specificity. Push back until
  verifiable on Saturday morning.
- **Whirlwind invasion (CE-10)** — session drifts into "let me tell you
  how busy my week was". Defeats the entire mechanism.
- **Skipping when busy (CE-11)** — hell weeks are exactly when the
  session matters most. Cancel-and-reschedule; never skip.
- **Commitment doesn't move the lead measure (CE-13)** — most-urgent
  whirlwind task labelled as commitment. Reject.
- **Boss-assigned / self-dictated commitments (CE-14)** — even in solo
  coaching, agent must NOT dictate the commitment.
- **Too many commitments** — cap at 2. 5 at 50% < 1-2 at 95%.
- **Dependency commitments** — "I'll get my boss to approve X". Reframe
  to user-controlled action.
- **Treating the session as report-out** — Plan is ~40% of time;
  Account ~25%.

### Author's blind spots
- **Solo cadence with no peer (CE-28)** — book assumes a peer team for
  public commitment + social pressure. Agent fills peer-witness role
  partially; users falling off entirely should consider a human
  accountability partner (coach, friend, public log).
- **Knowledge work with long feedback loops (CE-27)** — drug discovery,
  novel research = 6-month loops. CF Center workaround: commit to
  specific *experiments*, not outcomes; lengthen cadence if even
  experiment-cycles are sub-weekly impossible.
- **Goodhart-style gaming on lead measures (CE-24)** — periodic sanity
  check: is the lead still predictive of the lag, or has it become its
  own target?

### Easily-confused neighbours
- **GTD weekly review** (Allen) — broader scope, no WIG / lead-measure /
  cadence-sacred concept.
- **OKR check-in** — typically status-update format, no
  commitment-making segment.
- **Bullet-journal monthly migration** — reflection-heavy, no
  commitment-with-accountability mechanism.
- **Stand-up / scrum daily** — wrong cadence (daily ≠ weekly), wrong
  format (yesterday/today/blockers ≠ Account/Review/Plan).
