# Protocol: Member-Debrief — Honest self-account AFTER the team WIG Session

> Loaded by SKILL.md orchestrator when scope detection identifies a team
> member processing last week's commitment outcome (kept / partial /
> missed) before the next team WIG Session. Agent voice = personal
> coach to the member; output = ~30-second statement the member will
> deliver in tomorrow's Account segment.

## I — Interpretation (mode-specific)

The Account segment is short — maybe 30 seconds per person — but those
30 seconds are the trust-building act of the entire week. The book is
firm: **doing-what-you-said is the trust currency**, but **honestly
naming what you did and didn't is the integrity currency.** They are
different currencies. You can fail the first and still pass the second
— that is how trust survives a miss.

A healthy member debrief is built around five questions:

1. **What was the commitment?** — restated verbatim, not paraphrased.
2. **What happened?** — kept / partial / missed. One word, then
   evidence. No story-mode.
3. **If missed or partial: what blocked it?** — factual cause, not
   blame, not excuse-coaching.
4. **What did I learn?** — about commitment design, week, lead-measure
   mechanism.
5. **What's next?** — re-commit (same scope, different week) OR
   re-design (smaller scope, different shape).

The five common failure modes of member debriefs:

- **Excuse mode** ("I would have, but the whirlwind / Bob / weather…")
  drains team trust.
- **Shame mode** ("I'm the worst, I should have done it") blocks
  analysis.
- **Vague mode** ("kind of, partially, sort of") provides no learning
  signal — fails P-22.
- **No-mention mode** silently undermines D4.
- **Over-commit mode** ("I'll do it AND three more things") = Marcus
  pattern (CE-23) reflexed inward.

Marcus's own line is the model. Past tense, owned agency, named
mechanism, no apology theatre. Then propose a redesign. That's the
shape.

**Multi-miss reframing**: One miss is data. Two misses is a pattern.
Three misses in a row is no longer a debrief problem — it is a
**commitment-design problem**, and the right move is route to
member-prep mode for redesign (smaller scope, different shape).

Voice contrast: agent is **personal coach to the member**, non-
judgmental, locker-room brief — turn "I failed" into "here's what I
learned, here's what I'll say". Unlike member-prep (forward-facing,
shape the next commitment), this protocol closes the past.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: Marcus's own honest self-account (Ch. 10)
- West Team falling behind Q2 sales WIG. Marcus has missed his
  commitments and made prospecting calls himself instead of building
  the system. When Susan asks for his account, Marcus doesn't
  story-mode it. One sentence: *"I wasn't even able to keep my
  commitments for last week because I decided to just make prospecting
  calls myself to help make our numbers."* Past tense, named agency,
  named mechanism. No excuse, no wallowing.
- That sentence makes the rest of the dialogue productive. Susan can
  offer the five optimizing questions; Bianca can volunteer her
  prospecting script. Honest account *unlocks* the help.

### Case 2: Yolanda's clean back-room report-back (Ch. 11, derived)
- Store 334 — worst of 250. Yolanda commits to clean the back-room
  storage area as her week's commitment. Reports back specifically —
  not "I worked on it", but the deliverable was delivered, observable,
  photo-able. When the *commitment* was specific (P-22a passed at
  commitment-time), the *account* is trivially specific. Most debrief
  failure starts at commitment-design.
- Outcome: department score 13 → 38 / 50 in 10 weeks.

### Case 3: Mike Crisafulli — refusing favourable results without understanding (The Missing Ingredient)
- SVP of IT got *favourable* early results across 46 teams but didn't
  understand the mechanism. Refused to accept the favourable account.
  Demanded each team report on results AND lessons. Humility applied
  to a "kept" commitment — not just "did we hit it" but "do I
  understand why we hit it". Clean debrief isn't only for misses;
  *kept* commitments deserve same honest mechanism-naming.
- Outcome: 3× original goal by year-end.

## A2 — Future Trigger

When a member needs this protocol:

1. Member missed last week's commitment and team's WIG Session is
   tomorrow. Most common.
2. Member half-did the commitment and is unsure how to classify it.
3. Member kept commitment but with a quality problem (shipped Friday
   not Thursday with a regression) — kept or miss?
4. Member has dressed-up the miss in their head and a quiet voice says
   the team will see through it.
5. Member is dreading the session and tempted to no-mention or excuse.

EN: "I missed my commitment last week — how do I show up at the
session?", "I half-did it. Is that a kept or a miss?", "I want to walk
into the session clean about last week."
JP: 「先週のコミットメント果たせなかった、どう振り返る？」「セッション明日で、何て言えばいい？」「コミットメント部分達成、どう報告すればいい？」
zh-TW: 「上週 commitment 沒達成，這次 session 怎麼面對？」「半完成的承諾要怎麼講？」

## E — Execution

Output target: a prepared **30-second statement** the member can use
verbatim or as a stable spine in tomorrow's Account segment.

1. **Open & set the frame** (≤30 sec)
   - "Walk into the session clean. We have ~5 minutes. Five questions,
     then a script you can say in 30 seconds. No shame, no excuse —
     just straight."

2. **Q1 — Restate the commitment verbatim**
   - "What was your commitment last week — exactly the words?"
   - If member paraphrases ("something about outreach…"), push: "What
     did you actually say? The exact deliverable."
   - If they cannot recall: that's data — commitment was probably
     vague at commitment-time (P-22 violation upstream). Note for the
     redesign decision.

3. **Q2 — Classify the outcome straight**
   - "Kept, partial, or missed? — one word."
   - Apply **two-part P-22 test**:
     (a) Specific deliverable — concrete output someone else could
     verify? If no, classify **missed** regardless of effort. Effort
     isn't the unit; deliverable is.
     (b) Did it happen on the committed window? Late by a day, or late
     with a regression, is *not* kept — partial at best. Don't allow
     rounding up.
   - **Anti-CE-24 (Goodhart self-deception)**: if user reaches for
     "I'll count that as a yes", name it once gently — "be careful;
     the team needs a real signal, not a count" — and re-ask.

4. **Q3 — If missed or partial: what blocked it?** (skip if kept)
   - "What blocked it? — factual, not blame."
   - Listen for excuse-mode signals. Mirror without coaching the
     excuse: "OK, that's the situation. What's the *part of that
     within your control* that you didn't do?" — gently reclaims
     agency without shaming.
   - Listen for **Marcus pattern self-applied (CE-23)**: did member
     spend last week doing teammates' urgent tasks instead of own
     committed deliverable? If yes, name it: "That's the Marcus move —
     picking up someone else's urgent over your own committed."
   - Listen for **shame-mode** ("I'm just bad at this"). Interrupt
     once: "Stop. One miss is data, not a verdict. Stay with the
     facts."

5. **Q4 — What did I learn?**
   - Branch on Q2:
     - If **missed**: "What does this tell you about the *commitment
       design* — was it specific (P-22a), within your control, scoped
       to one week?"
     - If **partial**: "What does this tell you about your *week's
       capacity* — was the commitment too big, or was the week eaten?"
     - If **kept**: "What did you learn about the *lead-measure
       mechanism* — did doing this actually move the lead? (Crisafulli
       discipline — don't just count it; understand it.)"

6. **Q5 — Re-commit or redesign?**
   - Decision rule:
     - 1 miss in isolation, clean cause → **re-commit** same scope,
       different week.
     - 2 misses in a row OR partial-with-quality-problem → **resize
       down** — same shape, smaller deliverable.
     - 3+ misses in a row → **stop**. Hand off: "Three misses in a row
       is a commitment-design problem, not a willpower problem. Use
       member-prep mode to redesign the shape."
   - **Anti-over-commit guard**: if member proposes "I'll do it AND
     three more things this week" to make up for the miss, reject:
     "No. The lead measure won't move from piling. Pick one. Same
     one, smaller version, or different shape."

7. **Compose the 30-second script**
   - Build the spoken Account-segment statement with this spine:
     ```
     [Verbatim commitment]. [Kept / partial / missed].
     [If miss/partial: one-sentence factual cause, owned].
     [What I learned: one sentence].
     [Next: re-commit / resize / I'll bring a redesign].
     ```
   - Check the script against the five failure modes one last time:
     excuse mode? cut. Shame mode? cut. Vague mode? tighten. No-mention?
     must include commitment by name. Over-commit? cap to one next step.
   - Read it back. Time it — should be ~30 seconds spoken aloud.

   **Multilingual register cue (output)**:
   - **EN**: blunt, locker-room brief, past tense, owned agency.
     ("Committed X. Missed. Took on Bob's urgent. Re-committing same
     scope Wed.")
   - **JP**: 過去形 + 主語明確、「〜してしまった」「〜できませんでした」
     を含めて responsibility は受けるが over-apologize しない。「先週は
     X をコミットしました。果たせませんでした。同窓のタスクを優先して
     しまったのが原因です。今週は同じスコープで水曜までに再コミット
     します」
   - **zh-TW**: 直白、過去式、認帳但不過度道歉。「上週我承諾 X。沒達成。
     原因是把同事的緊急任務優先了。這週同樣 scope，週三前重新 commit。」

8. **Close**
   - "That's your Account. The team needs the clean signal. Go in,
     deliver it, sit down. Plan segment is where you'll re-commit or
     bring the redesign — not the Account segment."

## B — Boundary (mode-specific)

### Do NOT use this protocol for:
- **Solo / no-team contexts** — load `protocols/solo-session.md`; or
  for solo lapse, `4dx-sustain-personal-momentum-rescue`.
- **Chronic underperformance (3+ misses in a row)** — that's a
  commitment-design problem; route to member-prep mode for redesign.
- **Picking the next commitment** — load `protocols/member-prep.md`.
- **Leader-side facilitation** — Susan-side dialogue is out of scope;
  load `protocols/team-leader-session.md`.
- **Annual / quarterly retrospectives** — wrong cadence scope.
- **Therapy / shame work** — if dominant register is "I'm a failure",
  briefly normalize ("one miss is data, not a verdict"), then if shame
  doesn't move, decline the rest of the protocol.

### Author-warned failure modes (cited)
- **Excuse mode (CE-22 self-applied)** — "Whirlwind / Bob / weather"
  framings drain team trust. Reclaim agency: "What's the part within
  your control that you didn't do?"
- **Marcus pattern self-applied (CE-23)** — name it when present.
- **Goodhart self-deception (CE-24)** — "I'll count that as a yes" when
  deliverable wasn't actually delivered.
- **Pile-on response** — Marcus pattern reflexed inward. Reject in step 6.
- **Story-mode in the script** — 30-second Account is *not* a recap.
  Cut everything that isn't commitment → outcome → cause → learning →
  next.

### Author's blind spots
- **Book is leader-POV** — almost all of Ch. 5/10/15 written for Susan,
  not Marcus. Member-side debrief = symmetric inverse of leader-side
  accountability-with-respect; coherent with P-22, P-39, CE-22/23/24,
  but not directly authored. V1 ⚠️ partial.
- **Book assumes commitments were well-designed at commitment-time** —
  ~half of misses trace to vague-at-source commitments. Cleanest fix
  is upstream member-prep mode.
- **High-context-culture face-loss (CE-29 family)** — public miss-
  reporting in JP / ZH / KR can read as face-threatening. Brief /
  past-tense / owned-agency shape *helps* (no over-apology). If member
  unwilling to say it aloud, prefer written Slack/Teams Account
  submitted in advance.
- **Quality-problem classification ambiguity** — book treats kept-with-
  quality-issue as partial in spirit (P-22a "specific deliverable")
  but doesn't define a sharp rule. Working rule: shipped-but-with-
  regression / shipped-late = partial.

### Easily-confused neighbours
- **Agile retrospective** — team-level, multi-topic. WIG Account is
  individual, single-commitment, three-line.
- **OKR check-in** — score-based (0.0-1.0), no honest-account
  discipline.
- **Performance review / 1:1** — different frame entirely (multi-week,
  evaluative, asymmetric).
- **Therapy / coaching reflection** — feeling layer is upstream and
  out of scope. WIG debrief is in the deliverable layer.
