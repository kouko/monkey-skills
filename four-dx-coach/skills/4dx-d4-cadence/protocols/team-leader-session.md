# Protocol: Team-Leader — Facilitate a team weekly WIG Session

> Loaded by SKILL.md orchestrator when scope detection identifies a team
> leader (3-12 reports) facilitating their own team's weekly WIG Session.
> Agent voice = consultant-to-leader; the leader runs the room.

## R — Reading (verbatim source quote)

> Hold WIG Sessions to the same day at the same time in the same place every week (including electronically), regardless of the whirlwind. If you're gone, delegate leadership of the session to another team member.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 15

> Team members are more likely to take ownership of commitments they come up with themselves. Still, the leader should make sure the commitments meet the following standards: Specific … Aligned to moving the scoreboard … Timely.
>
> — *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 15

## I — Interpretation (mode-specific)

A team WIG Session is a 20-30 minute weekly meeting with one job: push
the team's WIG forward against the daily whirlwind. The leader
**facilitates**; team members **propose** their own commitments. The
fixed three-part agenda — **Account / Review / Plan** — is non-negotiable.

What changes vs solo and member protocols is *who is doing what to whom*:

1. **Sacred cadence is the leader's job to protect.** Same day, same
   time, same length, every week. If leader is travelling, session still
   runs (phone / video / delegated chair).
2. **Whirlwind is excluded by the leader.** Member tries to bring an
   operational issue → leader redirects: "park that for after — was the
   commitment kept?"
3. **Commitments come from members, not from the leader.** Authority
   shifts from *naming* to *vetoing*. If leader says "Alice, this week I
   want you to call five customers", facilitation has stopped, dictation
   has started, peer ownership evaporates.
4. **Specificity gate** applied to every commitment.
5. **Veto-not-dictate** — when proposed commitment is vague / won't move
   the lead / depends on others / repeats rote, leader vetoes and asks
   member to re-propose. Never substitute.
6. **Public accountability is structural.** Members report to *each
   other*, not to leader in 1:1.
7. **Recognition seeds engagement.** Catch members "doing something
   right" early in next session.

Voice contrast: agent is **consultant-to-leader** — does not address the
team directly; whispers to the leader. Output is leader-facing scripts,
prompts, and decision rules the leader uses live in the room.

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: Serena's Event Management team (Ch 15, canonical walkthrough)
- Hotel events team needed to lift corporate-event revenue $22M→$31M;
  lead measures = quality site visits + bar package upsell.
- Serena ran a Monday-morning WIG Session. **Opened with the
  scoreboard** (modeling the discipline herself), **reported her own
  commitments first** ("I committed to work with Kim and Karen for
  twenty minutes…"), then went round the room. Each member named what
  they kept, what the scoreboard showed, proposed next-week commitment.
- Mechanism: **leader models reporting and commitment-making first** —
  team won't commit to a discipline the leader is unwilling to perform.
- Outcome: lag ran $14M against $10.4M target after 3 months.

### Case 2: Jeff's missed commitment — "the moment that matters most" (Ch 15)
- Team member Jeff failed to keep his commitment, citing whirlwind (a
  major event ran into trouble; he firefought rather than executed).
- Serena did NOT skip the moment. Three-step response inside the
  session: (1) **Demonstrate respect** — acknowledge whirlwind was real;
  (2) **Reinforce accountability** — "when we make a commitment, we
  have to find a way to fulfill it no matter what"; (3) **Encourage
  performance** — "can we count on you to catch up, by fulfilling last
  week's plus this week's?"
- Mechanism: when whirlwind beats a commitment, leader's response in
  front of the team sets the standard for the whole team. Skipping the
  moment teaches team commitments are conditional.
- Outcome: Jeff carried both commitments the following week; team saw
  the standard hold; cadence preserved.

### Case 3: MICARE (Ch 5) — leader-modeled cadence at scale
- Mexican mining; every department leader ran a Monday WIG Session,
  webinar-connected for remote sites. CEO held cadence himself for
  years — modeled non-negotiability from the top.
- Outcome (7 years): lost-time accidents 700→60/yr; output per worker
  6,000→10,000 metric tons.

## A2 — Future Trigger

When a team leader needs this protocol:

1. Leader installed D1-D3 across the team (Team WIG defined, lead
   measures picked, scoreboard built) and asks how to run the weekly
   session that closes the loop.
2. Leader is about to run their first WIG Session and wants a walkthrough.
3. Leader's WIG Session has drifted into a status meeting — members
   report whirlwind, no specific commitments are made.
4. Leader is travelling / on PTO / sick and tempted to skip — protocol
   coaches them to run remotely or delegate the chair, never skip.
5. A member missed a commitment and leader doesn't know how to respond
   without shaming or letting standard erode.

## E — Execution

Agent is **consultant-to-leader** — provides leader-facing scripts and
decision rules. The leader runs the room.

1. **Pre-session check** (≤2 min, before room opens) — see `../standards/sacred-cadence.md`
   - Confirm: scoreboard updated? Last week's commitments logged and
     visible? Time-box set (20-30 min)? WIG stated the same way every
     week (verbatim banner)?
   - Coach prompt for leader: "Are you reporting *your own* commitments
     first? If not, the team won't commit to a standard you're not
     modeling."
   - Halt: if scoreboard not updated, don't run the session yet —
     "without the scoreboard, this is just another meeting" (Ch 15).

2. **Frame the session** (≤2 min)
   - Leader script: "Welcome to our weekly WIG Session. Our Team WIG is
     *[X]*. We have ~25 minutes. Three segments: Account, Review, Plan.
     Let's start with the scoreboard."

3. **Segment 1 — Account, leader first, then round-robin** (~7-10 min)
   - **Leader reports their own commitments first.** Coach reminder:
     "Model the standard. If you didn't keep yours, name it cleanly —
     no excuses — that gives the team permission to do the same."
   - Then round-robin: "Alice, last week you committed to *[Y]*. What
     happened?"
   - Classify aloud: kept / partially kept / not kept. Listen, mirror,
     no problem-solve.
   - **The moment that matters most** — when a member did NOT keep,
     run the **three-step response** (Ch 15):
     (a) **Respect** — acknowledge the whirlwind was real;
     (b) **Accountability** — "when we make a commitment, we find a way
     to fulfill it no matter what";
     (c) **Encourage** — "can we count on you to catch up, by
     fulfilling last week's plus this week's?"
   - Coach veto: do NOT skip the moment, do NOT shame, do NOT let "I
     was busy" pass without the three-step.

4. **Segment 2 — Review the scoreboard** (~5-7 min)
   - Leader prompt: "What is the scoreboard telling us this week — lead
     and lag?"
   - Probe trend, not snapshot. Ask team to interpret before leader does.
   - **Celebrate kept commitments and lead-measure wins by name** —
     "Bob, three site visits, 100% upsell conversation — that's the
     pattern we want."
   - If lead green + lag flat for ≥3 weeks, flag and route to
     post-session diagnostic (lead may need to change, not pushed
     harder).

5. **Segment 3 — Plan, member proposes, leader gates** (~8-10 min) — see `../standards/commitment-shape.md`
   - Canonical question, asked of each member in turn: "What are the
     **one or two** most important things you can do **this week** to
     move our **lead measures**?"
   - For each proposed commitment, leader applies the **specificity
     gate** aloud:
     (a) Specific enough that we'd know on Saturday?
     (b) Aligned to moving the scoreboard (lead specifically)?
     (c) Within member's own control (not "I'll get my boss to…")?
     (d) Different from a rote-repeated commitment (Ch 15: don't repeat
     same one >2 consecutive weeks)?
   - **Veto-not-dictate**: any gate fails → "that doesn't pass —
     re-propose" and wait. Coach reminder: "If you find yourself naming
     the commitment, stop. Ask: 'What do YOU think you can commit to
     that moves the lead?'"
   - Cap 1-2 per member. Path-clearing: if a member needs help from
     another member, that help becomes a commitment for the helper too.

6. **Confirm out loud, then log** (~2 min)
   - Each member states final 1-2 commitments aloud verbatim.
   - Leader (or chair) logs them where next week's Account can read.

7. **Close + confirm next session** (~1 min) — see `../standards/sacred-cadence.md`
   - "Same time, same place next week."
   - If leader is travelling: "I'll dial in / [member] will chair — we
     don't skip."
   - Coach reminder: "Catch one person doing something right early next
     session. Lowest-cost, highest-yield move."

**Whirlwind-creep redirect (any segment)** — see `../standards/whirlwind-exclusion.md`:
Member starts narrating operational drama → leader interrupts politely:
"Park that — let's keep this on the WIG. Was the commitment kept or
not?" Coach reminder: "Do this without apology. The rule is the rule."

## B — Boundary (mode-specific)

### Do NOT use this protocol for:
- **Solo-user contexts** — mechanism is peer-to-peer accountability;
  load `protocols/solo-session.md`.
- **Member-prep contexts** — member preparing their own commitment
  *before* attending; load `protocols/member-prep.md`.
- **Daily standups, status meetings, 1:1s, project reviews** — different
  artifact.
- **Goal-discovery / WIG-selection** — route to D1.
- **Annual / quarterly / monthly reviews** — wrong cadence scope.
- **Reactive / on-call / emergency-response teams** — see
  `4dx-meta-strategy-triage`.

### Author-warned failure modes (leader-specific)
- **Leader skips when busy / travelling (CE-11)** — cancel-and-reschedule
  within the week, never skip; if leader is out, delegate the chair.
- **Leader dictates the commitment** — fastest way to kill peer
  accountability. Veto vague proposals; never substitute.
- **Leader doesn't report their own commitments first** — sets signal
  that discipline is for team, not leader. Members mirror.
- **Whirlwind invasion (CE-10)** — leader's job is to hold the agenda
  without apology.
- **Compliance commitments / vague intents (CE-12)** — veto and
  re-propose.
- **Commitment doesn't move the lead (CE-13)** — leader veto: "Does that
  move the lead, or just feel important?"
- **Repeated commitment ≥3 weeks** (Ch 15) — veto and ask for new lever.
- **Accepting "I was too busy" without the three-step** (Ch 15) —
  skipping the moment teaches commitments are conditional.
- **Too many commitments per member** — cap at 2.
- **Dependency commitments** — reframe to member's own action.
- **Treating session as report-out** — Plan ≈ 40%, Account ≈ 30%,
  Review ≈ 25%.

### Author's blind spots
- **Knowledge work with long feedback loops (CE-27)** — adapt: commit
  to specific *experiments*, not outcomes (CF-Center pattern).
- **High-context-culture face dynamics (CE-29)** — public
  member-to-member accountability can read as face-threatening in JP /
  ZH / KR teams. Adapt the three-step: extend Respect longer; make
  Accountability about the *team's* shared standard rather than the
  individual's failing; frame Encourage as offering help.
- **Hybrid / fully-remote teams** — book assumes co-located scoreboard.
  Remote works (MICARE webinar precedent), but compensate: deliberate
  eye contact via video, shared digital scoreboard always on screen, no
  one's camera off during their report.
- **Goodhart-style gaming on lead measures (CE-24)** — leader's
  off-session sanity check.

### Easily-confused neighbours
- **Scrum / sprint review** — wrong cadence (~2 weeks), wrong format,
  no whirlwind-separation.
- **Daily stand-up** — wrong cadence (daily ≠ weekly).
- **OKR check-in** — typically status-update, no peer-commitment-making
  segment.
- **Status meeting / project review** — report-out, no
  commitment-with-accountability mechanism, whirlwind not excluded.
