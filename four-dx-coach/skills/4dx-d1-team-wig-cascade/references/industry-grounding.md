# Industry-experience grounding for 4dx-d1-team-wig-cascade

Supplements the book's cascade methodology by anchoring it against three older
cascade frameworks the book does not name. Helps practitioners distinguish what
4DX cascade actually requires from superficially similar habits imported from
Hoshin Kanri, OKR, or Balanced Scorecard.

## Three peer cascade methodologies (primary sources)

### Hoshin Kanri — the methodological precursor

- **Akao, Y. (ed.), *Hoshin Kanri: Policy Deployment for Successful TQM*
  (Cambridge, MA: Productivity Press, 1991)** — the canonical English-language
  reference; reissued by Routledge in 2004 (ISBN 978-1-56327-311-7) and by
  Taylor & Francis (DOI 10.4324/9780367554255).
  ([Routledge product page](https://www.routledge.com/Hoshin-Kanri-Policy-Deployment-for-Successful-TQM/Akao/p/book/9781563273117),
  [Taylor & Francis record](https://www.taylorfrancis.com/books/mono/10.4324/9780367554255/hoshin-kanri-yoji-akao),
  [Internet Archive scan](https://archive.org/details/hoshinkanripolic0000akao))

Hoshin Kanri's **catchball** (キャッチボール) — iterative back-and-forth
proposal between management layers until alignment is reached — is the
historical precursor to 4DX's "veto, don't dictate" Rule 3. Both refuse
top-down imposition. The differences are operational:

- **Cadence**: hoshin reviews are typically annual with monthly progress
  checks; 4DX runs the cascade once per Primary-WIG cycle but enforces
  weekly accountability via the WIG Session.
- **Tooling**: hoshin uses the **X-matrix**, a one-page document with
  long-term objectives, annual objectives, top-level improvement
  priorities, and metrics arrayed around a central diamond. 4DX uses
  the simpler "From X to Y by When" format plus a small-number
  Battles list.
- **Scope of objectives**: hoshin allows several "vital few" priorities
  per layer; 4DX is firm on **one** Team WIG per team and at most one
  WIG per individual.

If the org already runs hoshin, the 4DX cascade map can be slotted into
the X-matrix's bottom-right "improvement priorities" cell — but the
one-WIG-per-individual rule and weekly cadence are non-negotiable for 4DX.

### OKR — the cascade pattern 4DX explicitly rejects

- **Doerr, J., *Measure What Matters: How Google, Bono, and the Gates
  Foundation Rock the World with OKRs* (New York: Portfolio/Penguin,
  2018, ISBN 978-0-525-53622-2)** — the popular reference for OKR cascade
  practice; Doerr learned OKRs from Andy Grove at Intel.
  ([Publisher / book page](https://www.whatmatters.com/the-book),
  [Amazon listing](https://www.amazon.com/Measure-What-Matters-Google-Foundation/dp/0525536221))

Doerr's OKR is sometimes cascaded ("goals were handed down the org chart
like tablets from Mount Sinai") but Doerr himself attacks that pattern
and argues for **bidirectional goal-setting** — "high-functioning teams
thrive on a creative tension between top-down and bottom-up goal
setting", with annual company OKRs and quarterly team OKRs. Where 4DX
and OKR differ:

- **Number**: OKR allows 3-5 Os per team and 3-5 KRs per O; 4DX is
  firm on **one** Team WIG.
- **Cadence**: OKR runs typically quarterly cycles; 4DX runs the
  cascade per Primary-WIG cycle (often longer) but adds a weekly
  WIG Session for accountability.
- **Cascade rule**: OKR is bidirectional negotiation; 4DX is veto-only
  (the team-leader proposes; the leader-of-leaders can veto but cannot
  rewrite the proposal).

Don't smuggle multi-objective OKR habits — "let's give each team three
WIGs so we cover the space" — into a 4DX cascade. The collapse to one
Team WIG is the discipline.

### Balanced Scorecard — the third comparison point

- **Kaplan, R. S. & Norton, D. P., *The Strategy-Focused Organization:
  How Balanced Scorecard Companies Thrive in the New Business
  Environment* (Boston: Harvard Business School Press, 2001, ISBN
  978-1-57851-250-8)** — the definitive treatment of BSC cascade across
  ~200 implementing companies (Mobil, CIGNA, AT&T Canada).
  ([HBS Faculty book record](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=5595),
  [HBR Store listing](https://store.hbr.org/product/the-strategy-focused-organization-how-balanced-scorecard-companies-thrive-in-the-new-business-environment/2506))

The five Kaplan-Norton principles — translate strategy into operational
terms, align the organization to the strategy, make strategy everyone's
everyday job, make strategy a continual process, mobilize change through
leadership — overlap with 4DX in spirit but differ structurally:

- BSC tracks four perspectives (financial, customer, internal process,
  learning & growth) at every level; 4DX tracks one Team WIG plus 1-3
  lead measures per team.
- BSC's **strategy map** is its own visual concept (cause-effect chains
  across the four perspectives). The 4DX book's "Strategy Map" — a
  three-column visual of stroke-of-pen, behavioral-change zone, and
  whirlwind — is unrelated despite the name collision. Don't treat
  them as interchangeable.

## What 4DX practitioners learn from the comparison

The cascade rules that distinguish 4DX from these three peers are exactly
the rules most commonly violated by leaders coming from those backgrounds:

1. **One Team WIG, not three or five** — OKR habit smuggles in
   multi-objective cascade. Resist.
2. **Veto, not catchball, not bidirectional negotiation** — hoshin's
   iterative-proposal habit and OKR's bidirectional-tension habit both
   pull leaders toward rewriting subordinate proposals "for clarity".
   Rewriting is dictating. Veto means reject and request a new proposal;
   the team-leader still authors the replacement.
3. **Differentiated team contribution, not miniature-Primary-WIG**
   replication — BSC habit pushes "every team gets the same scorecard
   with different numbers". 4DX cascade requires each team's WIG to
   describe *that team's* unique contribution-shape (Bellstand's WIG is
   luggage delivery time, not "improve guest satisfaction by 1.3%").
4. **Single-pass, single-layer cascade** — the book's retailer case
   shows region → district as one pass, then district → store as a
   second independent pass. Trying to cascade three layers in one
   facilitation collapses fidelity and degrades veto authority into
   report-out theater. The hoshin and BSC literature does not always
   enforce this; 4DX does.
