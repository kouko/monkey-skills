# 4DX D1 — Team WIG Cascade

**English** | [日本語](README.ja.md) | [繁體中文](README.zh-TW.md)

> Coach you (the leader-of-leaders) to translate an already-set org Primary WIG into Team WIGs across 3-7 subordinate teams via Ch 7's four cascade rules.

## When this skill activates

- "How do I translate the org WIG to my team-leaders?"
- "Each of my managers needs a WIG — how do we pick them?"
- A WIG was handed down from above and you have to decompose it
- Multi-team org cascading region → district, division → team
- First-attempt cascade produced 7+ Battle WIGs or assigned the same WIG verbatim to every team

## What it does (the protocol in brief)

Consultant-to-leader-of-leaders. The skill's primary work is enforcing **Rule 3 — veto, don't dictate**:

1. **Confirm the upstream Primary WIG** — must be in *From X to Y by When* form
2. **Enumerate subordinate teams** — N teams, named functions (3-7 ideal)
3. **Classify cascade shape** — functionally diverse vs functionally similar
4. **Identify Key Battles** — the FEWEST possible (Opryland: 17 → 3); 2-3 typical
5. **Solicit Team WIG proposals** — pull, not push; team-leader proposes, you don't assign
6. **Apply the ladder-up test** — arithmetic, not vibe; sum of contributions ≥ Battle ≥ Primary WIG
7. **Apply the veto test (Rule 3)** — accept or return for re-proposal; never rewrite
8. **One-WIG-per-individual rule (Rule 1)** — no overload
9. **Cascade-depth check** — >2 layers? Re-run this skill at each layer
10. **Output the cascade map** — Primary WIG → Battles → Team WIGs, each verbally read back

## Source

Distilled from *The 4 Disciplines of Execution* (2nd ed., 2021), Chapter 7 — Translating Organizational Focus Into Executable Targets. Three anchor cases: Opryland Hotel (functionally diverse cascade — 75 teams, 17 → 3 Battles), Multi-outlet retailer (functionally similar cascade with leaf-team Battle-selection autonomy), Sydney accounting firm (small-company cascade, intermediate Battle layer collapses).
