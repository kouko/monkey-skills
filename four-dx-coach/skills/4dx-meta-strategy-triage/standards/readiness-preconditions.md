# Standard: Readiness preconditions

## Statement

Even when goal-shape is correct AND actor (user / team) clears shape
gates, three change-readiness preconditions must hold before 4DX can
install:

1. **Urgency** — actor articulates cost of inaction in one sentence. In
   team mode, *team*'s urgency must be ≥ leader's. (Kotter Stage 1)
2. **Structural alignment** — incentives, processes, rewards reinforce
   (not contradict) the WIG. (Galbraith STAR — Reward + Process levers)
3. **Cultural fit** — when WIG commitment conflicts with another demand
   (boss request, partner expectation), the WIG can win. Underlying
   assumption layer permits the discipline. (Schein)

Plus a fourth gate primarily for solo, also for leader's own
time-authority:

4. **Time-sovereignty / authority** — actor controls ~20% of weekly
   hours (solo) OR can carve out ~20% of team capacity (leader).
   Without protected time, no install surface. Heath Path layer:
   environment must be compatible.

If any precondition fails, return **TEAM-NOT-YET-READY** (team) or
**no-time-sovereignty** (solo) — fix upstream first; install on
unaddressed gap is the documented path to compliance theater.

## Source

Primary citations in `../references/industry-grounding.md`.

- **Kotter, *Leading Change* (HBS 1996; rev. 2012)** — Stage 1 (Urgency)
  upstream of any execution methodology; ~70% of transformations fail in
  Stage 1 or Stage 2 before methodology installs.
- **Galbraith STAR (Jossey-Bass 2002 / 3rd ed. 2014)** — five levers;
  any one misaligned degrades performance. Book covers Strategy + partial
  Structure; this adds Process + Reward + People.
- **Schein, *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (Jossey-Bass 1985 /
  5th ed. 2017)** — Artifacts / Espoused Values / Underlying
  Assumptions; methodology touches only Artifacts; assumptions win
  silently.
- **Heath & Heath, *Switch* (Crown 2010)** — Rider / Elephant / Path;
  book is Rider-heavy, barely engages Path. Incompatible environment →
  redesign precedes 4DX.

## Why it matters

6-verdict goal-shape rubric catches *what kind of goal* fails. This
standard catches *what kind of context* fails even when goal-shape is
right. Without it, triage produces APPLICABLE that collapses in week 3
(Kotter urgency-deficit), week 12 (Galbraith reward-conflict), or
month 6 (Schein assumption-drift).

Empirical bite from Kotter: ~70% transformation failure rate, mostly
upstream of methodology. For 4DX adoption: leader brings methodology to
non-urgent team → Friday Sessions become ceremony → no behavior change
→ leader concludes "4DX doesn't work for us." Correct diagnosis:
methodology installed on non-Stage-1-cleared team.

Galbraith addition is concrete: in many corporate settings, performance
reviews + bonuses reward whirlwind firefighting (responding to boss's
email faster) more than WIG progress (longer-feedback-loop behavior
change). Reward system wins within a quarter.

Schein addition is most under-appreciated: assumption-level conflicts
("real work is what the boss puts on your desk this morning, not what
you committed to last Friday") never surface in espoused-values
conversation but win silently in actual behavior. Diagnostic — "when
member's Friday WIG commitment conflicts with boss's Monday request,
what wins?" — exposes assumption layer.

## Application across modes

| Mode | How preconditions apply |
|---|---|
| **Solo** | Step 5 (time-sovereignty) = primary check (~20% under user's control). Heath Path implicit: open-plan office, interrupting partner, phone always within reach → environmental redesign first. Kotter urgency woven into Step 1's "what would change?" question. Galbraith / Schein don't apply at solo scale (no incentive system / culture to misalign with). |
| **Team-leader** | Step 7 explicitly runs three layered checks (Kotter urgency-symmetry / Galbraith reward / Schein assumption-conflict). Failure → TEAM-NOT-YET-READY (distinct from mission-misaligned, which is goal-shape). Step 4 covers leader's own time-authority. The four traps are necessary but not sufficient — perfectly-shaped team + goal collapses if any of urgency / incentive / assumption fails. |
| **Cross-mode** | Same logic, different load-bearing preconditions. Solo emphasizes time-sovereignty + Path. Team emphasizes urgency-symmetry + reward + assumption-fit. Both treat readiness as pre-condition for APPLICABLE / TEAM-APPLICABLE; failure routes to "fix this first" verdict. |

Discipline: never grant APPLICABLE / TEAM-APPLICABLE if any readiness
gate fails. Temptation is "but goal-shape is right, install and see" —
that produces the compliance theater this standard exists to prevent.
