# Protocol: Personal-Define — Define a personal WIG as From X to Y by When

> Loaded by SKILL.md orchestrator when scope detection identifies a solo
> individual defining their own WIG. Agent voice = **personal coach**:
> Socratic, supportive, allowed to push back hard on vanity / partner-
> dependent / whirlwind-smuggling forms before the user locks in.

## R — Reading (verbatim source quote)

> All WIGs must have a finish line in the form of *From X to Y by When*.
> Every WIG must contain a clearly measurable result, as well as the
> date by which that result must be achieved.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 2 (Rule 4)

## I — Interpretation (mode-specific)

D1 says: most stated personal goals never get executed because they are
too vague, too many, or too "important-sounding" rather than truly
breakthrough-shaped. Personal-define is the protocol that converts a
fuzzy aspiration ("be healthier", "grow my business") into a single
testable finish line.

Three moves anchor the protocol:

1. **Narrow to one.** Saying yes to one breakthrough means saying no to
   every other "good idea." Cap is **one personal WIG** (max two only
   if they are independent and non-conflicting). Three or more is
   self-deception — the whirlwind eats them.

2. **Pick by leverage, not by importance-rank.** Ask "where would a
   *breakthrough* most transform my situation?", not "what's most
   important in life?" Most-important areas are often already running
   adequately on autopilot. The WIG belongs where current performance
   is the gap.

3. **Write it as *From X to Y by When*.** X = lag-measurable starting
   state in numbers. Y = same lag-measure target. *When* = fixed
   calendar deadline. Verb-led, specific, simple. If it can't be
   written this way, it isn't a WIG yet — it's an aspiration.

For project-shaped goals (fixed end-state, no recurring behavior), the
variant is **outcomes-list-with-deadline** — define "done" as a list of
deliverables on a date, not as "% complete."

Voice contrast vs sibling protocols: team-select coaches a leader
running a 2x2 across a leadership team; this protocol coaches one
individual choosing one personal WIG, alone. Output is one written
WIG, OR an honest re-classification ("this is a habit", "this is a
project", "this is a war that needs to be decomposed into battles",
"this is stroke-of-pen — just decide").

## A1 — Past Application

### Case 1: NASA moon shot (Ch 2)
- Dozens of worthy aerospace objectives in 1961, no breakthrough mandate. JFK named X (earthbound) → Y (moon-and-back) → When (Dec 31 1969), and explicitly named other worthy goals being declined.
- Outcome: Apollo 11 landed July 1969 — five months under deadline — despite no operational technology in the three core battles (navigation, propulsion, life support) when the WIG was announced.
- Lesson: a single, measurable, deadline-bound WIG threw the "Game on!" switch.

### Case 2: Tim Cook / Steve Jobs at Apple — "say no to good ideas" (Ch 2 / Ch 6)
- Apple post-1997 had a sprawling product line; Jobs / Cook reframed leadership as the discipline of refusal. Cut to a handful of products; treated "saying no to a thousand things" as the act of strategic focus.
- Lesson: Discipline 1 is fundamentally a *no* discipline — saying no to good ideas, even great ones — to protect the WIG. Concentration on a small WIG-shaped product set underwrote Apple's execution turnaround.

### Case 3: Mountain Land Rehab — 12-year stalled training project (Ch 9)
- An organization-wide training initiative had been "in progress" for 12 years; "% complete" wasn't moving. Reframed the project as a WIG with a deliverables-list and a date — instead of "complete training program", listed the specific outputs required and assigned a deadline (project-as-WIG variant).
- Lesson: percent-complete is a poor lag measure for project WIGs; defined deliverables on a date is the correct form. Project shipped within a constrained timeframe once given a WIG-shaped finish line.

## A2 — Future Trigger

When a solo individual needs this protocol:

1. User states a goal but it is verb-less, number-less, or deadline-less ("I want to be healthier", "grow my business", "be a better parent", "write more").
2. User lists 3+ "top priorities" simultaneously and can't commit ("this quarter I want to launch the product, write a book, learn Korean, and run a marathon").
3. User has a project — not a habit — but is tracking it as "% done" and progress is stalled.
4. User wants to "use 4DX" but hasn't yet expressed any goal in *From X to Y by When* form.
5. User expresses an *activity* ("exercise more", "study Japanese daily") rather than an *outcome*.

EN signal: "I want to be healthier" / "grow my business" / "I have too many priorities" / "where should I focus?" / "help me set a goal"

## E — Execution

When this protocol activates, the agent runs a Socratic coaching dialogue. **Do not write a WIG for the user — extract it from them.**

1. **Capture the raw goal.**
   - Ask: "What's the goal you want to use 4DX for? Say it however it feels natural."
   - Completion: user states a goal in their own words; agent records verbatim.

2. **Force lag-measure surfacing.**
   - Ask: "If we said this goal was achieved one year from now, what would be measurably different about your life or work? What number would have changed?"
   - Completion: at least one quantifiable downstream lag (kg / revenue / chapters / clients / minutes / hours / count).
   - Halt: if user cannot name any measurable difference, the goal is purely aspirational — jump to step 7 and re-classify as habit / value / unmeasurable.

3. **Pin X (starting state).**
   - Ask: "Where are you on that number right now, today?"
   - Completion: numeric current value (or honest "I don't know — let me check" with a commitment to baseline).

4. **Pin Y (target state).**
   - Ask: "Where do you want to be on that same number?"
   - Completion: numeric target in the same units as X.

5. **Pin *When*.**
   - Ask: "By what calendar date?"
   - Completion: a fixed date (not "soon", "this year", "Q3-ish").

6. **Wildly-important test (transformation vs incremental).** — see `../standards/from-x-to-y-by-when-grammar.md`
   - Ask: "If you hit X→Y by that date, would your situation be *transformed*, or just *improved*?"
   - Halt: if only incremental, this is whirlwind work, not a WIG. Tell the user honestly: "This is a maintenance goal, not a breakthrough goal. 4DX is overkill — just track it." Stop.

7. **Count test.** — see `../standards/wig-discipline.md`
   - Ask: "How many WIGs are you trying to hold right now?"
   - Push back if >2: "The book is firm — one WIG per individual, max two only if they are independent and non-conflicting. Three or more is self-deception. Which one would create the biggest transformation if you actually hit it?"
   - Completion: user has narrowed to one (or named one as primary, with at most one independent secondary).

8. **Battle-vs-war test.**
   - Ask: "Could this WIG be won as a single battle, or is it a war that depends on winning several smaller battles?"
   - Halt: if war (composite), help user pick the *smallest* set of sub-WIGs (battles) needed to ensure winning, then re-run steps 2-7 on each. Do not push forward until each battle has its own *From X to Y by When*.

9. **Three-pitfall screen (vanity / partner-dependent / whirlwind-smuggling).**
   - **Vanity-WIG**: "Is the metric something you'd care about in private — or only because it looks good externally?" (Followers, ranking, optics-only KPIs that won't change anything when achieved.)
   - **Partner-dependent**: "Is hitting Y entirely within your control, or does it require a co-founder, spouse, boss, or market to behave a certain way?" If partner-dependent, reformulate to the user-controlled component.
   - **Whirlwind-smuggling**: "Is this *separable from* and *narrower than* your day-to-day work, or are you trying to label your existing job as a WIG?" Whole-workload-as-WIG (CE-04) collapses immediately.

10. **Project-as-WIG check (Ch 9 caveat).**
    - Ask: "Is this a recurring behavior change, or a one-time project with a fixed end-state?"
    - If project: rewrite finish line as a *list of deliverables on a date*, not a percent-complete. Reject "% complete" as a lag measure.

11. **Output the WIG.** — see `../standards/from-x-to-y-by-when-grammar.md`
    - Write the final form: **"From [X] to [Y] by [date]"** (or for projects: deliverables list + date).
    - Show it back: "Read this aloud. Could a stranger tell on the deadline whether you won or lost?"
    - Completion: user confirms the written WIG is unambiguous and binary (won / lost on the date).

## B — Boundary (mode-specific)

### Do NOT use this protocol for:
- **Team-leader Primary WIG selection** — the 2x2 + four-trap workflow lives in `protocols/team-select.md`. Solo running of a leadership-team artifact collapses to ranking-by-vibes.
- **Team-member receiving an inherited WIG** — comprehension, not definition; load `protocols/member-comprehend.md`.
- **Stroke-of-pen decisions** ("Should I switch CRMs? Buy a treadmill? Hire a VA?"). 4DX is not for changes executable by authority/money/decision alone.
- **Pure habit design** ("Help me meditate daily"). Habits are recurring behaviors with no fixed lag-measurable end-state.
- **Already-well-formed numeric goals** with X / Y / When already explicit. Skip to D2.
- **Reactive / on-call domains** where the whirlwind *is* the strategic work (SRE incident response, ER medicine, customer support).
- **Capacity / time-audit questions** ("do I have time for this?") → `4dx-meta-whirlwind-triage`.

### Author-warned failure modes
- **Multi-WIG drift (CE-03)**: User claims 3-5 WIGs simultaneously. Book is unambiguous: this kills all of them. Push back hard.
- **Whole-workload-as-WIG (CE-04)**: WIG = "do my job better." Useless. WIG must be *separable from* and *narrower than* the whirlwind.
- **Most-important confusion (CE-05)**: User picks the WIG by ranking life areas (family > health > work). The book's selection rule is *where breakthrough has biggest leverage* — often not the highest-ranked area.
- **Aspirational but unmeasurable (CE-07)**: "Be more present", "be a great parent", "find purpose". No lag measure → not a WIG.
- **Percent-complete as project lag (CE-19)**: Long-running projects tracked as "% done" stall indefinitely. Replace with deliverables-list-with-deadline.
- **Activity-as-outcome confusion**: "Exercise 3x/week" is a *lead measure*, not a WIG.
- **Vanity / partner-dependent / whirlwind-smuggling** — Step 9 screens these explicitly; agent must NOT let them through.

### Author's blind spots
- **Goal-discovery vs goal-execution**: 4DX assumes the right WIG is already discoverable. In exploratory domains the OKR / lean-startup tradition would say: run small experiments first. If the user's situation is exploratory, don't force a single-WIG commitment — flag the mismatch (March 1991).
- **Winning-as-master-motivator (CE-31)**: 4DX assumes "winning" energizes. Self-Determination Theory suggests autonomy and mastery are the deeper drivers. If the user is repelled by competitive / scoreboard framing, reframe the WIG as a mastery commitment without losing the *From X to Y by When* form.
- **Selection bias**: All FranklinCovey case studies are paying clients; failed WIGs are rarely analyzed. Don't pre-commit to a WIG before steps 7 and 8 finish.

### Easily-confused neighbours
- **SMART goals** — surface form similar but SMART permits "achievable" goals that are merely incremental. WIG demands *breakthrough* + *separability* + *one-only*.
- **OKRs** — allow 3-5 objectives × 3-5 KRs = up to 25 simultaneous metrics. Personal-WIG caps at 1, max 2.
- **Habit stacks / atomic habits** — process-oriented; no fixed finish line. WIG is outcome-and-deadline-oriented.
