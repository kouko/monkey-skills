# Standard: Ladder-up test — line-of-sight upward

## Statement

Every WIG below the top of the chain must demonstrably **serve the WIG
one layer above it** (or, at the top of the chain, the actor's
**stated mission**). The line of sight runs upward:

- **Individual slice → Team WIG**: each member's recurring commitment
  must move a team lead measure that moves the team WIG.
- **Team WIG → Org Primary WIG**: each team's WIG must demonstrably
  contribute to the org Primary WIG; the leader should be able to
  draw a one-sentence line of sight upward.
- **Org Primary WIG → Mission**: the top-of-chain WIG must serve the
  organization's stated mission; a measurable / feasible /
  break-through WIG that is mission-disconnected produces an
  organization that "achieves its goals but loses its soul."

The book's Ch 7 veto rule: a team WIG that cannot pass the ladder-up
test (does not visibly contribute to the org WIG) is rejected and
re-scoped, regardless of how well-formed it is in *From X to Y by When*
form. Form is necessary but not sufficient; line-of-sight is the final
gate.

## Source

> Without line of sight from the team WIG to the individual's daily
> work, execution will fragment.
>
> — McChesney/Covey/Huling, *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 7 (line-of-sight principle)

> Each team WIG must demonstrably contribute to ensuring the WIG
> at the level above it. The "ensure not support" test: the team WIG
> must *guarantee* the org WIG hits, not merely make a contribution.
>
> — *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 7 (paraphrased — ensure-not-support test for cascade)

> Such organizations achieve their goals but lose their soul.
>
> — *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 6 (Trap 4 — disconnected from mission)

## Why it matters

Line-of-sight is what makes D1 *aligned* rather than merely *focused*:

1. **Without ladder-up, individual focus diverges from team strategy**
   — a team can be fully focused on a perfectly-formed team WIG that
   does not actually move the org WIG. Local optimization, global
   misalignment.
2. **The daily question depends on line-of-sight** — Ch 12's daily
   question ("what's the most important thing I can do today to
   advance my team's lead measure?") presupposes the member knows the
   team's lead measure ladders upward to a WIG that ladders upward to
   org Primary WIG. Break the chain at any link and the question is
   unanswerable.
3. **Trap 4 (mission-disconnect) is the last filter, not the first**
   — a Primary WIG can clear Traps 1-3 (cap / not-too-broad /
   measurable) and still fail mission alignment. The book's
   thrift-store case (Ch 6 sidebar) is exactly this: financial WIGs
   that drift from mission cause organizations to "achieve their
   goals but lose their soul."
4. **Cascade integrity depends on this test** — when a Primary WIG
   gets cascaded down (`4dx-d1-wig-cascade`), the ensure-not-
   support test is the cascade's hard gate. Same test, same logic,
   different cycle position.

## Application across modes

| Mode | How this standard applies |
|---|---|
| **Personal-define** | Most personal WIGs sit *outside* a team / org context — the ladder-up test then defers to the user's deeper "why" (personal mission / values). For personal WIGs that *do* sit inside an org context (a manager's individual WIG inside their team's WIG), Step 9 verifies the personal WIG ladders up to a team / org WIG it visibly serves. |
| **Team-select** | Step 9 explicitly runs the test: "if there is an org WIG above this team, does the candidate Primary WIG demonstrably *serve* it? Could you draw a one-sentence line of sight from this team WIG up to the org WIG?" Failure → return to Step 6 (ripple-effect filter) with a candidate that does ladder up. If team is at top of chain, the test defers to mission alignment (Trap 4). |
| **Member-comprehend** | Step 4 (locate the personal slice) is the member-side ladder-up test in miniature: the member identifies a recurring behavior they personally control that contributes to a team lead measure, which contributes to the team WIG. Step 7 escalation: if the team WIG itself doesn't ladder up cleanly to a stated org WIG / mission, the member raises it as a clarifying question — they do *not* fix it themselves. |

In all three modes the test direction is the same: **upward line of
sight**. What differs is *who runs the verification* (user with agent
prompting / leader on the leadership-team's candidate / member testing
their own slice and the WIG itself) and *what the consequence of
failure is* (re-scope the personal WIG / return to brainstorm /
escalate as clarifying question upward).
