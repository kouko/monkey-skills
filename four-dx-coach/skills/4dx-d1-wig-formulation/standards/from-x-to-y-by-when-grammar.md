# Standard: From-X-to-Y-by-When grammar

## Statement

Every WIG must be written in the canonical form: **"From [X] to [Y]
by [When]"**, where:

- **X** = the lag-measurable starting state, in numbers.
- **Y** = the lag-measurable target state, in the *same units* as X.
- **When** = a fixed calendar deadline (specific date, not "soon",
  "this year", or "Q3-ish").

For project-shaped goals (one-time fixed end-state, no recurring
behavior), the variant is **outcomes-list-with-deadline** — define
"done" as a list of named deliverables on a date, NOT as "% complete."

The form must pass the **stranger test**: a stranger who has never met
the user / team should be able to look at the WIG on the deadline date
and tell — without further explanation — whether it was won or lost.

### Anti-patterns rejected by this standard

- **Verb-less / number-less aspirations**: "be healthier", "grow my
  business", "be a better parent", "lead the industry in innovation"
  — no measurable Y → not a WIG.
- **Deadline-less commitments**: "eventually reach 100k revenue",
  "someday finish the book" — no fixed *When* → not a WIG.
- **Activity-as-outcome**: "exercise 3x / week", "study Japanese
  daily", "make more sales calls" — these are *lead measures*, not
  WIGs. The WIG is the lag the activity predicts.
- **Percent-complete as project lag**: "complete the training
  program at 80% by Q4" — % complete stalls indefinitely; replace
  with named-deliverables list.
- **Identity-locked / vanity targets**: "be a writer", "be a top-tier
  consultant" — identity-framed, no measurable finish line, often
  vanity-driven.

> as the date by which that result must be achieved.
>

>
> — *4DX* 2nd ed., Ch. 6 (Trap 3 — aspirational but unmeasurable)

## Why it matters

The grammar is what makes a WIG *executable* rather than *aspirational*:

1. **Measurability enables tracking** — without a numeric X and Y, the
   D3 scoreboard has nothing to plot, the D4 weekly Review has nothing
   to read, and the user has no signal of progress vs drift.
2. **Deadline forces convergence** — without a fixed *When*, the WIG
   slides indefinitely and is never lost (and therefore never won).
   The deadline is what makes the bet a bet.
3. **Same-units discipline prevents goal-creep** — if X is "78kg" and
   Y becomes "feel fitter", the lag is no longer trackable. Same-unit
   X → Y is the only way the binary won/lost test works on the date.
4. **Stranger-test enforces specificity** — the form is correct when
   ambiguity is squeezed out. Verbatim *From X to Y by When* with
   numeric X / Y / fixed date passes; anything that requires user
   explanation fails.

The 4DX book's most-cited Ch 6 trap (aspirational-but-unmeasurable) is
*entirely* a grammar failure: "become the preferred provider", "lead
the industry in innovation", "earn world-class loyalty" — every one
fails the stranger test. The fix is always the same: rewrite in
*From X to Y by When* form, or admit it's not a WIG.

## Application across modes

| Mode | How this standard applies |
|---|---|
| **Personal-define** | Steps 2-5 (lag-surface / X / Y / When) build the form one piece at a time. Step 11 outputs the final sentence and runs the stranger test aloud. Verb-less / number-less / deadline-less inputs are explicitly rejected at each pin. |
| **Team-select** | Step 10 expresses the chosen Primary WIG in this form *after* the 2x2 + four-trap filtering. The form is the final output gate — a Primary WIG that cleared all four traps but cannot be expressed in *From X to Y by When* fails Trap 3 (unmeasurable) post-hoc; return to step 6. |
| **Member-comprehend** | Step 1 is a *recall test*, not a writing exercise: can the member state the team WIG verbatim in this form? If only a paraphrase comes out, the leader's communication failed — homework: ask leader for exact words. The member does not rewrite; they verify they can repeat. |

The grammar is the same across all three scopes; what differs is *who
authors the sentence* (user / leadership team / leader being quoted by
member) and at *what step in the cycle* (definition / selection-output /
comprehension-recall).
